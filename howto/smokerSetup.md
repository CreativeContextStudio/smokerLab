Here’s a practical, budget‑friendly kit to upgrade your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S offset smoker** plus a simple layout for your first rib or pork‑butt cook.  

***

## Concrete shopping list

These are common, widely used items that work well on budget offsets like the CC1830S. You don’t need these exact brands, but this style/spec is what you want. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z3Mnl-HHrgs)

### 1. Door gasket (for lid + firebox)

- **Type:** High‑temp smoker gasket tape, fiberglass, self‑adhesive, rated to at least ~800–1000°F. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0oSNDGDh3go)
- **Example spec:**  
  - Width: about 1/2"–3/4"  
  - Thickness: about 1/8"  
  - Length: 15–30 ft roll (enough for cook chamber door and firebox door)  
- **Use:** Stick around the main chamber door and firebox door to stop leaks and improve temp control. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z3Mnl-HHrgs)

Search phrase to use: **“LavaLock high temp BBQ gasket 1/2 inch x 1/8 inch”**. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0oSNDGDh3go)

### 2. High‑temp RTV sealant (for seams)

- **Type:** Food‑safe, high‑temperature RTV silicone, rated to around 600°F or higher. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z3Mnl-HHrgs)
- **Use on the CC1830S:**  
  - Seams where the firebox meets the cook chamber.  
  - Any bolt holes or obvious gaps that leak smoke (but not the inside of the stack). [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

Search phrase: **“red high temp RTV silicone gasket maker 650°F”**. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0oSNDGDh3go)

### 3. Charcoal basket

- **Type:** Rectangular or square **charcoal basket** made from expanded metal, sized to fit in a small offset firebox. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)
- **Target dimensions:** Roughly 10"–12" long, 8"–10" wide, 4"–6" tall so it sits comfortably in the CC1830S firebox with air around it. [lowes](https://www.lowes.com/pd/Royal-Gourmet-26-77-in-W-Black-Barrel-Charcoal-Grill/5014685623)
- **Features:**  
  - Elevated bottom (or legs) to let ash fall through.  
  - Sturdy expanded metal sides so charcoal stays bunched together. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

Search phrase: **“charcoal basket for small offset smoker expanded metal”**. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

If you can’t find one that’s a perfect fit, you can also use a **small charcoal “Vortex” basket** or have expanded metal bent into a simple rectangle.

### 4. Digital thermometer (grate + meat)

- **Type:** Dual‑probe (or 4‑probe) digital BBQ thermometer with cables rated for smoker temps. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)
- **Features to look for:**  
  - At least one **grate probe** (attach with a small clip or through a potato/onion) and one **meat probe**.  
  - Cables rated to ~700°F.  
  - Basic alarm and continuous readout. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

Search phrase: **“dual probe BBQ thermometer smoker 700F probe”**. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

### 5. Optional but recommended

- **Chimney starter:** Standard metal chimney to light charcoal without lighter fluid. Search: **“charcoal chimney starter large”**.  
- **Disposable aluminum pans:** 9"×13" pans for water/drip under meat and for catching firebox ash.  
- **Lump charcoal + small splits or chunks:**  
  - Lump or briquettes as your base fuel.  
  - Wood chunks or small splits of hickory, oak, or pecan for smoke.

## Simple mod setup on the CC1830S

Do this once and you’re set.

### Step 1: Seal doors and seams

1. Clean surfaces (degrease, dry).  
2. Apply the **gasket tape** to:  
   - The lip of the main cook chamber door.  
   - The firebox door lip. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z3Mnl-HHrgs)
3. Apply **RTV silicone** along:  
   - The seam where the firebox bolts to the cook chamber.  
   - Any other visible gaps around that joint. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z3Mnl-HHrgs)
4. Let cure per instructions before firing up. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z3Mnl-HHrgs)

## Baffle & coal placement diagram (text version)

Think of the cook chamber as a sideways rectangle:

- **Left side:** Firebox connection.  
- **Right side:** Chimney/exhaust stack.  

We want heat/smoke to enter from the firebox, travel under a baffle/plates, rise and move across the grate, then exit the stack.

### DIY baffle / tuning plate layout

Inside the main chamber, under the cooking grates:

1. **Immediately next to the firebox opening (left side):**  
   - Place a **flat steel plate or heavy pizza pan** just above the opening, extending 8"–12" into the chamber. [ddrbbqsupply](https://ddrbbqsupply.com/collections/tuning-plates-for-offset-smokers-heat-control-even-cooking)
   - It should partially block the direct blast of heat so it goes under and along the bottom instead of straight onto the grate.  

2. **Across the rest of the chamber bottom (toward the stack):**  
   - Lay 2–3 pieces of flat steel or similar plates side‑by‑side under the grate, leaving small adjustable gaps between them. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8otOcXiyB8U)
   - Start with the plates closer together near the firebox side and slightly more spaced toward the stack side.  

Visually in text:

- `[FIREBOX] | [BAFFLE PLATE covering entry] | [TUNING PLATE 1][gap][TUNING PLATE 2][gap][TUNING PLATE 3] | [STACK]`  

This makes the firebox side less nuclear and brings the stack side up so temps are closer across the grate. [ddrbbqsupply](https://ddrbbqsupply.com/collections/tuning-plates-for-offset-smokers-heat-control-even-cooking)

## First cook fire setup (ribs or pork butt)

### Firebox (left side) setup

1. **Charcoal basket in the firebox:**  
   - Fill basket 2/3 full with unlit briquettes or lump.  
   - Make a small well at one end. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

2. **Light a small amount of charcoal in the chimney** (about 1/3 chimney).  
3. **Pour the lit charcoal into the well** on one side of the basket so the fire gradually burns through the unlit coals (minion‑style). [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)
4. **Add 1–2 wood chunks** on or just beside the lit coals once the pit is warmed up (not right at startup to avoid heavy white smoke). [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

### Vents and exhaust

- **Exhaust stack (chimney):**  
  - Run it **fully open** or nearly so; control temps mostly with the firebox intake. [reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/BBQ/comments/ai4ubr/how_critical_is_sealing_an_offset_smoker/)
- **Firebox intake vent:**  
  - Start around half open while the pit comes up to temp, then gradually close toward 1/4–1/3 open to hold ~225–275°F. [reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/BBQ/comments/ai4ubr/how_critical_is_sealing_an_offset_smoker/)

Adjust in small increments and give it 10–15 minutes to respond.

### Water/drip pan

Inside the main chamber:

- Place a **foil pan with water** on the grate between the firebox side and the meat, or directly on the tuning plates under the meat, to catch drips and help stabilize temps.  

### Meat placement

- Put the **ribs or pork butt on the main grate toward the stack end**, not right next to the firebox opening.  
  - Ribs: bone‑side down, then rotate halfway through if you want to even things out further.  
  - Pork butt: fat cap up or down (your choice), but keep it on the stack side where temps are gentler.  

### Thermometer probes

- Clip or place the **grate probe** near the meat on the cooking grate (not over the baffle opening). [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)
- Stick the **meat probe** in the thickest part of the butt or the center of a rib rack (if using). [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

Target temps:  

- **Cook chamber (grate):** 225–275°F.  
- **Pork butt internal:** ~200–205°F for pullable texture.  
- **Ribs:** Probe tender around 195–203°F; bones should twist easily and the meat should bend nicely.
