# Grill Shopping List

Everything you need to get the **Royal Gourmet CC1830S main chamber** running as a proper charcoal grill. No offset firebox, no smoke wood — just clean, direct and indirect charcoal heat. These are practical, widely available items; you don't need exact brands, but match the specs.

---

## 1. Chimney starter

A chimney starter is the single most important piece of equipment for charcoal grilling. It eliminates lighter fluid, gives you consistent, fully-lit coals in 15–20 minutes, and lets you control exactly how much charcoal goes on the fire.

- **Size:** Large (100 cubic inch capacity, about 6 lb charcoal). This is a full chimney load — enough for the CC1830S at high heat.
- **Style:** Standard cylindrical metal chimney with a heat-resistant handle. Weber Rapidfire and Char-Griller are the most common.
- **How to light:** Stuff 2 sheets of crumpled newspaper or 2 wax fire-starter cubes in the bottom chamber. Set on the lower charcoal grate or a fireproof surface, fill top with briquettes, light the paper/cube from underneath.

Search phrase: **"large charcoal chimney starter 100 cubic inch"**

---

## 2. Charcoal

Kingsford Original briquettes are the most reliable choice for the CC1830S main chamber. They burn predictably, hold heat well, and give you consistent 45–60 minute burn times per chimney load.

- **Briquettes vs. lump:** Briquettes are more consistent for grilling (predictable burn rate, easier temp control). Lump charcoal burns hotter and faster — fine for searing but harder to hold steady medium temps.
- **Bag size:** 20 lb bag lasts 3–4 typical grill sessions.
- **Never exceed 6 lb in the main chamber.** The CC1830S main chamber is sized for charcoal grilling, not a bonfire.

Search phrase: **"Kingsford original charcoal briquettes 20 lb"**

---

## 3. Instant-read thermometer

The single tool that will most improve your grilling. Stop guessing doneness by feel or time alone.

- **Type:** Instant-read digital, 2–3 second read time.
- **Recommended:** Thermapen One or Thermapen MK4 (the benchmark), or ThermoWorks Thermopop for a budget option.
- **Use:** Insert into thickest part of meat, away from bone or fat. Pull chicken at 165°F, steaks at your doneness target (130°F rare, 135°F medium-rare, 145°F medium), pork at 145–160°F depending on cut.

Search phrase: **"instant read meat thermometer fast 2-3 second"**

---

## 4. Long-handled tongs and spatula

You need reach when working over 500°F coals.

- **Tongs:** 16-inch or longer, spring-loaded, locking optional. One pair handles most grilling.
- **Spatula:** Wide offset spatula for burgers and fish. The offset blade gets under food cleanly without breaking it.
- **Grill gloves:** Silicone or leather, rated to 500°F+. For adjusting charcoal pan height, moving grates, or grabbing the lid handle when it's hot.

Search phrase: **"16 inch locking BBQ tongs grill"**, **"heat resistant grill gloves 500F"**

---

## 5. Grill brush

Keep your grates clean for better sear marks, less sticking, and longer grate life.

- **Type:** Stiff wire bristle brush (standard) or bristle-free grill stone/scraper (safer option if you're worried about wire bristles).
- **Technique:** Brush hot grates right after preheat, before oiling and loading food. Quick scrub while hot is far easier than scrubbing cold.

Search phrase: **"grill brush for charcoal grill stainless steel"**

---

## 6. Disposable aluminum pans

These are worth keeping a stack on hand. Uses on the CC1830S:

- **Drip pan:** Place in the cool zone below food during indirect cooking to catch fat and juices (and keep your charcoal pan clean).
- **Resting pan:** Pull meat off the grill, let it rest in a pan loosely tented with foil.
- **Veggie grilling:** Toss cut veggies in oil and salt, spread in a pan, set on the grate over indirect heat.
- **Water pan:** Fill halfway with hot water and set in the indirect zone during long indirect cooks to add moisture and buffer temps.

Spec: 9"x13" half-steam-table pans. Buy by the 20-30 pack.

---

## 7. Grill basket

For items that would fall through the grate or need to be tossed: shrimp, scallops, diced veggies, asparagus, thin fish fillets, small whole fish.

- **Material:** Stainless steel mesh or perforated. Avoid non-stick coatings at high heat.
- **Size:** One standard basket (roughly 11"x7") handles most jobs.

Search phrase: **"stainless steel grill basket for vegetables fish"**

---

## 8. Metal skewers

Better than wood for charcoal grilling — no soaking, no burning, reusable.

- **Style:** Flat blade (not round) so food doesn't spin when you flip. 12-inch length.
- **Quantity:** 8–12 skewers handles a kabob party platter for 4–6 people.

Search phrase: **"flat metal skewers 12 inch stainless steel"**

---

## 9. Pantry basics to keep stocked

| Item | Why |
| --- | --- |
| Kosher salt (coarse, Diamond Crystal or Morton) | Primary seasoning for everything on the grill |
| Coarse black pepper (16-mesh or freshly cracked) | The other half of the SPG baseline rub |
| Garlic powder | Goes into almost every dry rub |
| Smoked paprika | Color, mild heat, works well on chicken and pork |
| Neutral oil (canola or vegetable) | Oiling grates before loading food |

---

## What you do NOT need for the CC1830S main chamber

- Wood chunks or wood chips — those are for the offset smoker firebox. The main chamber is for charcoal grilling (direct and indirect heat). The charcoal itself provides a subtle smoky note.
- Lighter fluid — use a chimney starter instead. Lighter fluid leaves chemical flavor and makes consistent fire management harder.
- A smoker box — that's a gas-grill accessory.

---

## Starter kit priority order

If you're starting from scratch, buy in this order:

1. Chimney starter
2. Bag of charcoal
3. Instant-read thermometer
4. Long tongs + grill gloves
5. Grill brush
6. Aluminum pans (pack)
7. Grill basket + skewers (once you're past the basics)
