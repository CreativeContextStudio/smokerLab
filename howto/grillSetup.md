# Grill Setup & First Cook

A practical, step-by-step guide for setting up the **Royal Gourmet CC1830S main grill chamber** for your first cook — or any cook. This covers the charcoal pan positions, chimney lighting, two-zone setup, grate oiling, and the preheat routine. The offset firebox doesn't exist for this guide; everything happens in the main barrel.

---

## The three controls on the CC1830S main chamber

Before the first cook, understand these. Every recipe decision flows from them.

| Control | Function | Range |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Charcoal amount & layout** | Sets total heat output | 1/4 chimney (low) to 2 full chimneys (max) |
| **Charcoal pan height** | Sets how close coals are to the grate | High (searing) / Mid (standard) / Low (indirect) |
| **Vents / dampers** | Fine-tunes airflow and burn rate | Fully open (hot) to 1/4 open (cool and slow) |

Use pan height and charcoal amount for **coarse** temp control. Use vents for **fine** adjustment. Always change one thing at a time and wait 10–15 minutes to see the result.

---

## Step 1: Before you light — check the grill

**Do's**
- Make sure the ash tray is empty or nearly empty; a full ash tray restricts airflow and kills fire.
- Check that the charcoal pan is installed correctly on the adjustment bracket.
- Confirm the cooking grate is seated flat and clean.
- Set the grill on a stable, level surface outdoors — away from structures, overhangs, and anything flammable.

**Don'ts**
- Don't light the grill on a deck or under a covered patio unless there's serious clearance above (8+ ft) and good cross-ventilation. Treat charcoal grills like open fire.
- Don't skip the ash check. Restricted airflow = stubborn fire that won't come up to temp.

---

## Step 2: Set the charcoal pan height

Before adding charcoal, decide on your cook style and set the pan position accordingly.

| Pan position | Distance to grate | Best for | Expected lid temp |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **High** | ~3\u20134 inches | Searing steaks, burgers, smash burgers, quick chars | 450\u2013600\u00B0F |
| **Mid** | ~5\u20136 inches | Chicken pieces, sausage, thick chops, most grilling | 375\u2013450\u00B0F |
| **Low** | ~7\u20138 inches | Gentle indirect roasting, finishing thick cuts | 275\u2013375\u00B0F |

The pan slides into notches on the bracket inside the main chamber. To adjust: open the lid, remove the grate if needed, lift the pan by its handles and reposition it on the correct notch.

---

## Step 3: Light the chimney starter

This is the correct way to start a charcoal fire on the CC1830S. No lighter fluid.

1. **Set the chimney** on the lower charcoal grate inside the main chamber, or on a concrete/brick surface outside.
2. **Fill the chimney** with charcoal briquettes — full load is about 5–6 lb (100 cubic inch chimney filled to the top).
3. **Stuff 2 sheets of crumpled newspaper** (or 2 wax fire-starter cubes) in the bottom chamber of the chimney.
4. **Light the paper/cubes** from underneath using a long lighter or match.
5. **Wait 15–20 minutes.** The chimney is ready when:
   - The top coals are fully gray with glowing orange at the edges.
   - You can see heat shimmer above the chimney.
   - There's no more black charcoal showing on the top.

**Do's**
- Let the coals come fully to temperature before dumping. Partially lit coals = uneven heat and excessive smoke.
- Use the chimney handle and a heat-resistant glove when dumping — the chimney is extremely hot.

**Don'ts**
- Never add lighter fluid to an existing fire, even if the chimney seems slow. Wait it out.
- Don't dump a chimney of partially-lit coals and expect full temp quickly — you'll wait longer and get worse results.

---

## Step 4: Build the fire layout

Once the chimney is ready, pour the coals into the main chamber charcoal pan according to your fire layout:

### Two-zone fire (default recommendation for most cooks)

Pour the coals onto **one side only** of the charcoal pan, leaving the other side empty.

```
[GRATE ABOVE]
[COALS LEFT HALF] [EMPTY RIGHT HALF]
```

- **Hot zone (coals side):** Direct searing heat, 450–550°F at grate level.
- **Cool zone (no coals side):** Indirect gentle heat, 275–350°F with lid closed.

Use the hot zone to sear and develop crust. Use the cool zone to finish thick cuts through to temperature without burning.

### Full-bed fire (for mass grilling or high-volume cooking)

Spread coals evenly across the entire charcoal pan. Use for burgers, hot dogs, many thin items at once where everything is going direct.

### Banked fire (for indirect roasting)

Push all coals to one end only (not just half — tight bank to one end). Food goes on the opposite far end. Use for spatchcock chicken, small roasts, dense thick cuts where you want slow indirect heat.

---

## Step 5: Set vent positions

Open the main chamber vents and lid vent before placing the cooking grate:

| Cook style | Bottom vents | Lid vent |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High heat sear | Fully open | Fully open |
| Medium-high grilling | 3/4 open | 3/4 open |
| Medium indirect | 1/2 open | 1/2 open |
| Low indirect / roasting | 1/4 open | 1/4\u20131/2 open |

Always start more open than you think you need, then close down gradually. It's easier to drop temp than raise it.

---

## Step 6: Preheat with the lid closed

Place the cooking grate over the coals (use gloves — the grate gets hot fast). Close the lid and let the grill preheat:

- **High heat:** 10–15 minutes to 450–550°F lid thermometer
- **Medium-high:** 15–20 minutes to 375–450°F lid thermometer
- **Medium / indirect:** 20–25 minutes to 325–375°F lid thermometer

The lid thermometer on the CC1830S reads the air temperature near the lid, which runs about 25–50°F hotter than grate level during a two-zone setup. If you have a grate-level probe, use it to confirm actual cooking temp.

---

## Step 7: Oil the grates

Right before loading food — not earlier:

1. Fold a paper towel into a small pad.
2. Grip it with long tongs.
3. Dip in neutral oil (canola or vegetable).
4. Wipe across the grate 2–3 passes.

This prevents sticking and creates a better sear. Don't oil a cold grate — the oil sits and carbonizes unevenly.

---

## Step 8: Load the food

Now you're ready. A few universal rules:

**Do's**
- Start thick cuts (chicken thighs, pork chops, thick steaks) on the **cool zone** to cook through, then move to the **hot zone** for the final sear.
- Start thin cuts (burgers, hot dogs, thin sausage, skewers) on the **hot zone** immediately — they cook fast enough without indirect time.
- Keep the lid **closed** as much as possible during cooking. Every time you open the lid, you lose 25–50°F and add 5–10 minutes.
- Use an instant-read thermometer to confirm doneness — time alone is unreliable.

**Don'ts**
- Don't put cold, wet food directly on a screaming-hot grate — blot dry first.
- Don't overcrowd the grate; leave space between items so heat circulates.
- Don't leave the lid propped open while food is on indirect heat — you've just made an outdoor oven with the door open.

---

## First cook checklist

Use this before every cook until it's automatic:

- [ ] Ash tray emptied
- [ ] Charcoal pan height set for this cook
- [ ] Chimney lit and fully ready (all coals gray)
- [ ] Coals poured into correct layout (two-zone / full-bed / banked)
- [ ] Vents set to starting position
- [ ] Grate on, lid closed, preheat timer running
- [ ] Instant-read thermometer within reach
- [ ] Tongs and gloves at the grill
- [ ] Food prepped, dried, seasoned, and at room temp
- [ ] Grate oiled right before loading

---

## After the cook: shutdown and cleanup

1. Close all vents fully to starve the fire of oxygen.
2. Close the lid.
3. Let the grill cool completely — minimum 2 hours, often overnight.
4. Once fully cold, empty the ash tray into a metal container (never plastic; embers can survive 24+ hours).
5. Brush grates clean while slightly warm if possible.
6. Store with the lid closed.

**Never leave a lit grill unattended.** Charcoal fires can reignite if vents are left open.
