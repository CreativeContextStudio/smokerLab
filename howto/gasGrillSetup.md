# Weber Spirit II – Setup & First Cook

**Platform:** Weber Spirit II E-210 (2 burners) or E-310 (3 burners), LP gas.

***

## 1. Propane tank connection and leak check

**Before your first cook:**

1. Close all burner knobs (turn to OFF).
2. Connect the LP regulator to the tank valve — hand-tight, no tools.
3. Open the tank valve **slowly** (counterclockwise) one full turn.
4. Perform a leak check: brush soapy water on the regulator hose and connections. Bubbles = leak. Close valve, tighten connections, recheck before proceeding.
5. Never check for leaks with a flame.

**Do's**
- Always open the tank valve slowly to avoid triggering the regulator safety shutoff.
- Close the tank valve after every cook.

**Don'ts**
- Don't use the grill indoors, in a garage, or under a canopy.
- Don't store a connected propane tank inside any structure.

***

## 2. Lighting procedure (matchless ignition)

1. Open the grill lid fully before lighting.
2. Turn one burner knob to **HIGH** and press the ignitor button continuously until it lights (1–3 clicks).
3. Visually confirm the burner is lit before closing the lid.
4. If it doesn't light in 5 clicks, wait 1 minute (to clear gas) and try again.
5. Turn remaining burners to HIGH once the first is confirmed lit.
6. Close lid to begin preheat.

**Do's**
- Always light with lid **open** to prevent gas accumulation.
- Use a long match or lighter through the side access hole if ignitor fails.

**Don'ts**
- Don't close lid until at least one burner is confirmed lit.
- Don't leave unlit gas running for more than 5 seconds.

***

## 3. Preheat routine

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| All burners HIGH | Lid closed |
| 10–15 minutes | Wait for lid thermometer to reach 500°F+ |
| Brush grates | Use a grill brush while grates are hot |
| Adjust burners | Dial to your cooking pattern |

**Why 10–15 minutes?** The porcelain-enameled cast iron grates on the Spirit II have significant thermal mass. They need this time to fully saturate with heat. The lid thermometer shows air temp—grate temp takes longer to catch up.

**Do's**
- Preheat to HIGH every time, even for lower-temp cooks.
- Brush grates after preheat (not before), when residue burns loose easily.

**Don'ts**
- Don't start cooking until preheat is complete; food sticks and sears unevenly.

***

## 4. Zone cooking configurations

### Direct heat — full grate
All burners on. Food directly over flame. Best for: burgers, hot dogs, skewers, seafood, vegetables.

### Two-zone (direct + indirect)
**E-310:** Left burner HIGH or MEDIUM-HIGH (direct). Center burner OFF or LOW (buffer). Right burner OFF (indirect). Food on right for indirect.

**E-210:** Left burner HIGH or MEDIUM-HIGH (direct). Right burner OFF (indirect). Food on right for indirect.

Best for: thick steaks (reverse sear), pork chops, chicken pieces, sausages.

### Indirect roasting
**E-310:** Left + right burners LOW-MEDIUM. Center burner OFF. Food over center. Lid closed throughout.

**E-210:** Left burner LOW-MEDIUM. Right burner OFF. Food on right. Lid closed throughout.

Best for: whole chicken, spatchcock chicken, dense vegetables, thick roasts.

| Zone Pattern | Lid Therm Target | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| All burners HIGH | 500–600°F | Searing, burgers, shrimp |
| Two-zone (direct HIGH, indirect OFF) | 400–500°F | Steaks, chops, chicken |
| Indirect (outer LOW, center OFF) | 325–400°F | Roasting, gentle finish |
| All burners MEDIUM | 375–425°F | Kebabs, vegetables, bread |

***

## 5. Temperature control via burner knobs

The Spirit II has no vents to manage—temperature is controlled entirely by knob position.

| Knob setting | Approximate grate zone temp |
| --- | --- |
| HIGH | 550–650°F directly over burner |
| MEDIUM-HIGH | 450–550°F |
| MEDIUM | 375–425°F |
| LOW | 300–350°F |
| OFF | Ambient (indirect zone) |

**Note:** The lid thermometer reads air temperature at grate height, not grate surface. It typically reads 50–75°F lower than grate surface on HIGH. For cooking decisions, use internal meat temperature (instant-read thermometer), not lid thermometer alone.

**Do's**
- Make small adjustments (one click at a time) when tuning temperature.
- Account for wind: cold or windy days can drop grate temps by 50–100°F.

**Don'ts**
- Don't rely solely on the lid thermometer for doneness—always use an instant-read for meat.

***

## 6. Cleaning routine

**After every cook:**
1. Turn all burners to HIGH for 5 minutes with lid closed (burns off residue).
2. Open lid; brush grates while hot.
3. Turn all burners OFF. Close tank valve.
4. Allow to cool before covering.

**Monthly (or every 5 cooks):**
- Pull grates and Flavorizer bars; scrape debris into grease management system.
- Check grease tray and drip pan; empty and wipe when 1/4 full.
- Inspect burner tubes for blockages (spider webs are common in gas grills).

**Seasonally:**
- Deep-clean burner ports with a wire brush.
- Check igniter wires and battery.
- Inspect hose and regulator for cracks.

**Do's**
- Keep grease tray empty; grease fires cause flare-ups and can damage the grill.
- Use a cover when not in use.

**Don'ts**
- Don't use oven cleaner on the exterior—it damages porcelain enamel.
- Don't put grates or Flavorizer bars in the dishwasher.
