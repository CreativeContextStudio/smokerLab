Here’s a **compact “control panel” guide** for your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S** that you can print, laminate, and tape to a shelf or the side of the grill cart. It treats the smoker like a simple thermostat, not a textbook.

***

## Royal Gourmet CC1830S “Control Panel”

***

### 1. Main names and jobs

- **CC1830S** = **Grill main chamber + offset firebox**  
- **Firebox** (left side): Where you burn **charcoal + wood**.  
- **Grill chamber** (right side): Where you cook **brisket, ribs, chicken, veggies, etc.**  
- **Charcoal basket** (in the firebox): Holds your coal in a tidy pile for clean burning.  

***

### 2. The three main knobs (vents)

Think of these as your **temperature dials**:

| Control | Location | What it does | How to use it |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Firebox intake vent** | On the **bottom of the firebox** | Controls **how much air enters the firebox**; your main “heat knob.” | - **Open more** = hotter, faster burn.  
- **Close more (1/4–1/3 open)** = slower, steadier burn for 225–275°F. [best practice] |
| **Main top vent** | On the **lid of the main chamber** | Lets hot air and smoke escape; keeps the chamber from getting suffocating. | - **Keep mostly open** most of the time.  
- **Partially close** only if you need to cool the pit slightly. [best practice] |
| **Side chimney / baffle** | Connector between firebox and grill chamber | Guides smoke under the grate instead of rushing up the stack. | - **Fully open** for even heat.  
- **Partially closed** for more smoke and slower airflow. [best practice] |

***

### 3. Charcoal and fire basics

- **Basket:**  
  - Put the **charcoal basket in the firebox** and fill it with unlit charcoal, then add a chimney‑full of lit charcoal on top.  
- **Fuel:**  
  - Use **charcoal as your main fuel** and **small wood chunks** for smoke flavor.  
- **Airflow:**  
  - Let air in through the **firebox intake** and let smoke out through the **main top vent**.  

***

### 4. How to hold temp at 225–250°F (most common)

1. **Build a fire** in the firebox with a charcoal basket and 1–2 small wood chunks.  
2. **Open the firebox intake** fully until the smoker reaches 225–250°F.  
3. **Close the firebox intake to 1/4–1/3** once you’re there.  
4. **Keep the main top vent mostly open**.  
5. **Adjust:**  
   - If it’s too hot, close the firebox intake a bit more.  
   - If it’s too cold, open the firebox intake a bit more.  

***

### 5. Safety and best practices

- **Always keep the lid closed** as much as possible to maintain heat and smoke.  
- **Use a heat‑resistant glove** when handling the firebox or main grate.  
- **Clean the ash pan** regularly to keep air flowing.
