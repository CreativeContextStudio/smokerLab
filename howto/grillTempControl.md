# Temperature Control on the Charcoal Grill

How to dial in and hold the temperatures you want on the **Royal Gourmet CC1830S main chamber**. Charcoal grilling doesn't have a knob to turn — but it has three levers that work together predictably once you understand them. This guide explains each lever, how to combine them, and how to recover when temperature drifts.

---

## The three levers

Every temperature decision on the CC1830S comes down to these three controls:

### Lever 1: Charcoal amount and layout

This sets the total heat capacity of your fire. More charcoal = more heat output and longer burn time. Layout determines where the heat goes.

| Amount | Charcoal (approx) | Good for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1/2 chimney | ~2\u20133 lb | Small indirect cook, finishing, keeping things warm |
| 3/4 chimney | ~3\u20134 lb | Medium-heat grilling, 2\u20134 items |
| 1 full chimney | ~4\u20135 lb | Standard grill session, two-zone, most recipes |
| 1.5\u20132 chimneys | ~5\u20136 lb | High-volume cooking, mass grilling, full-bed high heat |

Never exceed **6 lb total** in the main chamber.

### Lever 2: Charcoal pan height

This sets how close the coals are to the cooking grate. Pan height is your biggest single-variable adjustment for temperature intensity.

| Position | Heat at grate | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **High** | 450\u2013600\u00B0F directly above coals | Searing, smash burgers, quick char finishes |
| **Mid** | 375\u2013450\u00B0F | Standard grilling — chops, chicken thighs, sausage, veggies |
| **Low** | 275\u2013375\u00B0F | Indirect roasting, gentle finishing, holding temps |

Changing pan height is a **coarse** adjustment — do it before you light the fire, or carefully mid-cook with heat-resistant gloves before the grate is loaded.

### Lever 3: Vents (airflow)

Vents control how much oxygen reaches the coals, which directly controls burn rate and temperature.

- **More open = more oxygen = hotter and faster burn**
- **More closed = less oxygen = cooler and slower burn**

| Vent position | Effect | Use when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Fully open | Maximum temp, fastest burn | Searing, preheat, trying to come up to temp |
| 3/4 open | High-medium heat | Most active grilling at 400\u2013500\u00B0F |
| 1/2 open | Medium heat | Chicken thighs, pork chops, sausage at 350\u2013400\u00B0F |
| 1/4 open | Low-medium | Indirect roasting at 300\u2013350\u00B0F |
| Nearly closed | Very low, controlled slow burn | Finishing, holding, extending a dying fire |

**Always adjust in small increments.** A vent move from 1/2 to fully open will take 10–15 minutes to fully show its effect. Don't chase the thermometer by slamming vents open or closed — you'll overshoot every time.

## Reading the lid thermometer

The CC1830S's lid-mounted thermometer is useful but not precise. Use it as a reference alongside these cues:

**Visual cues for heat level:**
- Hold your hand 5 inches above the grate (over coals side, no food):
  - **1–2 seconds:** High heat (450°F+)
  - **3–4 seconds:** Medium-high (375–450°F)
  - **5–6 seconds:** Medium (325–375°F)
  - **7+ seconds:** Low heat

**When to trust the lid thermometer:**
- For overall chamber temp during indirect cooking (it reads reasonably well with lid closed and no lid-opening)
- For tracking temperature trend over time (rising vs. falling)

**When NOT to trust it:**
- Right after opening the lid — it drops fast and takes 3–5 minutes to rebound
- For precise meat doneness — always use an instant-read thermometer in the meat

## Managing charcoal additions mid-cook

Charcoal fires run 45–75 minutes on a full chimney load before the heat starts dropping noticeably. Here's how to extend a cook:

**Option A: Add unlit charcoal (slower):**
- Carefully lift the grate or push food to one side.
- Pour 1–2 handfuls of unlit briquettes directly onto the existing coals.
- The existing fire will slowly ignite them over 15–20 minutes.
- Good for low-and-slow indirect cooks where you can afford a small temp dip.

**Option B: Add pre-lit coals (faster):**
- Start a half-chimney in a separate chimney starter while the cook is in progress.
- When the coals are ready, carefully add them to the pan.
- Minimal temperature disruption.
- Best approach for maintaining precise temp on longer indirect cooks.

**Do's**
- Plan ahead — if you're 45 minutes into a 90-minute cook, start the extra chimney at the 30-minute mark.
- Always add charcoal to the hot zone side, not under food.

**Don'ts**
- Never add lighter fluid to a hot fire or existing coals. This is a safety hazard and ruins food flavor.
- Don't dump a large load of unlit coals on a small dying fire — they'll smother it.

## Recovering from temperature problems

### Running too hot (over target by 50°F+):

1. **Close vents down** by 1/4 to 1/3 of current position.
2. Wait 10–15 minutes.
3. If still too hot, move food to cool zone and close vents another notch.
4. As a last resort: carefully remove some coals to a metal bucket with a lid.

### Running too cool (under target by 50°F+):

1. **Open vents** by 1/4 increments.
2. Wait 10 minutes.
3. If still low, add a small amount of pre-lit charcoal.
4. Open lid briefly to give the fire more oxygen — then close and let it recover.

### Fire going out:

This usually means the ash tray is clogged (check first) or there's not enough charcoal left. Use a chimney to restart coals, then add to the existing bed.

## Vent strategy cheat sheet

**For a two-zone cook at medium-high (most chicken, chops, sausage):**
- Start: vents 3/4 open
- After preheat (15–20 min, lid ~400°F): vents 1/2 to 3/4 open
- During cook: adjust 1/4 at a time if temp drifts more than 25°F

**For a high-heat sear (steaks, burgers, smash):**
- Start: vents fully open
- Keep fully open the whole sear — this is a short, hot cook

**For indirect roasting (spatchcock chicken, thick cuts):**
- Start: vents 1/2 open
- After preheat: vents 1/3 to 1/2 open depending on lid thermometer reading
- If running hot: close down to 1/4 open

## Do's / Don'ts

**Do's**
- Make one adjustment at a time and wait the full 10–15 minutes before tweaking again.
- Use pan height for the big jumps, vents for fine tuning.
- Keep the lid closed as much as possible — every opening costs you 25–50°F and 5–10 minutes of recovery time.
- Get a grate-level probe if you want to stop guessing at the lid-to-grate correction factor.

**Don'ts**
- Don't chase a perfect number; charcoal grilling has a 25–50°F natural swing. Work in ranges, not exact degrees.
- Don't open the lid repeatedly to check food — you're bleeding heat and slowing the cook.
- Don't fully close both top and bottom vents while food is on the grill; you'll choke the fire and potentially get off-flavors from an oxygen-starved smolder.
- Don't skip the ash tray check before lighting. A clogged ash tray is the #1 cause of "my fire won't come up to temp."
