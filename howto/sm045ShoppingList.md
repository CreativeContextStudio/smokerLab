# SM045 Shopping List

Essential supplies and accessories for the **Cookshack SuperSmoker Elite SM045** electric smoker.

---

## What the SM045 already includes

Before you buy anything, know what comes in the box:

- Built-in digital controller with meat probe
- Three 14" x 18" nickel-plated cooking racks
- Removable steel wood box
- Aluminum drip pan
- Side racks

You do not need a separate thermometer to start cooking. The built-in probe handles meat temp monitoring.

---

## Essential supplies

### Wood chunks

Wood is the most important consumable on the SM045. You need chunks — not chips, not pellets — cut to roughly 1–2" pieces.

| Wood species | Best for | Smoke character |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hickory | Brisket, pork butt, ribs | Classic BBQ, strong and savory |
| Oak | Brisket, beef, pastrami | Clean, medium intensity |
| Apple | Pork, poultry, bacon, seafood | Mild, slightly sweet |
| Cherry | Poultry, pork, salmon | Mild, slightly fruity |
| Pecan | Brisket, pork, ribs | Nutty, medium intensity |
| Alder | Salmon, delicate seafood | Very mild, almost neutral |
| Maple | Bacon, pork belly, poultry | Light, slightly sweet |

**How much to buy:** Start with a variety pack. Most SM045 cooks use 1–2 oz of wood per cook. A 1 lb bag of any species will last dozens of cooks.

**Where to buy:** Any BBQ supply store, hardware store, or online. Look for fist-sized chunks or buy larger pieces and break them down.

### Kitchen scale

You need this to weigh wood. "1 oz of hickory" is meaningless if you're eyeballing it. Over-smoking is the most common SM045 mistake and a $15 kitchen scale prevents it completely.

### Disposable aluminum drip pans

The SM045 comes with one drip pan. Stock several extras — they catch rendered fat and drips, and make cleanup trivial. Standard 9"x13" pans work well.

### Butcher paper and foil

- **Butcher paper (unwaxed pink/peach):** For wrapping brisket and pork butt mid-cook to push through the stall. Breathes slightly, preserves bark better than foil.
- **Heavy-duty aluminum foil:** For wrapping, resting, and holding finished meats.

---

## Recommended accessories

### Secondary instant-read thermometer

The SM045's built-in probe is accurate but has one fixed location. An instant-read thermometer lets you check multiple spots on a large piece and verify doneness before pulling.

### Wire cooling rack

A wire rack over a sheet pan is useful for:
- Forming a pellicle (tacky surface) on salmon, bacon, and pastrami before smoking
- Resting meats after pulling from the cabinet
- Air-drying poultry skin before a smoke run

### Vacuum sealer

Smoked meats freeze and reheat exceptionally well under vacuum. If you batch-cook brisket, pork butt, or ribs, a vacuum sealer pays for itself quickly.

---

## What you do NOT need for the SM045

- Chimney starter — no charcoal
- Charcoal — no fire management
- Gasket tape or RTV sealant — the SM045 is factory-sealed
- Baffle plates or tuning plates — no firebox
- High-temperature rated thermometer probes — 300°F max cabinet temp is well within standard probe range
- Spray bottle for spritzing — the sealed cabinet retains moisture naturally
