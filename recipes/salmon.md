Here’s a **tight, step‑by‑step guide** for smoked salmon on your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S** at **225–250°F**, with **do’s / don’ts** and time targets for a **1–2 lb salmon fillet** (skin‑on). This method focuses on **moist, flaky salmon with a light smoke flavor** and optional crispy skin.

***

## 1. Prep the salmon (1–2 hours before smoking)

- **Choose the salmon:**  
  - Skin‑on salmon fillet (1–2 lb), preferably with even thickness.  
- **Prepare the skin:**  
  - Use a **sharp fillet knife** to remove any pin bones.  
  - Rinse the salmon under cold water and pat extremely dry with paper towels.  

**Do’s**  
- Leave the skin on for moisture and support during smoking.  
- Let the salmon sit uncovered in the fridge for **1–2 hours** to dry the surface slightly (optional but great for crisp).  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t leave the salmon at room temp for long; keep it cold until you’re ready to smoke.  
- Don’t skip the drying step; wet skin = soggy salmon.  

***

## 2. Brine the salmon (1–2 hours before smoking)

- **Brine ingredients (per 1 lb salmon):**  
  - 1 quart water  
  - 50 g kosher salt  
  - 30 g sugar  
  - 1 tsp black pepper  
  - 1 tsp garlic powder (optional)  
  - 1 tsp smoked paprika (optional)  

**Instructions:**  
- Dissolve salt and sugar in water, then add remaining spices. Cool to room temp.  
- Submerge the salmon in the brine, skin‑side up, and refrigerate for **1–2 hours**.  
- After 1–2 hours, remove the salmon, rinse well under cold water, and pat extremely dry with paper towels.  

**Do’s**  
- Use a **plastic container** to keep the brine contained.  
- Poke a few small holes in the skin with a fork to help the brine penetrate.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t brine longer than 2 hours; it can make the salmon too salty.  
- Don’t skip the rinsing step; excess salt can ruin the flavor.  

***

## 3. Season the salmon (right before smoking)

- **Choose a rub/seasoning (optional):**  
  - Simple option: Salt, pepper, garlic powder, and a pinch of paprika.  
  - Optional: Add a light glaze of **brown sugar** or **honey** for a candy‑crust effect.  

**Do’s**  
- Pat the salmon dry, then rub the seasoning lightly on the flesh side (not the skin side).  
- Let it sit at room temp for **10–15 minutes** to let the seasoning penetrate.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t oversalt; the brine already adds salt.  
- Don’t apply a thick glaze; keep it light for a delicate smoke flavor.  

***

## 4. Set up the Royal Gourmet smoker

- **Target temp:** 225–250°F for salmon (this is **hotter** than brisket, which is fine for a small fish).
- **Fire:**
  - Charcoal basket in the firebox, building a **hot charcoal bed** (not minion‑style; this is a fast, high‑heat cook).
  - Add 1–2 small wood chunks (apple, hickory, or maple) for a light smoke flavor. [best practice]
- **Vents:**
  - Stack **fully open**; firebox intake **1/2–3/4 open** to reach 225–250°F. [best practice]
- **Pan:**
  - Place a shallow water pan under the grate opposite the heat source to catch drips and keep the smoker clean. [best practice]

**Do’s**
- Let the smoker come to temp **before** you load the salmon.
- Use a **grate‑level thermometer** near the salmon to monitor the surface temp.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t start the salmon too early; the smoker should be hot and stable before you load it.
- Don’t overcrowd the grate; salmon needs space for even air circulation.

## SM045 Setup

Set your SM045 to **200–225°F** — slightly lower than the offset version because carryover in the insulated cabinet is more pronounced. Preheat fully with the door closed.

Load **1 oz of apple or alder chunks** in the wood box. These are gentle woods; in the SM045’s low-airflow environment, even mild wood produces noticeable smoke on delicate fish. Less is more.

Place the brined, dried salmon skin-side down on the middle rack. If you have a wire rack insert, use it so air circulates under the fillet. Insert the probe into the thickest part.

Begin probing at **20 minutes**. Pull at **130–135°F** for medium texture or **140°F** for well-done flaky. Carryover in the SM045 adds 5–10°F after the door opens, so pulling a few degrees early is the right call.

The SM045’s humid environment is excellent for salmon — moisture retention is natural, and the brine-and-cabinet combination produces consistently moist results. No basting needed during the cook.

## 5. Place the salmon on the offset

- **Position:** Stack side of the chamber, not directly over the hottest coals.  
- **Orientation:**  
  - Place the salmon **skin‑side down** on a lightly oiled wire rack or directly on the grate to allow air circulation.  
  - If you’re worried about the skin sticking, place a **thin sheet of parchment paper** under the salmon.  

**Do’s**  
- Use **tongs or a heat‑resistant glove** to place the salmon gently on the grate.  
- Double‑check that the skin is not stuck to the grate before you close the lid.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t put the salmon directly over the hottest coals; it can burn the skin.  
- Don’t move it around once it’s on the grate.  

***

## 6. Smoke time and temp targets (225–250°F)

**Goal:** 145°F internal at the thickest part, with **flaky, moist meat** and optional **crispy skin**. [best practice]  

| Temp range | Time (approx) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 225–250°F | 15–30 minutes | Great for a 1–2 lb salmon fillet, with a nice balance of smoke and tenderness. |

**Do’s**  
- Use a **meat‑probe thermometer** inserted into the thickest part of the salmon.  
- Check the temp every 5–10 minutes after the first 10 minutes.  
- If the skin is getting too dark, move the salmon to a cooler part of the grate or slightly lower the temp.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the salmon.  
- Don’t let the internal temp exceed 145°F; it can dry out.  

***

## 7. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (1–2 lb salmon fillet at 225–250°F)

Use this as a mental guide; adjust for your salmon’s behavior.

| Time since loading the salmon | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| 0–10 min | Load the salmon skin‑side down on the offset, close the lid. [best practice] |
| 10–15 min | No opening; let the salmon come to temp and the smoke start to build. |
| 15–20 min | Check the internal temp; it should be 110–120°F. If it’s on the lower side, keep going. |
| 20–25 min | Check again; if it’s 130–140°F, start thinking about finishing. |
| 25–30 min | Check again; if it’s 140–145°F, remove the salmon. [best practice] |

**Target finish:** 145°F internal, **flaky and moist** with optional **crispy skin**.  

***

## 8. Finish with a crispy sear (optional but highly recommended)

- **Option A (internal crisp):**  
  - Increase the smoker temp to 300–350°F for the last 5–10 minutes to crisp the skin.  
  - Keep the lid closed and watch for the skin to turn golden and crackle.  

- **Option B (external sear):**  
  - Remove the salmon at 145°F and let it rest for 5–10 minutes.  
  - Then, pan‑sear the skin side in a hot skillet with a little oil for 1–2 minutes per side for a super‑crispy crust.  

**Do’s**  
- Use a **thermometer** to confirm 145°F before you finish.  
- Let the salmon rest for 5–10 minutes before serving to let the juices redistribute.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t let the internal temp exceed 145°F; it can dry out.  
- Don’t skip the rest; it’s critical for juicy meat.  

***

## 9. Serve and store

- **Serve warm** or at room temp, depending on your preference.  
- **Optional:**  
  - Drizzle with a little olive oil or butter for extra flavor.  
  - Add a squeeze of lemon or herbs for brightness.  
- **Store** in the fridge for up to 2 days; reheat in a 350°F oven for 5–10 minutes to crisp the skin again.  

***

## 10. Do’s / Don’ts for smoked salmon on your offset

**Do’s**  
- Use a **sharp fillet knife** for trimming.  
- Keep the skin on and dry before seasoning.  
- Use a **thermometer** and **probe tenderness** as your final decision‑maker. [best practice]  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t remove the skin; it’s key for moisture and support.  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the salmon.  
- Don’t skip the brine; it enhances flavor and moisture.
