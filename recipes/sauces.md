Here are **8–10 sauces** that pair beautifully with the smoked entrees we’ve talked about—**brisket (sliced & pulled), pork butts, ribs, chicken pieces, bacon, salmon, swordfish, and smoked veggies**. All of them are **easy to pull off on a townhome patio setup** and can live on a board or platter next to the food.

***

### 1. Classic ranch‑style ranch (for chicken, pork, ribs, veggies)

- **Why it works:** Cools heat, carries smoke, and clings to crispy skin and juicy meat.  
- **Core idea:** Sour cream base with buttermilk, herbs, and garlic.  
- **Serve with:** Spatchcocked chicken, chicken thighs/wings, ribs, smoked veggies, bacon.

**Simple formula:**  
- 1 cup sour cream  
- 1/2 cup buttermilk (or mayo + splash of milk)  
- 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder  
- 1 tbsp each parsley and dill (fresh or dried)  
- Salt & pepper to taste  

***

### 2. Cilantro‑lime crema (for pork, chicken, fish, veggies)

- **Why it works:** Bright acidity cuts through smoke and fat, especially on ribs, pork butt, swordfish, and veggies.  
- **Serve with:** Pork ribs, smoked pork butt, salmon, swordfish, smoked veggie platter, spatchcocked chicken.

**Simple formula:**  
- 1 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt  
- Juice of 1–2 limes  
- 1/2 cup cilantro, finely chopped  
- 1 small garlic clove, minced  
- 1/2 tsp salt, tiny pinch of cumin  

***

### 3. Alabama‑style white BBQ sauce (perfect for chicken & pork)

- **Why it works:** Tangy, creamy, vinegar‑forward; balances smoke on chicken, ribs, and pulled pork.  
- **Serve with:** Smoked chicken pieces, ribs, pulled pork or brisket sliders, grilled chicken thighs.

**Simple formula:**  
- 1 cup mayonnaise  
- 2–3 tbsp apple cider vinegar  
- 1 tbsp lemon juice  
- 1 tsp sugar, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika  
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp onion powder  
- Salt & pepper to taste  

***

### 4. Smoked salsa / grilled salsa fresca (for almost everything)

- **Why it works:** Uses exactly the smoked veggies you made earlier—tomatoes, peppers, onions, cilantro, lime.  
- **Serve with:** Brisket, pulled pork, smoked pork butt, ribs, chicken, fish, and as a topping for avocado or chips.

**Simple formula:**  
- 2 cups smoked & peeled tomatoes, roughly chopped  
- 1 smoked bell pepper, seeded & chopped  
- 1/2 smoked onion, finely diced  
- 1–2 jalapeños (raw or smoked), minced  
- 1/2 cup cilantro, chopped  
- 1–2 limes, juiced  
- Salt to taste  

***

### 5. Spicy “candy‑sriracha” glaze (for smoked bacon, ribs, pork butt)

- **Why it works:** Sweet, sticky, spicy finish for bacon‑style bacon, ribs, or pulled pork.  
- **Serve with:** Smoked bacon pieces, pork ribs, smoked pork butt (sliced or pulled), smoked chicken thighs.

**Simple formula:**  
- 1/2 cup brown sugar  
- 1/3 cup sriracha (or chili‑garlic sauce)  
- 2 tbsp soy sauce  
- 1 tbsp honey  
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika  
Simmer 5–7 minutes until thickened; use as a brush or dip.

***

### 6. Mango‑habanero glaze (for pork, chicken, ribs, salmon)

- **Why it works:** Sweet, tropical, spicy; balances richer meats and oily fish beautifully.  
- **Serve with:** Pork ribs, pulled pork or brisket sliders, salmon, swordfish, chicken thighs.

**Simple formula:**  
- 1/2 cup mango puree (or finely chopped mango)  
- 2–3 tbsp apple cider vinegar  
- 1–2 tbsp honey or brown sugar  
- 1 small habanero (seeded and minced) or 1 tsp habanero sauce  
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder, salt to taste  

***

### 7. Smoked garlic aioli (for fish, chicken, smoked veggies, as a sandwich spread)

- **Why it works:** Rich, garlicky, smoky; great on sandwiches or as a veggie dip.  
- **Serve with:** Salmon, swordfish, smoked chicken, smoked veggies, sliders, pulled pork or brisket in bread.

**Simple formula:**  
- 1 cup mayonnaise  
- 3–4 smoked or roasted garlic cloves, mashed  
- 1 tbsp lemon juice  
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika  
- Salt & pepper to taste  

***

### 8. Chimichurri (for beef, pork, chicken, fish)

- **Why it works:** Fresh parsley, garlic, acid, and oil; cuts through smoke and fat brilliantly.  
- **Serve with:** Sliced or pulled brisket, pork ribs, chicken thighs, salmon, or drizzled over smoked veggies.

**Simple formula:**  
- 1 cup parsley, finely chopped  
- 1/4 cup cilantro (optional)  
- 4–6 garlic cloves, minced  
- 1/2 cup olive oil  
- 3 tbsp red wine vinegar  
- 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes, salt & pepper to taste  

***

### 9. Smoky chili‑lime crema (for ribs, pork, chicken, bacon, veggies)

- **Why it works:** Fairly versatile—spicy, limey, creamy.  
- **Serve with:** Pork ribs, smoked pork butt, smoked bacon, chicken wings, smoked veggies.

**Simple formula:**  
- 1 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt  
- 1–2 tbsp lime juice  
- 1 tsp smoked paprika  
- 1/2 tsp chili powder  
- 1/2–1 tsp hot sauce (like Cholula)  
- Salt to taste  

***

### 10. Sweet & tangy BBQ‑style mop (for pork, ribs, brisket, chicken)

- **Why it works:** Thin, tangy, slightly sweet; great for basting or using as a table‑side mop.  
- **Serve with:** Pork ribs, brisket, smoked pork butt, spatchcocked chicken.

**Simple formula:**  
- 1 cup cider vinegar  
- 1/2 cup ketchup or tomato paste  
- 1/4 cup brown sugar  
- 1 tbsp mustard  
- 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder  
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika  
- Salt & pepper to taste  

***

### How to pair sauces with your lineup

- **Brisket (sliced or pulled):** Chimichurri, Alabama white sauce, smoky chili‑lime crema, BBQ mop.  
- **Smoked pork butt / ribs:** Alabama white sauce, sweet & tangy BBQ mop, spicy sriracha glaze, mango‑habanero.  
- **Chicken pieces:** Cilantro‑lime crema, Alabama white sauce, ranch, BBQ mop.  
- **Bacon:** Spicy sriracha‑sweet glaze, chili‑lime crema, BBQ mop.  
- **Fish (salmon, swordfish):** Smoked garlic aioli, cilantro‑lime crema, chimichurri.  
- **Smoked veggies:** Smoked salsa, chimichurri, ranch, cilantro‑lime crema.
