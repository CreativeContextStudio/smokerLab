**Step‑by‑step guide** for **garlic bread or flatbread**, tuned for **baguette slices or naan/flatbreads** over **direct medium heat**. Total time: **3–5 minutes active grilling** after preheat, smoky char, buttery crust.

***

## 1. Prep the bread (10 minutes before grilling)

- **Choose the bread:**  
  - **Garlic bread:** Baguette or French loaf, sliced 3/4–1" thick.  
  - **Flatbread:** Naan, pita, or lavash (store‑bought or homemade).  
- **Garlic butter:**  
  - 1/2 cup softened butter + 4–6 minced garlic cloves + 1 tbsp parsley (chopped) + pinch salt.  

**Do’s**  
- Slice baguette diagonally for more surface area.  
- Spread garlic butter generously on **one side** of each slice/flatbread.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t slice too thin (<1/2"); burns fast.  
- Don’t overload butter; drips flare up.  

***

## 2. Set up the grill

<!-- equipment: charcoal-grill -->
- **Target temp:** Direct **medium (375–425°F)**.
- **Fire setup:**
  - **Charcoal pan in mid position.**
  - **Full bed** of charcoal across the grate (1 chimney load, even layer). [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)
- **Vents:**
  - **Mostly open** for steady airflow. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

**Do’s**
- **Preheat 10 minutes** with lid closed until lid thermometer reads ~400°F.
- Brush grates clean while hot.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t preheat too long; bread cooks fast.
<!-- /equipment -->

<!-- equipment: gas-grill -->
- **Target temp:** Direct **medium (375–425°F)**.
- **Weber Spirit II setup:**
  - **E‑310:** All three burners MEDIUM.
  - **E‑210:** Both burners MEDIUM.
  - Light with lid **open**, then close to preheat.
- **Preheat:** All burners MEDIUM, lid closed **10 min** until ~400°F.

**Do’s**
- Gas MEDIUM is ideal for bread—consistent heat, no flare-ups from butter drips if you manage knobs.
- If butter causes a flare-up, just lower one burner briefly.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t go above MEDIUM; garlic burns at HIGH and turns bitter.
<!-- /equipment -->  

***

## 3. Place the bread on the grill

- **Position:**  
  - **Direct over coals** across the hot grate.  
- **Orientation:**  
  - **Butter/garlic side up** first (protects topping).  

**Do’s**  
- Grill **1.5–2 minutes per side** (butter‑side up first, then flip).  
- Use **long tongs** to flip; watch for char marks.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t overcrowd; steaming dulls crust.  
- Don’t press down; crusts best untouched.  

***

## 4. Grill time and temp targets (375–425°F direct)

**Goal:** Golden char, melted butter, no burnt garlic. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

| Bread type | Time per side | Visual cue |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Baguette slices** | 1.5–2 min | Golden edges, grill marks. |
| **Naan/flatbread** | 2–3 min | Bubbly, charred spots. |

**Do’s**  
- Check after first side; pull when golden/charred.  
- No thermometer; visual + smell is enough.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t let garlic burn black; bitter.  

***

***

## 6. Rest and serve

**Do’s**  
- Serve **immediately** while hot.  
- Pair with **steaks, chops, ribs, seafood** or as **appetizer base** with salsa.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t stack while hot; steams crust.  
- Don’t reheat; loses magic.  

***

## 7. Do’s / Don’ts for garlic bread/flatbread on CC1830S main grill

**Do’s**  
- Use **full medium grate** and **lid thermometer** to confirm ~400°F.  
- Butter **one side only**, grill butter‑up first.  
- Grill **lid open** for crust control.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t skip preheat; soft bread.  
- Don’t overcrowd; soggy instead of crisp.  

**Scale notes:**  
- **4–6 pieces:** Same timing.  
- **12+ pieces:** Cook in batches.
