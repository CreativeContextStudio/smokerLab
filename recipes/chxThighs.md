Here’s a **tight, step‑by‑step guide** for **smoked chicken thighs** on your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S**, tuned for **crispy skin, juicy meat**, and easy handling at **325–375°F**. This assumes **bone‑in, skin‑on chicken thighs** (about 1–1.5 lb per 4–6 pieces; adjust quantities as needed).

***

## 1. Prep the chicken thighs (1–2 hours before smoking)

### Trim and pat dry
- **Goal:** Remove excess fat and loose skin, but keep the main skin layer intact for crispy skin.

| Step | How to do it |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Rinse the thighs under cold water to remove any blood or debris. |
| 2 | Pat them extremely dry with paper towels. |
| 3 | Trim any excess fat or loose skin around the edges, but keep the skin on. |

**Do’s**  
- Use a **sharp boning or kitchen shears** to trim cleanly.  
- Let them sit uncovered in the fridge for **1–2 hours** to dry the skin slightly (optional but great for crisp).  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t remove all the skin; it’s key for crisp.  
- Don’t skip the drying step; wet skin = soggy thighs.  

***

## 2. Season the thighs (1–2 hours before smoking)

- **Choose a seasoning:**  
  - Simple option: Salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and a touch of sugar.  
  - Optional: Add herbs like rosemary, thyme, or oregano under the skin and on the surface.  

**Do’s**  
- Rub the seasoning **under and over the skin**, especially on the skin and where the thigh meets the leg.  
- Let it sit uncovered in the fridge for **1–2 hours** to let the rub penetrate.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t oversalt; you can always add more at the end.  
- Don’t apply a wet glaze yet; keep it dry for the smoke.  

***

## 3. Set up the Royal Gourmet smoker

- **Target temp:** 325–375°F for chicken thighs (this is **hotter** than brisket, which is fine for a small bird).  
- **Fire:**  
  - Charcoal basket in the firebox, building a **hot charcoal bed** (not minion‑style; this is a fast, high‑heat cook).  
  - Add 1–2 small wood chunks (apple, hickory, or maple) for a light smoke flavor. [best practice]  
- **Vents:**  
  - Stack **fully open**; firebox intake **1/2–3/4 open** to reach 325–375°F. [best practice]  
- **Pan:**  
  - Place a shallow water pan under the grate opposite the heat source to catch drips and keep the smoker clean. [best practice]  

**Do’s**  
- Let the smoker come to temp **before** you load the chicken.  
- Use a **grate‑level thermometer** near the thighs to monitor the surface temp.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t start the thighs too early; the smoker should be hot and stable before you load them.  
- Don’t overcrowd the grate; chicken thighs need space for even air circulation.  

***

## 4. Place the thighs on the offset

- **Position:** Stack side of the chamber, not directly over the hottest coals.  
- **Orientation:**  
  - Lay the thighs **skin‑side up** so the skin is exposed to the heat.  
  - Arrange them in a single layer, not stacked.  

**Do’s**  
- Use **tongs or a heat‑resistant glove** to place the thighs gently on the grate.  
- Double‑check that the skin is not stuck to the grate before you close the lid.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t put the thighs directly over the hottest coals; it can burn the skin.  
- Don’t move them around once they’re on the grate.  

***

## 5. Smoke time and temp targets (325–375°F)

**Goal:** 165°F internal at the thickest part of the thigh, **juicy with crispy skin**. [best practice]  

| Temp range | Time (approx) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 325–350°F | 30–45 minutes | Great for 4–6 thighs, with a nice balance of smoke and tenderness. |
| 350–375°F | 25–40 minutes | Faster cook, crisper skin, but watch for burning. |

**Do’s**  
- Use a **meat‑probe thermometer** inserted into the thickest part of the thigh.  
- Check the temp every 10–15 minutes after the first 20 minutes.  
- If the skin is getting too dark, move the thighs to a cooler part of the grate or slightly lower the temp.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the thighs.  
- Don’t let the internal temp exceed 170°F; it can dry out.  

***

## 6. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (6 bone‑in, skin‑on chicken thighs at 325–350°F)

Use this as a mental guide; adjust for your thighs’ behavior.

| Time since loading the thighs | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| 0–10 min | Load the thighs skin‑side up on the offset, close the lid. [best practice] |
| 10–20 min | No opening; let the thighs come to temp and the smoke start to build. |
| 20–30 min | Check the internal temp; it should be 120–140°F. If it’s on the lower side, keep going. |
| 30–40 min | Check again; if it’s 150–155°F, start thinking about finishing. |
| 40–45 min | Probe every 5–10 minutes until it reaches 165°F. [best practice] |

**Target finish:** 165°F internal, **juicy with crispy skin**.  

***

## 7. Finish with a crispy sear (optional but highly recommended)

- **Option A (internal crisp):**  
  - Increase the smoker temp to 375–400°F for the last 5–10 minutes to crisp the skin.  
  - Keep the lid closed and watch for the skin to turn golden and crackle.  

- **Option B (external sear):**  
  - Remove the thighs at 165°F and let them rest for 5–10 minutes.  
  - Then, pan‑sear the skin side in a hot skillet with a little oil for 1–2 minutes per side for a super‑crispy crust.  

**Do’s**  
- Use a **thermometer** to confirm 165°F before you finish.  
- Let the thighs rest for 5–10 minutes before serving to let the juices redistribute.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t let the internal temp exceed 170°F; it can dry out.  
- Don’t skip the rest; it’s critical for juicy meat.  

***

## 8. Serve and store

- **Serve warm** or at room temp, depending on your preference.  
- **Optional:**  
  - Drizzle with a little olive oil or butter for extra flavor.  
  - Add a squeeze of lemon or herbs for brightness.  
- **Store** in the fridge for up to 3–4 days; reheat in a 350°F oven for 10–15 minutes to crisp the skin again.  

***

## 9. Do’s / Don’ts for smoked chicken thighs on your offset

**Do’s**  
- Use a **sharp boning or kitchen shears** for trimming.  
- Keep the skin intact and dry before seasoning.  
- Use a **thermometer** and **probe tenderness** as your final decision‑maker. [best practice]  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t remove all the skin; it’s key for crisp.  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the thighs.  
- Don’t skip the rest after cooking; it’s critical for juicy meat.
