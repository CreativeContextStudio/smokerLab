Here’s a **full, vegetarian step‑by‑step plan** that you can run on your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S** for a crowd of **vegetarian guests**. It focuses on **flavorful, hearty smoked and grilled veggie dishes** that feel substantial and special, not “just sides.” You can serve this as a **centerpiece spread** or with bread, grains, or chips.

***

## 1. Menu plan overview

- **Main attraction:** Smoked eggplant and portobello mushrooms (like “meaty” mains).  
- **Supporting stars:** Smoked onions, peppers, and tomatoes for salsa & toppings.  
- **Fresh pop:** Warm grilled zucchini, asparagus, or squash salads.  
- **Carb side:** Warm crusty bread or pita for smearing/scooping.  

All of this fits inside a realistic **2.5–3.5 hour offset window** if you prep ahead and stagger smokes.

***

## 2. Prep ahead (Day of or the night before)

### Cleanup and organization

- **Clean the Royal Gourmet** and remove any old ash.  
- **Read your user manual** so you know about vents, ash pan, and safety tips. [royalgourmetcorp](https://www.royalgourmetcorp.com/pdfs/item/2022/20220310/7b0df926-9dae-4506-a09b-fecf9c00a128__CC1830S%20Use%20and%20Care%20Manual.pdf)
- **Pre‑soak charcoal** (if your coals need it, per the manual).  

### Veg prep

- **Wash, chop, and season veggies** so they’re ready to go on the smoker or grill:

| Veg | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| Eggplant (2 large) | Slice into 1/2" rounds, salt lightly, sit 15–30 min, then pat dry. |
| Portobello mushrooms (4–6 large caps) | Remove stems, brush dirt off, keep whole. |
| Bell peppers (3–4) | Quarter them, remove seeds and ribs. |
| Onions (2–3) | Cut into 1" wedges, leave skin on. |
| Tomatoes (2–3 large) | Halve or quarter; leave on the vine if possible. |
| Zucchini or summer squash (2–3) | Slice into 1/2" rounds. |
| Asparagus (1 bunch) | Trim ends. |
| Optional: Pineapple or mango (for salsa) | Cut into thick slices or chunks. |

**Do’s**  
- Use a **large sheet pan** or tray to keep each veg grouped.  
- Drizzle a little **olive oil** and light salt on everything except tomatoes if you want char but not mush.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t over‑salt; you can always add more at the end.  
- Don’t cut veggies too thin or they’ll fall through the grate.  

***

## 3. Set up the Royal Gourmet smoker (1–1.5 hours before serving)

- **Target temp:** 325–375°F for a **hot, fast smoke** (this is great for veggies).  
- **Fire:**  
  - Charcoal basket in the firebox, building a **hot charcoal bed** with 1–2 mild wood chunks (apple, maple, or oak). [royalgourmetcorp](https://www.royalgourmetcorp.com/pdfs/item/2020/20200922/45bdea1a-4c30-4965-976d-e9fea0aa2fa4__CC1830S.pdf)
- **Vents:**  
  - Stack **fully open**; firebox intake **1/2–3/4 open** to hold 325–375°F. [best practice]  
- **Pan:**  
  - Place a shallow water pan under the grate opposite the heat source to catch drips. [best practice]  

**Do’s**  
- Let the smoker come to temp before loading veggies.  
- Use a **grate‑level thermometer** near the veggies to monitor the surface temp.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t start the veggies too early; the smoker should be hot and stable before you load them.  
- Don’t overcrowd the grate; veggies need space for even air circulation.  

***

## 4. Smoke the stars (eggplant & portobello)

- **Load time:** First layer on the smoker, about 45–60 minutes before guests arrive.  
- **Place on the offset:**  
  - Eggplant slices **skin‑side down** on the grate.  
  - Portobello caps **gills up** on the grate.  

**Smoke time and temp targets (325–375°F):**

| Veg | Time (approx) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Eggplant | 15–20 minutes | Skin should be charred, flesh soft and creamy. |
| Portobello | 10–15 minutes | Gills should be soft and juicy, caps slightly charred. |

**Do’s**  
- Use a **meat‑probe thermometer** inserted into the thickest part of a mushroom cap (optional; usually not needed).  
- Check the veggies every 5–10 minutes after the first 10 minutes.  
- If the skin is getting too dark, move the veggies to a cooler part of the grate or slightly lower the temp.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the veggies.  
- Don’t let the internal temp exceed 145°F; it can dry out.  

**Serve ideas:**  
- Slice eggplant and portobello, then drizzle with **balsamic glaze** or **olive oil and lemon**.  
- Serve on a platter with **toasted bread**, **grilled flatbread**, or **pita** for scooping.  

***

## 5. Smoke the supporting veggies (onions, peppers, tomatoes, optional fruit)

- **Load time:** 30–45 minutes before guests arrive.  
- **Place on the offset:**  
  - Onions and peppers **cut‑side down** on the grate.  
  - Tomatoes **cut‑side down** on the grate.  
  - Pineapple or mango **slices** on the grate.  

**Smoke time and temp targets (325–375°F):**

| Veg / Fruit | Time (approx) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Onions | 20–30 minutes | Outer layers should be charred, interior soft but not falling apart. |
| Peppers | 20–30 minutes | Skin should be blistered and charred, flesh softened. |
| Tomatoes | 15–25 minutes | Skin should be blistered and charred, flesh soft. |
| Pineapple / mango | 15–25 minutes | Edges should be slightly caramelized and softened. |

**Do’s**  
- Check after the lower end of the time window; pull each item as it looks ready.  
- Pile them in a bowl, then cover with a plate or foil to steam and loosen skins. [best practice]  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t over‑smoke them into mush; they’ll turn into sauce instead of salsa.  
- Don’t continue smoking once they’re done; different veggies finish at different times.  

**Serve ideas:**  
- Use the smoked veggies to make a **smoked salsa** (see previous guide).  
- Serve on a platter with **toasted bread**, **grilled flatbread**, or **pita** for scooping.  

***

## 6. Grill the fresh veggies (zucchini, asparagus, squash)

- **Load time:** 15–20 minutes before guests arrive.  
- **Place on the offset:**  
  - Zucchini or summer squash **slices** on the grate.  
  - Asparagus **spears** on the grate.  

**Smoke time and temp targets (325–375°F):**

| Veg | Time (approx) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Zucchini / squash | 5–10 minutes | Should be slightly charred and tender. |
| Asparagus | 5–10 minutes | Should be bright green and slightly charred. |

**Do’s**  
- Use a **tongs** to move the veggies around.  
- Check them every 2–3 minutes after the first 3 minutes.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t let them cook too long; they should be tender but still crisp‑tender.  
- Don’t over‑season; let the smoke and oil speak for themselves.  

**Serve ideas:**  
- Drizzle with **olive oil**, **lemon juice**, and **fresh herbs** (parsley, basil, or cilantro).  
- Toss with a light vinaigrette for a warm salad.  

***

## 7. Put it all together (the spread)

| Component | How to arrange it |
| --- | --- |
| Smoked eggplant & portobello | On a large platter, garnished with **fresh herbs** and **citrus zest**. |
| Smoked onions, peppers, tomatoes, and optional fruit | On a separate platter or in a bowl as a **smoked salsa**. |
| Fresh grilled zucchini, asparagus, and squash | On a platter with a light vinaigrette. |
| Warm bread or pita | In a basket or on a plate. |

**Do’s**  
- Use a **thermometer** to confirm 325–375°F before loading any veggies.  
- Keep the veggies warm by covering them with a **clean cloth** or **foil**.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t serve the veggies cold; they should be warm and inviting.  
- Don’t skip the fresh herbs; they keep the flavors bright and fresh.  

***

## 8. Do’s / Don’ts for a full veggie spread on your offset

**Do’s**  
- Use a **sharp knife** for cutting.  
- Keep the smoke mild and the veggies blistered, not blackened.  
- Use a **thermometer** to confirm 325–375°F before loading them.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t over‑salt; the veggies are delicate.  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the veggies.  
- Don’t skip the rest; let the veggies cool slightly before serving.
