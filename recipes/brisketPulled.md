Here’s a **tight, step‑by‑step guide** for **3–5 lb pulled brisket** on your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S offset**, dialed for 250–275°F. This script prioritizes **tender, juicy pulled texture** with clean bark, and includes **Do’s / Don’ts** and time targets personalized for that size brisket.  

***

## 1. Choose and prep the brisket

- **Target:** 3–5 lb brisket flat or small packer, with some fat cap (1/8–1/4") and decent marbling.  
- **Trim:**  
  - Keep a thin fat cap; trim big ragged pieces and any papery skin.  
  - Don’t over‑trim; you want fat to help keep it juicy when you pull.  

**Do’s**  
- Trim and pat dry before seasoning.  
- Apply a **salt‑heavy rub** (e.g., 2:1 salt/pepper, garlic/onion powder, a pinch of paprika) 30–60 minutes before fire‑up. [best practice]  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t shave off all fat.  
- Don’t oversugar the rub if you’re smoking low‑and‑slow.  

***

## 2. Set up the Royal Gourmet smoker

- **Target temp:** 250–275°F for a 3–5 lb pulled brisket.
- **Fire:**
  - Charcoal basket in the firebox, building a **minion‑style fire** (2/3 unlit, 1/3 lit) with 1–2 small wood chunks (hickory, oak, pecan). [best practice]
- **Vents:**
  - Stack **mostly open**; firebox intake **1/4–1/3 open** to hold 250–275°F. [best practice]
- **Pan:**
  - Place a shallow water pan on the grate on the **stack side** to buffer temps. [best practice]

**Do’s**
- Double‑check that **door gaskets and seams are sealed**. [best practice]
- Use **baffle / tuning plates** so the heat travels smoothly from firebox to stack side. [best practice]

**Don’ts**
- Don’t open the lid during the warm‑up phase.
- Don’t crank the firebox vent wide open and the stack closed; that creates chaos.

## SM045 Setup

Set your SM045 controller to **225–250°F** and preheat fully before loading meat. Load **2 oz of hickory, oak, or pecan chunks** in the wood box at the start — this is your only smoke charge.

Place the brisket fat-side up on the middle rack and insert the meat probe into the thickest part. Close the door and leave it.

**No spritzing needed.** The SM045 holds moisture naturally; the wrap is still the right move to push through the stall, but do it with minimal door-open time.

When the brisket hits **170–180°F**, open quickly, wrap tightly in butcher paper with a small splash of beef broth inside the wrap, and return it to the cabinet. The door-open time should be under 2 minutes.

Budget **6–10 hours** for a 3–5 lb piece. Target **200–208°F probe-tender** for pulled brisket — deeper than sliced because you need the collagen fully broken down. Rest wrapped for 1–2 hours before pulling.

## 3. Place the brisket on the offset

- **Position:** Stack side of the chamber, not next to the firebox.  
- **Orientation:**  
  - Put the **thicker end toward the firebox side** if there’s a big difference in thickness.  
  - Fat‑side up or down is fine; fat‑side up gives a bit more forgiveness and better bark. [best practice]  

**Do’s**  
- Insert a **meat‑probe thermometer** into the thickest part, avoiding big fat pockets.  
- Insert a **grate‑level probe** near the meat to track smoker temp. [best practice]  
- Close the lid and **do not open it for 2 hours** to let the bark form. [best practice]  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t put the brisket on the hottest side of the grill.  
- Don’t move it around during the first part of the cook.  

***

## 4. Smoke naked to build bark (2–4 hours)

- **Goal:** Dark, tacky, rub‑locked bark; internal temp ~170–180°F.  
- **Spritz:**  
  - After 2 hours, open the lid **quickly** and spritz the surface with beef broth, water, or a 50/50 mix. Repeat every 30–45 minutes if the surface looks dry. [best practice]  

**Do’s**  
- Let the bark set before you think about wrapping; for a pulled brisket, you usually **wrap in butcher paper or foil around 170–180°F internal**. [best practice]  
- Probe at 160–170°F, then 180°F, checking tenderness.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t wrap too early; that softens the bark and makes it harder to pull cleanly.  
- Don’t keep the lid open for long photos or unnecessary checks.  

***

## 5. Time and temp for 3–5 lb pulled brisket at 250–275°F

**Goal:** 198–205°F internal, **probe‑tender** (slides in like softened butter, no resistance). [best practice]  

| Situation | Time |
| --- | --- |
| Naked 2–3 hours, then wrapped | 2–3 hours naked, 2–4 hours wrapped (total 4–7 hours) for a 3–5 lb piece at 250–275°F. |

**Approximate milestones:**

- After 2–3 hours: Check bark and temp; it should be dark and set at ~170–180°F.  
- Wrap in **butcher paper or foil** with a light splash of liquid (beef broth, apple juice, or water) on the inside. [best practice]  
- After wrapping, the brisket usually goes from 180°F to 200°F in **2–4 hours** at 250–275°F. [best practice]  

**Do’s**  
- Use **probe tenderness**, not just temp, as your cutoff. [best practice]  
- If you see it pushing past 205°F and getting mushy, pull it sooner.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t force it past 205°F; you’ll lose texture.  
- Don’t skip the wrap; it protects the brisket and speeds up the cook.  

***

## 6. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (3–5 lb pulled brisket)

Use this as a mental guide; adjust for your brisket’s behavior.

| Time since fire‑up | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| 0–30 min | Fire up the CC1830S; build a minion‑fire, stabilize at 250–275°F. [best practice] |
| 30–60 min | Load the brisket fat‑side up on the stack side, close the lid. [best practice] |
| 2 hours | Quick spritz; check bark. If it’s still light, keep it naked and spritz again in 30–45 minutes. [best practice] |
| 2–3 hours | Check internal temp; it should be 170–180°F. If it’s there, wrap tightly in butcher paper or foil and place it back on the stack side. [best practice] |
| 3–5.5 hours (if wrapped early) | Continue smoking, only opening the lid to check the probe at 180°F, 190°F, and 200°F. [best practice] |
| 4–7 hours (total) | Probe every 30–45 minutes once above 190°F. Aim for 198–205°F and probe‑tender. [best practice] |

**Target finish:** 198–205°F internal, **probe‑tender** (slides in like softened butter).  

***

## 7. Resting and pulling

**Do’s**  
- Pull when it’s **198–205°F and probe‑tender**, then keep it **wrapped in foil or butcher paper** and rest **at least 1.5–2 hours** on the counter. [best practice]  
- After 1.5–2 hours, unwrap carefully and let it sit 15–30 minutes to firm up slightly.  
- Pull the brisket into chunks with forks or wear gloves and pull/shred.  
- Toss the pulled brisket with a bit of the collected juices for moisture and flavor. [best practice]  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t pull it immediately off the smoker; it will be tough and dry.  
- Don’t over‑pull and shred into dust; keep some nice texture.  
- Don’t discard the juices; they hold great flavor.  

***

## 8. Do’s / Don’ts for pulled brisket on your offset

**Do’s**  
- Use a moderate rub and let it sit 30–60 minutes before lighting.  
- Keep the smoker sealed, use baffle/tuning plates, and manage temps via the firebox intake. [best practice]  
- Wrap around 170–180°F internal, using butcher paper or foil with a light splash of liquid. [best practice]  
- Use a **thermometer** and **probe tenderness** as your final decision‑maker. [best practice]  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t over‑smoke; keep wood chunks modest and stop active smoke once the bark is set.  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check on the brisket.  
- Don’t skip the rest; it’s critical for juicy, pullable meat.
