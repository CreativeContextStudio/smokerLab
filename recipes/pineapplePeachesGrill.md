**Step‑by‑step guide** for **pineapple or peach rings**, tuned for **thick fruit rings** over **direct medium heat**. Total time: **4–6 minutes active grilling** after preheat, caramelized edges, warm juicy centers—dessert or savory pairing.

***

## 1. Prep the fruit rings (10 minutes before grilling)

- **Choose the fruit:**  
  - **Fresh pineapple** (1 whole, cored) or **ripe peaches** (4–6 medium, freestone).  
- **Cut:**  
  - Pineapple: 3/4–1" thick rings (core out center).  
  - Peaches: Halved/pitted or 3/4" thick rings.  
- **Seasoning:**  
  - Neutral oil (avocado) + light sugar glaze (brown sugar or honey brush).  

**Do’s**  
- Pat fruit dry, brush lightly with oil on both sides.  
- Brush **very lightly** with 1:1 honey/brown sugar + water glaze.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t use canned fruit; too soft/watery.  
- Don’t overload sugar; burns fast.  

***

## 2. Set up the grill

<!-- equipment: charcoal-grill -->
- **Target temp:** Direct **medium (375–425°F)**.
- **Fire setup:**
  - **Charcoal pan in mid position.**
  - **Full bed** of charcoal across the grate (1 chimney load, even layer). [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)
- **Vents:**
  - **Mostly open** for steady airflow. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

**Do’s**
- **Preheat 10 minutes** with lid closed until lid thermometer reads ~400°F.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t preheat too long; fruit cooks fast.
<!-- /equipment -->

<!-- equipment: gas-grill -->
- **Target temp:** Direct **medium (375–425°F)**.
- **Weber Spirit II setup:**
  - **E‑310:** All three burners MEDIUM.
  - **E‑210:** Both burners MEDIUM.
  - Light with lid **open**, then close to preheat.
- **Preheat:** All burners MEDIUM, lid closed **10 min** until ~400°F.

**Do’s**
- Gas MEDIUM gives very consistent, controllable heat—great for caramelizing sugar without burning.
- Keep an eye out for flare-ups from sugar drips; lower a burner quickly if needed.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t use MEDIUM‑HIGH or HIGH; sugar glaze burns fast on gas.
<!-- /equipment -->  

***

## 3. Place the rings on the grill

- **Position:**  
  - **Direct over coals** across the hot grate.  
- **Orientation:**  
  - Lay rings flat, **not overlapping**.  

**Do’s**  
- Grill **2–3 minutes per side**.  
- Use **long tongs** to flip; look for caramelized edges.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t overcrowd; steaming dulls caramel.  
- Don’t press down; juice escapes.  

***

## 4. Grill time and temp targets (375–425°F direct)

**Goal:** Caramelized edges, warm/soft centers. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

| Fruit | Time per side | Visual cue |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Pineapple rings** | 2–3 min | Golden edges, grill marks, sugar bubbling. |
| **Peach halves/rings** | 2–3 min | Charred skin side, softened flesh. |

**Do’s**  
- Check after first flip; pull when caramelized.  
- No thermometer; visual + fork‑tender is enough.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t let sugar burn black; bitter.  

***

***

## 6. Rest and serve

**Do’s**  
- Rest off heat **1 minute** (minimal).  
- Serve with **vanilla ice cream, whipped cream, or savory** (cilantro‑lime crema for pork/ribs pairing).  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t over‑rest; fruit cools fast.  
- Don’t stack; juices run.  

***

## 7. Do’s / Don’ts for pineapple/peach rings on CC1830S main grill

**Do’s**  
- Use **full medium grate** and **lid thermometer** to confirm ~400°F.  
- Light oil + sugar glaze, grill **lid open**.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t skip preheat; soft fruit.  
- Don’t overload sugar; flare‑ups.  

**Scale notes:**  
- **4 rings:** Same timing.  
- **8+ rings:** Cook in batches.
