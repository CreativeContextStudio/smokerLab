From-scratch dry rub guide with 7 rubs: Texas brisket, Kansas City rib, Carolina pork, Alabama chicken, coffee “cowboy” rub, lemon-pepper chicken, and an all-purpose sweet heat.

General notes for all rubs:
- Makes ~1 cup unless noted; scale freely.
- Use **coarse kosher salt** and **coarse black pepper** when possible for better bark.
- Mix thoroughly and store in an airtight jar in a cool, dark place; most keep their punch 1-3 months if kept dry.

***

## 1. Texas brisket rub (simple, beef-forward)

Central Texas brisket rubs are famously minimal -- often just salt and pepper, sometimes with light support from garlic or paprika.

**Ingredients**

- 1/4 cup coarse kosher salt
- 1/4 cup coarse black pepper (16-mesh style if you can get it)
- 1 tbsp garlic powder (optional but common)
- 1 tbsp smoked or sweet paprika (optional)

**Steps**

1. Combine all ingredients in a bowl and mix very thoroughly so salt and pepper distribute evenly.
2. Store in an airtight jar. Shake before each use so heavier salt doesn’t settle.
3. For brisket, use about 1-1.5 tbsp per pound of meat. Apply an even, heavy coat on all sides 30-60 minutes before it hits the pit (or the night before for a stronger cure).

Use on: brisket, beef ribs, tri-tip, chuck roasts.

***

## 2. Kansas City rib rub (sweet, paprika-heavy)

KC rib rubs lean **brown-sugar-forward** with paprika, garlic, onion, and a little cayenne.

**Ingredients**

- 1/2 cup brown sugar
- 1/4 cup paprika
- 1 tbsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp kosher salt
- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tbsp garlic powder
- 1 tbsp onion powder
- 1 tsp cayenne pepper (reduce if you want it mild)

**Steps**

1. Break up any sugar clumps with your fingers or a fork.
2. Whisk all ingredients together in a bowl until the color is totally uniform.
3. Use generously on pork ribs (and chicken) at least 30-60 minutes before cooking; overnight in the fridge gives deeper flavor.
4. Store in a sealed jar; shake before using, as sugar can settle.

Use on: spare/St. Louis ribs, baby backs, chicken, pork shoulder.

## 3. Carolina pork rub (balanced, herb and spice mix)

Carolina pork rubs often combine paprika, sugar, salt, chili powder, cayenne, oregano, thyme, cumin, pepper, garlic, and onion powders.

**Ingredients**

- 1/4 cup paprika (regular or smoked)
- 1/4 cup brown sugar
- 2 tbsp kosher salt
- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tsp cayenne
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp onion powder

**Steps**

1. Combine all ingredients in a bowl and whisk until well blended; crush any herb clumps between your fingers.
2. Coat pork shoulder or pork chops evenly on all sides; let sit at least 15-30 minutes before cooking.
3. Pair with a Carolina vinegar or Carolina mustard sauce for classic pulled pork profiles.

Use on: pork shoulder/butt, country-style ribs, chops.

## 4. Alabama chicken rub (pairs with white sauce)

A simple, slightly sweet/savory blend that plays nicely with tangy white sauce. Uses brown sugar, black pepper, salt, smoked paprika, garlic, onion, mustard, and a little cayenne.

**Ingredients**

- 1/4 cup brown sugar
- 1 tbsp black pepper
- 2 tsp kosher salt
- 2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp ground mustard
- 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper

**Steps**

1. Mix all ingredients thoroughly in a bowl. Break up sugar lumps.
2. Pat chicken dry; sprinkle rub evenly and massage into skin and under skin where possible.
3. Rest 15-30 minutes so it draws a little moisture and adheres.
4. Smoke/roast chicken, then finish by dunking or drizzling with Alabama white sauce.

Use on: whole chickens, thighs, leg quarters, wings.

## 5. Coffee “cowboy” BBQ rub (deep, roasty, great on beef)

Fine ground coffee, brown sugar, salt, chili powder, paprika, black pepper, onion and garlic powder, cayenne, and sometimes cocoa.

**Ingredients**

- 2 tbsp finely ground dark-roast coffee
- 2 tbsp brown sugar
- 1 tbsp coarse kosher salt
- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 1/2 tbsp paprika
- 1/2 tbsp black pepper
- 1/2 tbsp onion powder
- 1/2 tbsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp cayenne (more if you like it spicy)
- 1/2 tsp unsweetened cocoa powder (optional but classic)

**Steps**

1. Stir all ingredients together until uniform; the coffee and cocoa should be evenly dispersed.
2. Use a lighter coat than sugar-heavy rubs -- coffee can get intense.
3. Let meat sit 20-30 minutes before cooking so rub hydrates slightly.
4. Best cooked indirect or at moderate heat to avoid scorching the sugar and coffee.

Use on: brisket flats, chuck roasts, beef ribs, steaks, pork loin.

## 6. Lemon-pepper BBQ chicken rub (bright and punchy)

Black pepper, dried lemon peel, salt, garlic, and sometimes citric acid for extra tang.

**Ingredients**

- 4 tbsp coarse black pepper
- 3 tbsp dried lemon peel granules or powder
- 2 tbsp kosher salt
- 1 tbsp granulated garlic
- 1/4 tsp citric acid (optional, to boost lemon tang)

**Steps**

1. Stir together pepper, lemon peel, salt, garlic, and citric acid (if using) until well mixed.
2. Taste a pinch: if you want more lemon, increase lemon peel or citric acid; if too sharp, add a bit more salt or garlic.
3. Coat chicken thoroughly, including under the skin where possible.
4. Cook around 250-300\u00B0F; this rub shines when paired with a butter or oil baste toward the end.

Use on: chicken leg quarters, wings, whole spatchcocked birds, even grilled fish with a lighter hand.

## 7. All-purpose sweet-heat BBQ rub (house blend)

An all-purpose blend in the spirit of competition-style chicken/pork rubs: brown sugar, salt, paprika, garlic, onion, mustard, and cayenne.

**Ingredients**

- 1/4 cup brown sugar
- 2 tbsp kosher salt
- 2 tbsp paprika (split between sweet and smoked if you like)
- 1 tbsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp garlic powder
- 1 tbsp onion powder
- 1 tsp ground mustard
- 1 tsp chili powder
- 1/2 tsp cayenne (adjust to taste)

**Steps**

1. Combine everything in a bowl; whisk until uniform.
2. Taste a pinch: if it leans too salty, bump sugar; if too sweet, add a bit more salt and chili powder.
3. Use as a go-to rub for anything when you don’t want to overthink it.
4. For ribs or chicken, apply 30-60 minutes before cooking; for big cuts, you can go overnight.

Use on: ribs, pork shoulder, chicken, turkey, even grilled veggies.
