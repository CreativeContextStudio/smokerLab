**Step‑by‑step guide** for **lamb chops or rack**, tuned for **1" thick lamb loin chops or frenched rack** using **two‑zone fire**. Total time: **8–12 minutes active grilling** after preheat, crusty exterior, pink juicy center.

***

## 1. Prep the lamb chops/rack (30–45 minutes before grilling)

- **Choose the lamb:**  
  - **8–12 loin chops** (1" thick, 4–6 oz each) or **1 small frenched rack** (8 ribs).  
- **Seasoning:**  
  - Kosher salt, coarse black pepper, rosemary (fresh chopped or dried), garlic powder.  

**Do’s**  
- Pat lamb dry with paper towels.  
- Rub: 1 tsp salt + 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp garlic + 1 tsp rosemary per pound; let sit room temp **30–45 minutes**.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t trim all fat; renders for flavor.  
- Don’t marinate long; salt draws moisture.  

***

## 2. Set up the grill

<!-- equipment: charcoal-grill -->
- **Target temp:** Two‑zone **high (450–550°F)** overall.
- **Fire setup (two‑zone):**
  - **Charcoal pan in high position.**
  - **Hot zone:** 1.5–2 chimney loads piled under **half** grate.
  - **Cool zone:** No charcoal under other half. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)
- **Vents:**
  - **Fully open** for max airflow. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

**Do’s**
- **Preheat 15–20 minutes** lid closed until lid thermometer ~500°F.
- Brush grates clean while hot.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t skip preheat; sticking kills crust.
<!-- /equipment -->

<!-- equipment: gas-grill -->
- **Target temp:** Direct **high (450–550°F)**.
- **Weber Spirit II setup:**
  - **E‑310:** Left + center burners HIGH. Right burner LOW (buffer/rest zone).
  - **E‑210:** Left burner HIGH. Right burner LOW.
  - Light with lid **open**, then close to preheat.
- **Preheat:** All burners HIGH, lid closed **10–15 minutes** until lid thermometer ~500°F.
- Brush grates; **oil the lamb, not the grates**.

**Do’s**
- Cast iron grates on the Spirit II hold heat well for a fast sear—this is a perfect gas‑grill cook.
- Keep a LOW burner zone ready to rest chops off direct heat.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t skip preheat; lamb chops are thin and need instant crust on contact.
<!-- /equipment -->  

***

## 3. Place the lamb on the grill

- **Position:**  
  - **Hot zone** (over coals) for sear.  
- **Orientation:**  
  - Chops flat; rack bone‑side down first.  

**Do’s**  
- Sear **2 minutes per side** chops or **2–3 min per side** rack.  
- Move to **cool zone** for 1–2 min finish if needed.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t overcrowd; steaming dulls crust.  
- Don’t press down; juices escape.  

***

## 4. Grill time and temp targets (450–550°F two‑zone)

**Goal:** Internal **130°F (medium‑rare)** or **135°F (medium)**. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

| Cut | Sear time per side | Internal pull temp |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Loin chops (1")** | 2 min | 125–130°F |
| **Frenched rack** | 2–3 min | 125–130°F |

**Do’s**  
- Check internal after first flip.  
- **Fast‑read thermometer** thickest part, away from bone.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t overshoot 140°F; toughens fast.  

***

**Target finish:** **125–130°F internal** before resting.  

***

## 6. Rest and serve

**Do’s**  
- Rest **5 minutes** tented loosely with foil (carryover hits 135°F).  
- Serve with **chimichurri, garlic aioli, or ranch**.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t cut early; juices redistribute.  

***

## 7. Do’s / Don’ts for lamb chops/rack on CC1830S main grill

**Do’s**  
- Use **two‑zone high fire** and **lid thermometer** ~500°F.  
- Pat dry, simple rosemary rub.  
- Sear **lid open** for crust.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t skip preheat; no crust.  
- Don’t overshoot 135°F; chewy.  

**Scale notes:**  
- **4–6 chops:** Same timing.  
- **Rack:** Add 1–2 min per side.  

Fancy, fast, impressive.
