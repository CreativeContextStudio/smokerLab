Here's a **tight, step‑by‑step guide** for **smoked tri‑tip** on the **Royal Gourmet CC1830S offset smoker**, focused on juicy, sliceable steak‑like bites with good smoke flavor and a beefy profile similar to brisket. This assumes a **2–3 lb tri‑tip** smoked at 250°F to medium-rare, then rested and sliced. Always use a **meat‑probe thermometer** to hit your target doneness.

Here's a **tight, step‑by‑step guide** for **reverse-sear tri‑tip** on the **main grill chamber** of your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S**, using banked coals for a low indirect phase then a high-heat sear for the crust. This assumes a **2–3 lb tri‑tip** cooked to medium-rare, rested in butter, and sliced. No offset firebox, no smoke wood — the charcoal itself provides a subtle char-kissed flavor. Always use a **meat‑probe thermometer**.
<!-- /equipment -->

***

## 1. Choose and prep the tri‑tip

- **Target:** 2–3 lb tri‑tip roast, ideally with some intermuscular fat and a fat cap.
- **Trim:**
  - Trim the fat cap down to about **1/4 inch** — you don't have 12–18 hours to render a thick cap like you would on a brisket.
  - Clean up any jagged edges or loose flaps for even cooking and a clean presentation.

**Do's**
- Pat dry with paper towels before seasoning.
- Apply **16‑mesh black pepper first, then seasoned salt** — season it like a thick‑cut steak. [best practice]
- Let it sit out for **15–20 minutes** until the surface looks wet and glossy and the salt activates. [best practice]

**Don'ts**
- Don't leave more than 1/4 inch of fat cap; there's not enough cook time to render it down.
- Don't skip the sweat time; a glossy surface means the salt has activated and the rub will stick.

***

<!-- equipment: offset-smoker -->
## 2. Set up the Royal Gourmet CC1830S offset smoker

- **Target temp:** 250°F in the cook chamber.
- **Fire:**
  - Charcoal basket in the firebox, building a **minion‑style fire** (2/3 unlit, 1/3 lit) with 1–2 small wood chunks (oak, hickory, or pecan). [best practice]
- **Vents:**
  - Stack **three‑quarters open**; firebox intake **1/4–1/3 open** to hold 250°F. [best practice]
- **Pan:**
  - Place a shallow water pan on the grate on the **stack side** to buffer temps. [best practice]

**Do's**
- Double‑check that **door gaskets and seams are sealed**. [best practice]
- Use **baffle / tuning plates** so heat travels smoothly from firebox to stack side. [best practice]

**Don'ts**
- Don't open the lid during the warm‑up phase.
- Don't crank the firebox vent wide open and the stack closed.

***

## 3. Place the tri‑tip on the offset

- **Position:** Closer to the stack side, away from residual firebox heat. [best practice]
- **Orientation:**
  - Fat‑side up to let the fat baste down through the meat during the cook.
  - Thicker end toward the firebox side if there's a significant thickness difference.

**Do's**
- Insert a **meat‑probe thermometer** into the thickest part of the tri‑tip.
- Insert a **grate‑level probe** near the meat to track smoker temp. [best practice]
- Close the lid and **do not open it for 1–1.5 hours** to let the smoke build and bark form. [best practice]

**Don'ts**
- Don't put the tri‑tip directly next to the firebox; it can overcook the thin end.
- Don't move it around during the cook.

***

## 4. Smoke to target temp (1.5–2.5 hours)

- **Goal:** **130–135°F internal** — medium-rare to medium, with good smoke color and bark.
- **Spritz:**
  - After 1 hour, open the lid **quickly** and check color. Spritz with beef broth or water if the surface looks dry. [best practice]

**Do's**
- Treat this like a steak cook — you're going for **doneness**, not breaking down connective tissue. [best practice]
- Start probing after 1 hour and check every 15–20 minutes once it passes 115°F.
- Pull at **130–135°F** for medium‑rare to medium; it will carry over 5–10°F during the rest. [best practice]

**Don'ts**
- Don't cook this to 180–200°F like a brisket; it will be tough and overcooked.
- Don't wrap the tri‑tip; it stays naked the entire cook for maximum bark and smoke. [best practice]

***

## 5. Time and temp for 2–3 lb tri‑tip at 250°F (offset smoker)

**Goal:** 130–135°F internal, sliceable with a juicy, steak‑like bite. [best practice]

| Target doneness | Pull temp | Time (approx) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Medium-rare | 130°F | 1.5–2 hours |
| Medium | 135°F | 2–2.5 hours |

**Approximate milestones:**
- After 1 hour: Check temp and color; should be 100–115°F with bark forming.
- After 1.5 hours: Should be 120–130°F; start probing every 15 minutes.
- After 2–2.5 hours: Should hit 130–135°F; pull immediately.

**Do's**
- Use **internal temp** as your cutoff — this is a steak, not a braise. [best practice]
- Pull a few degrees early; carryover cooking will bring it up during the rest.

**Don'ts**
- Don't force it past 140°F; you'll lose the juicy, steak‑like texture.
- Don't wrap it; naked the whole way for tri‑tip.

***

## 6. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (offset smoker, 2–3 lb tri‑tip at 250°F)

| Time since fire‑up | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| 0–30 min | Fire up the CC1830S; build a minion‑fire, stabilize at 250°F. [best practice] |
| 30–60 min | Load the tri‑tip fat‑side up on the stack side, close the lid. [best practice] |
| 1 hour | Quick check; spritz if dry. Check color — bark should be starting to form. |
| 1–1.5 hours | Check internal temp; should be 100–120°F. Keep smoking. |
| 1.5–2 hours | Probe every 15 minutes once above 115°F. Pull at 130–135°F. [best practice] |
| 2–2.5 hours | If it hasn't hit temp, keep going; thicker tri-tips take longer. |

**Target finish:** 130–135°F internal, juicy with good smoke color.

## 2. SM045 Setup

Set your SM045 to **225–250°F** and preheat fully. Load **1 oz of oak or hickory chunks** in the wood box. Tri-tip is a short cook — one light wood charge is all you need.

Place the tri-tip fat-side up on the middle rack. Insert the meat probe into the thickest part. Close the door.

This is a **steak cook**, not a brisket cook. You are targeting doneness, not breakdown. Pull at **130–135°F** for medium-rare — the insulated SM045 cabinet will carry over 5–10°F after the door opens, so pulling at 130°F is right.

Budget **2–3 hours** at 225–250°F for a 2–3 lb tri-tip. Start probing at 90 minutes. Rest in a pad of butter wrapped in foil for 40–60 minutes, then slice against the grain (tri-tip has two grain directions — rotate halfway through slicing).

## 2. Set up the Royal Gourmet CC1830S main grill chamber

The charcoal-grill reverse sear on tri-tip uses banked coals for a low indirect phase, then a sear blast to finish.

- **Indirect phase target:** **275–325°F** (lid thermometer reading ~325°F).
- **Charcoal pan:** **Low position** for the indirect phase.
- **Fire layout (banked):**
  - Light **3/4 chimney** (~3–4 lb briquettes).
  - Pour all coals **tightly banked to one end only** of the charcoal pan — not half the pan, but pushed firmly to one end for maximum cool zone on the other side.
- **Vents:** **1/2 open** during indirect phase. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

**Do's**
- Preheat 20–25 minutes with lid closed; confirm the far end is 275–325°F (lid thermometer ~325°F).
- Insert the meat probe before closing the lid — you don't want to open it again for 45+ minutes.

**Don'ts**
- Don't use the high pan position for the indirect phase; it runs too hot.
- Don't spread coals across half the pan; a tight bank creates a longer, more effective cool zone.

***

## 3. Cook the tri‑tip indirect (low phase)

- **Position:** Tri-tip on the **far end of the grate** from the coal bank, fat-side up.
- **Thicker end:** Angle it so the thicker part points toward the coal bank if there's a big thickness difference.

**Do's**
- Cook **lid closed** until tri-tip hits **115–120°F internal** (medium-rare target before sear).
- Check temp at 45 minutes, then every 15 minutes after.
- Flip once at the halfway point of the indirect cook.

**Don'ts**
- Don't open the lid more than once per 30 minutes — every open costs you 25–50°F.
- Don't let the indirect zone run above 350°F; close vents down if it's running hot.

***

## 4. Transition to sear (ramping the fire)

When the tri-tip hits 115–120°F:

1. **Pull the tri-tip** and tent with foil loosely (10-minute rest while the fire ramps).
2. **Raise charcoal pan to high position** (use heat-resistant gloves).
3. **Optional but recommended:** Add a fresh **1/2 chimney** of pre-lit coals on top of the existing bank.
4. **Open vents fully open**.
5. **Wait 5–8 minutes** for the hot zone to reach 500°F+ before searing.

***

## 5. Sear the tri‑tip

- **Move tri-tip to the hot zone** (directly over the coal bank).
- **Sear 2–3 minutes per side** until a deep brown crust forms across the surface.
- **Pull at 130°F** (medium-rare) or **135°F** (medium).

**Do's**
- Sear **lid open** so you can see exactly what's happening and control char.
- Use **long tongs** — fat drips can cause flare-ups at this temp.
- Rotate the tri-tip 90° once during each side for even crust formation.

**Don'ts**
- Don't walk away — at 500°F+, 30 seconds is the difference between crust and char.
- Don't skip the transition rest; the sear needs the fire ready before the meat hits the grate.

***

## 6. Time and temp (charcoal grill reverse sear)

| Phase | Time (approx) | Internal temp target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Indirect low heat** | 1–1.5 hours at 275–325°F | 115–120°F |
| **Transition / rest** | 10 min | Holding |
| **Sear** | 2–3 min/side at 500°F+ | Pull at 130–135°F |

***

## 7. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (charcoal grill, 2–3 lb tri-tip)

| Phase | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| **Grill setup** | 3/4 chimney banked to one end, pan low, vents 1/2 open. Preheat 20 min. |
| **0 min** | Tri-tip on far end, fat-side up. Insert meat probe. Lid closed. |
| **45 min** | First temp check; should be 90–110°F. Keep going. |
| **60–90 min** | Probe every 15 min once above 110°F. Pull at 115–120°F. |
| **At 115°F: Transition** | Pull, tent foil. Raise pan to high. Add fresh coals (optional). Vents fully open. |
| **Sear setup (5–8 min)** | Let fire ramp to 500°F+ over hot zone. |
| **Sear (4–6 min total)** | Tri-tip over bank, 2–3 min/side, lid open. Pull at 130–135°F. |

**Target finish:** Deep crust, even pink throughout, juicy.

***

## 7. Resting and slicing

**Do's**
- Pull when it hits **130–135°F**, immediately set it in a pad of **butter**, wrap in **foil or butcher paper**, and rest for **40 minutes to 1 hour** on the counter. [best practice]
- After resting, unwrap and let it sit for **10–15 minutes** to firm up slightly before slicing.
- Identify the grain direction — tri‑tip has two different grain directions that meet in the middle. Slice **against the grain** in **1/4–1/2 inch thick slices**. [best practice]
- Hit the slices with a **flaky finishing salt** as you plate them for an extra pop. [best practice]

**Don'ts**
- Don't slice immediately; the rest is critical for juicy meat.
- Don't slice too thin; you'll lose the juicy, steak‑like bite.
- Don't forget that tri‑tip has **two grain directions** — rotate halfway through slicing.
- Don't discard the juices from the wrap; drizzle them over the sliced meat.

***

## 8. Do's / Don'ts summary

**Do's (offset smoker)**
- Season like a thick steak — 16‑mesh black pepper first, then seasoned salt. [best practice]

- Keep it naked the entire cook — no wrapping on tri‑tip. [best practice]
- Use a thermometer as your final decision‑maker.
- Rest in butter and foil for at least 40 minutes before slicing. [best practice]

**Don'ts (offset smoker)**
- Don't cook past 140°F; this is a steak, not a braise.
- Don't open the lid constantly.
- Don't skip the rest.
- Don't forget the two grain directions when slicing.

**Do's (charcoal grill)**
- Season like a thick steak — 16-mesh pepper first, then seasoned salt.
- Bank coals tightly to one end, pan on low for indirect phase.
- Ramp the fire hard before the sear — pan to high, vents fully open, fresh coals if needed.
- Sear lid open so you can see and control the crust.
- Rest in butter, wrapped in foil, 40+ minutes before slicing.

**Don'ts (charcoal grill)**
- Don't use the high pan position for the indirect phase.
- Don't skip the transition rest — the sear needs the fire ready.
- Don't cook past 140°F; this is a steak cut.
- Don't forget the two grain directions when slicing.
