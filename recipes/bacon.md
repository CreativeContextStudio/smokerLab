# Smoked Candy-Sriracha Bacon

Step-by-step guide for making smoked bacon with a sweet "candy" crust and spicy sriracha crunch on a Royal Gourmet offset smoker. Start with a pork belly slab, brine it, coat in a sugar-spice-sriracha glaze, then smoke and finish with a charcoal sear.

---

## 1. Choose the pork belly

- **Size:** 2–3 lb slab of pork belly, skinless, with even thickness if possible.
- **Fat-to-meat ratio:** Aim for about 50/50; fat renders and holds the glaze.

**Do's**
- Trim any ragged edges; keep the fat layer intact.
- Rinse and pat dry before brining.

**Don'ts**
- Don't use a super-lean cut — you need fat to render and hold the glaze.

---

## 2. Brine / cure (12–24 hours)

Use a **light cure** to get bacon flavor without excessive saltiness.

### Brine ingredients (for ~2–3 lb belly)

| Ingredient | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Water | 1 quart |
| Kosher salt | 50 g |
| Brown or white sugar | 30 g |
| Pink curing salt (optional) | 1 tsp (or substitute 1–2 tsp extra salt) |
| Black pepper, coarsely ground | 1 tsp |
| Garlic, smashed (optional) | 2–3 cloves |
| Smoked paprika (optional) | 1 tsp |
| Crushed red pepper (optional) | 1 tsp |

**Do's**
- Mix until salt and sugar dissolve, cool completely, then submerge the belly and refrigerate 12–24 hours.
- Flip the belly once after 6–8 hours.

**Don'ts**
- Don't brine longer than 24 hours — it will get too salty.
- Don't skip the sugar — it's key for the candy-crust effect.

---

## 3. Rinse, dry, and prep

1. Rinse the belly under cold water to remove excess salt.
2. Pat **very dry** with paper towels.
3. Set on a wire rack over a tray and leave uncovered in the fridge for **2–4 hours** to form a pellicle — the sticky-tacky surface that holds the glaze.

**Do's**
- Use a wire rack so air circulates all around the belly.
- If short on time, towel-dry thoroughly and proceed.

**Don'ts**
- Don't leave the belly at room temp for an extended period while uncovered.

---

## 4. Make the candy-sriracha glaze

A **thick, sticky, partially cooked sauce** that caramelizes on the bacon surface.

### Glaze ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Brown sugar, packed | 1/2 cup |
| Sriracha (or chili-garlic sauce) | 1/3 cup |
| Soy sauce | 2–3 tbsp |
| Honey or maple syrup | 1–2 tbsp (more = sweeter) |
| Smoked paprika | 1 tsp |
| Garlic powder | 1/2–1 tsp |
| Black pepper | 1/2 tsp |
| Cayenne (optional) | pinch |

**Do's**
- Whisk everything in a saucepan and **simmer 5–7 minutes** until thickened and syrupy.
- Cool until still spreadable but not hot before coating the belly.

**Don'ts**
- Don't leave it too runny — you want it to stick and bubble, not drip off.
- Don't skip the sugar — it creates the "candy" crunch.

---

## 5. Coat the belly

1. Lay the belly on a tray or cutting board.
2. **Optional:** Score the fat side in a diamond or crosshatch pattern about 1/4–1/2 inch deep to help fat render and glaze penetrate.
3. Brush a **thick, even layer** of glaze on the fat side and edges (skip the bottom — it cooks against the grate).
4. Sprinkle a **light extra layer of brown sugar** over the wet glaze for an even crunchier crust when it caramelizes.
5. Let the coated belly rest **15–20 minutes** so the glaze sets slightly before it hits the smoker.

**Do's**
- Place the bacon **fat-side up** on the grate.

**Don'ts**
- Don't dump glaze in a puddle under the belly — it will flare and burn.
- Don't over-glaze — one thick, even coat plus optional sugar sprinkle is enough.

---

## 6. Set up the offset smoker

- **Target temp:** 225–250°F
- **Fuel:** Charcoal basket with a minion-style fire, plus mild wood chunks — apple, hickory, or maple.
- **Placement:** Stack side of the grate, not the firebox side. Use a baffle or tuning plates so the fat isn't over the fiercest heat.
- **Vents:** Stack mostly open; control temp with the firebox intake (1/4–1/3 open at 225–250°F).
- **Drip pan:** Shallow pan under the belly to catch drips.

**Do's**
- Use a grate probe to monitor smoker temp continuously.

**Don'ts**
- Don't crank the heat hoping for faster crispiness — you'll burn the sugar and render fat unevenly.
- Don't over-smoke; keep wood chunks modest.

## SM045 Setup

Set your SM045 to **200–225°F** — slightly lower than the offset version. The sealed cabinet traps heat around the glazed belly, and the sugar in the glaze can scorch at higher temps if it can't dissipate.

Load **1–2 oz of apple or maple chunks** in the wood box. These are mild, sweet woods that complement the candy glaze without competing with it. Load at the start; don't add more mid-cook.

Place the glazed belly **fat-side up** on the middle rack with a drip pan below to catch the rendering fat and glaze drips. Insert the meat probe.

Smoke **2.5–4 hours** until the glaze is deep amber to dark orange and the internal temp is 160–170°F. Press lightly through a gloved hand — it should feel firm, not soft.

**No charcoal sear is possible on the SM045.** Instead, slice the belly into 1/4–1/3 inch strips after the smoke, then finish in one of these ways:
- **Broiler:** 1–2 minutes per side under a high broiler, watching closely.
- **Cast iron:** 1–2 minutes per side in a screaming hot dry pan.

Either method sets the candy crust and adds the snappy texture the offset's charcoal sear provides. Cool on a wire rack before serving.

## 7. Smoke — time and checkpoints

**Total smoking time:** 2–3.5 hours at 225–250°F depending on thickness.

| Time | What to check |
| --- | --- |
| ~1–1.5 hours | Peek briefly — glaze should be bubbling and starting to caramelize. |
| ~2–2.5 hours | Look for deep orange-reddish, sticky, slightly crispy glaze. |
| Done | Bacon is brown to dark amber, firm but not rock-hard; internal ~160–170°F. |

**Do's**
- Press the bacon lightly with a heat-resistant glove — it should feel firm but not soft.
- If the glaze looks pale at 1 hour, keep going.
- If it starts looking like a brittle, blackened crust, move it to a cooler part of the grate or lower the temp slightly.

**Don'ts**
- Don't open the lid more than once before the 1-hour mark.
- Don't let the glaze burn to a black, acrid crust.

---

## 8. Charcoal sear (finishing step)

After the smoke, a quick charcoal sear locks in a snappy, caramelized shell.

### Step 1 — Finish the smoke and rest

- Remove the belly when the glaze is **deep, shiny, and candy-like** and the internal temp is around 160–170°F.
- Rest on a wire rack at room temp for **10–20 minutes** so the surface firms up while the meat stays warm.

### Step 2 — Build a hot charcoal zone

- Add a small pile of very hot charcoal (from your existing fire or a fresh chimney) to one area of the grill grate, directly under where you'll place the bacon.
- Target **450–550°F** over that zone.
- Open the stack and firebox intake wide to generate intense heat — this is effectively a grill-top broiler.

### Step 3 — Sear the bacon

- Slice the belly **1/4–1/3 inch thick** across the grain for bacon-style strips.
- Place strips directly over the hot charcoal zone, fat-side down for maximum crackle.
- Sear **1–2 minutes per side**, watching closely — the glaze will bubble and edges will go dark and crunchy.
- Don't walk away; it can burn quickly.

### Step 4 — Cool and hold

- Remove from heat and cool on a wire rack to set the crust.
- Can hold at room temperature for up to **1–2 hours** before serving — the charcoal sear keeps the crispy texture longer than a pan sear.

**Do's**
- Keep the initial smoke-only slices slightly thicker so the sear doesn't dry them out.
- Monitor the glaze during the sear closely.
- Serve on a rimmed tray with strips grouped so the bottom doesn't steam.

**Don'ts**
- Don't skip the hot sear — for a room-temperature bite, crisp is everything.
- Don't wrap the bacon tightly while it's hot — it will steam and soften the crust.

---

## Summary timeline

| Step | Duration |
| --- | --- |
| Brine in fridge | 12–24 hours |
| Rinse, dry, pellicle | 2–4 hours |
| Glaze prep | ~15 min |
| Coat and rest | 15–20 min |
| Smoke at 225–250°F | 2–3.5 hours |
| Rest on rack | 10–20 min |
| Charcoal sear | 1–2 min per side |
| Cool and serve | within 2 hours |

---

## Serving

- Serve with a cooling side — coleslaw, cucumber salad, or pickles — to balance the heat and sugar.
- Reserve drippings from the drip pan to brush over strips before or after the sear for extra sticky, spicy-sweet crunch.
