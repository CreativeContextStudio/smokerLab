**Step‑by‑step guide** for **shrimp or scallop skewers**, tuned for **large shrimp (16/20 count) or sea scallops** over **direct high heat**. Total time: **4–6 minutes active grilling** after preheat, sweet char, juicy inside.

***

## 1. Prep the skewers (15–30 minutes before grilling)

- **Choose the seafood:**  
  - **Large shrimp** (16/20 count, peeled/deveined, tails on) or **sea scallops** (U10–U12 size).  
  - About 1–1.5 lb for 4 servings (12–16 shrimp or 12–16 scallops).  
- **Seasoning:**  
  - Neutral oil (avocado/grapeseed), salt, pepper, garlic powder, lemon zest.  

**Do’s**  
- Pat seafood dry with paper towels.  
- Toss in 2 tbsp oil + 1 tsp salt + 1/2 tsp pepper + 1/2 tsp garlic powder; let sit 15–30 min.  
- Thread onto **metal skewers** (2–3 shrimp/scallops per skewer).  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t use olive oil; smokes too fast.  
- Don’t overcrowd skewers; needs space to char.  

***

## 2. Set up the grill

<!-- equipment: charcoal-grill -->
- **Target temp:** Direct **high (450–550°F)**.
- **Fire setup:**
  - **Charcoal pan in high position.**
  - **Full bed** of charcoal across the grate (1.5–2 chimney loads, even layer). [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)
- **Vents:**
  - **Fully open** for max airflow and clean heat. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

**Do’s**
- **Preheat 15 minutes** with lid closed until lid thermometer reads ~500°F.
- Brush grates clean while hot.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t preheat too long; seafood cooks fast.
- Don’t use two‑zone; full hot grate for quick char.
<!-- /equipment -->

<!-- equipment: gas-grill -->
- **Target temp:** Direct **high (450–550°F)**.
- **Weber Spirit II setup:**
  - **E‑310:** All three burners HIGH.
  - **E‑210:** Both burners HIGH.
  - Light with lid **open**, then close to preheat.
- **Preheat:** All burners HIGH, lid closed **10–15 min** until ~500°F.
- **Oil the seafood**, not the grates.

**Do’s**
- Full‑grate HIGH is exactly right for shrimp/scallops—consistent heat, no flare‑up risk from charcoal ash.
- Metal skewers get hot on gas; use tongs, not bare hands, to handle.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t use wood skewers; they can ignite over gas flames.
<!-- /equipment -->  

***

## 3. Place the skewers on the grill

- **Position:**  
  - **Direct over coals** across the hot grate.  
- **Orientation:**  
  - Lay skewers flat, **not overlapping**.  

**Do’s**  
- Grill **2 minutes per side** (shrimp) or **2–2.5 min per side** (scallops).  
- Turn with **long tongs**; look for opaque meat and char marks.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t overcrowd; steaming kills crust.  
- Don’t flip too early; let char develop.  

***

## 4. Grill time and temp targets (450–550°F direct)

**Goal:** Opaque throughout, slight char, internal **140°F** max (carryover hits 145°F). [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

| Seafood | Time per side | Visual cue |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Large shrimp** | 2 min | Opaque pink, slight curl, char spots. |
| **Sea scallops** | 2–2.5 min | Opaque white, firm but springy, golden crust. |

**Do’s**  
- Check after first flip; pull if opaque and charred.  
- No thermometer needed; visual + feel is enough.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t overshoot; rubbery seafood is sad.  
- Don’t sauce on grill; burns easily.  

***

**Target finish:** Opaque, charred crust, slight spring.  

***

## 6. Rest and serve

**Do’s**  
- Rest skewers **1–2 minutes** off heat.  
- Serve with **cilantro‑lime crema, chimichurri, or garlic aioli** + lemon wedges.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t over‑rest; seafood likes warm.  
- Don’t sauce before serving; keeps char crisp.  

***

## 7. Do’s / Don’ts for shrimp/scallop skewers on CC1830S main grill

**Do’s**  
- Use **full hot grate** and **lid thermometer** to confirm ~500°F.  
- Pat dry, oil seafood (not grates).  
- Grill **lid open** for char control.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t skip preheat or dry seafood; steaming kills crust.  
- Don’t overcrowd; needs space to char.  

**Scale notes:**  
- **8–12 pieces:** Same timing.  
- **16+ pieces:** Cook in 2 batches, 2 min apart.  

Sweet char, juicy bite, party starter.
