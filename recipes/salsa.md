Here's a **step‑by‑step guide** for **smoking vegetables to make a smoked salsa** on the **Royal Gourmet CC1830S offset smoker**, with clear **do's / don'ts**, **smoking times**, and how to finish it into a fresh, bright salsa. This works for a classic **tomato‑based salsa** with **bell peppers, onions, garlic, and optional fruit (like pineapple or mango)**.
<!-- /equipment -->

<!-- equipment: charcoal-grill -->
Here's a **step‑by‑step guide** for **charcoal-grilled salsa** on the **main grill chamber** of your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S**. Charcoal grilling is actually the best method for salsa — the higher direct heat gives you more char, more caramelization, and a brighter smokiness than the offset. No wood chunks needed; the charcoal and the char do the work.
<!-- /equipment -->

***

## 1. Choose and prep the vegetables

### Core veggies (for about 4–6 cups finished salsa)

- **Tomatoes:** 2–3 lbs (about 6–8 medium); Roma or plum tomatoes work best because they're meaty.
- **Peppers:** 2–3 bell peppers (any color), plus 1–2 jalapeños or serranos for heat (optional).
- **Onion:** 1 medium white or yellow onion.
- **Garlic:** 4–6 cloves, unpeeled.
- **Optional fruit:** 1–2 cups diced pineapple or mango for sweetness.

**Do's**
- Rinse veggies under cold water and pat dry.
- Cut tomatoes and peppers in half; leave whole for easier handling on the grill.
- Cut onion into 1–1.5" wedges, skin on for protection.
- Leave garlic cloves whole in the skin.

**Don'ts**
- Don't over-cut or dice before cooking; you want larger pieces that won't fall through the grate.
- Don't skip the drying step; wet veggies steam instead of char.

***

## 2. Season lightly (optional)

- **Simple:**
  - A light sprinkle of salt on the tomato and pepper cut-sides.
  - Optional: 1–2 tbsp olive oil on tomatoes/peppers if you want a tiny bit of extra browning.

**Do's**
- Keep seasoning minimal; the char and raw salsa-making will bring most of the flavor.

**Don'ts**
- Don't add sugar, thick rubs, or heavy spices before cooking; they can burn or overpower the salsa.

***

## 3. Set up the Royal Gourmet CC1830S offset smoker

- **Target temp:** 325–375°F (hot enough to blister skins but not mush the insides).
- **Fire:**
  - Charcoal basket in the firebox, building a **hot charcoal bed** with 1–2 mild wood chunks (apple, maple, or oak). [best practice]
- **Vents:**
  - Stack **fully open**; firebox intake **1/2–3/4 open** to hold 325–375°F. [best practice]
- **Pan:**
  - Place a shallow water pan under the grate opposite the heat source to catch drips. [best practice]

**Do's**
- Let the smoker come to temp before loading the veggies.
- Use a **grate-level thermometer** to confirm you're in the 325–375°F band.

**Don'ts**
- Don't rush the warm-up; you want clean, even heat.

***

## 4. Place the vegetables on the offset

- **Position:** Stack side, not directly over the hottest coals.
- **Arrangement:**
  - Tomatoes and peppers: Cut-side down on the grate.
  - Onion wedges: On the grate, cut-side down.
  - Garlic: Whole cloves on the grate.
  - Fruit (pineapple/mango): Cut into thick slices and lay on the grate.

**Do's**
- Use **tongs or a heat-resistant glove**.
- Give them space so they smoke evenly and don't steam each other. [best practice]

**Don'ts**
- Don't crowd the grate.
- Don't flip constantly; let them blister and char.

***

## 5. Smoke time targets (offset smoker, 325–375°F)

Aim for **blistered skins, soft but not mushy interiors**. Exact times vary with temp and thickness.

| Veg / Fruit | Approx smoke time (325–375°F) | Visual cue |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tomatoes | 15–25 minutes | Skin blistered and charred, flesh soft. |
| Bell peppers | 20–30 minutes | Skin blistered and charred, flesh softened. |
| Jalapeños/serranos | 15–20 minutes | Skin blistered and charred, peppers softened. |
| Onion wedges | 20–30 minutes | Outer layers charred, interior soft but not falling apart. |
| Garlic cloves | 10–15 minutes | Skin charred, cloves soft and buttery. |
| Pineapple / mango | 15–25 minutes | Edges slightly caramelized and softened. |

**Do's**
- Check after the lower end of the time window; pull each item as it looks ready.
- Pile in a covered bowl to steam and loosen skins. [best practice]

**Don'ts**
- Don't over-smoke them into mush; they'll turn into sauce instead of salsa.
- Don't continue smoking once they're done; different veggies finish at different times.

## 3. SM045 Setup

Set your SM045 to **275–300°F** — its maximum practical range. The lower temp versus an offset means skins won't char as dramatically, but the veggies will still soften beautifully with good smoke flavor.

Load **1 oz of apple or oak chunks** in the wood box. Smoke is more concentrated in the closed SM045 cabinet; 1 oz is plenty for salsa vegetables and will give a clean, mild smokiness without overpowering the finished salsa.

Arrange vegetables on the middle rack: tomatoes and peppers cut-side up (to collect juices rather than lose them to the drip pan), onion wedges, and whole garlic cloves. Give them space so air circulates.

Check at 20 minutes. Pull each item as it softens and takes on color — tomatoes usually first (20–30 min), then peppers and onion (25–35 min), garlic last. The skins may not blister as aggressively as on a hot offset; look for flesh softness as the primary doneness cue. Rest in a covered bowl to steam and loosen skins, then finish into salsa per the main recipe.

## 3. Set up the Royal Gourmet CC1830S main grill chamber

Charcoal grill salsa uses direct heat to char and blister — more aggressive than the offset, and that's a good thing.

- **Target temp:** Full-bed direct heat, **400–450°F** at the grate.
- **Fire setup (full-bed):**
  - **Charcoal pan in mid position.**
  - Light **3/4 chimney** (~3–4 lb briquettes).
  - Pour coals **evenly across the entire pan** — this is a full-bed cook, not two-zone.
- **Vents:** **3/4 open** during preheat; close to 1/2 open once at temp. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

**Do's**
- **Preheat 15 minutes** with lid closed until lid thermometer reads ~425°F.
- If you have a grill basket, use it for garlic and small items; it keeps them from falling through the grate.

**Don'ts**
- Don't go to the high pan position; you want blistering, not scorching.
- Don't skip preheat — a cold grate will stick and tear the veggie skins.

***

## 4. Place the vegetables on the charcoal grill

- **Arrangement:**
  - **Tomatoes:** Cut-side down directly on the grate.
  - **Bell peppers:** Cut-side down or skin-side down (whole halves blister better skin-side down).
  - **Jalapeños/serranos:** Whole, directly on grate, rotate every 3–4 minutes.
  - **Onion wedges:** Cut-side down on the grate.
  - **Garlic cloves:** Wrap in a loose foil packet or use a grill basket; they burn fast on direct heat.
  - **Pineapple/mango:** Thick slices, cut-side down for caramelization.

**Do's**
- Lid **mostly closed** between checks; open briefly to flip or check.
- Work in batches if needed — don't crowd the grate.

**Don'ts**
- Don't flip constantly; you need sustained contact for good char.
- Don't use foil under the tomatoes; you want direct grate contact for char marks.

***

## 5. Grill time targets (charcoal grill, 400–450°F direct)

| Veg / Fruit | Approx grill time | Visual cue |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Tomatoes** | 12–18 min | Skin blistered, flesh soft, juices starting to pool. |
| **Bell peppers** | 15–20 min | Skin charred and starting to collapse. |
| **Jalapeños/serranos** | 10–15 min | Skin blistered all around; rotate. |
| **Onion wedges** | 12–18 min | Outer layers charred, inner layers soft. |
| **Garlic (foil pack)** | 12–15 min | Cloves turn golden and squeeze soft. |
| **Pineapple/mango** | 10–15 min | Cut-side caramelized, edges starting to brown. |

**Do's**
- Pull each item as it's done — they don't all finish at the same time.
- Put done items in a covered bowl or foil tent to steam and loosen skins.

**Don'ts**
- Don't let any item go black all over — some char is flavor, total blackening is bitter.
- Don't rush garlic; it goes from raw to burnt fast if unwrapped on direct heat.

***

## 6. Cool, peel, and prep for salsa

| Step | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Pull each veg/fruit off once it's blistered and soft, and let it cool in a covered bowl. [best practice] |
| 2 | Peel off the skins from tomatoes, peppers, and onions once they cool enough to handle. |
| 3 | Remove stems and seeds from peppers if you want less heat. |
| 4 | Squeeze the garlic cloves out of their skins. |
| 5 | Core and dice pineapple or mango if you used it. |

**Do's**
- Leave a little char on the peppers and tomatoes for flavor.
- Taste jalapeño before seeding; if you want more heat, leave in some seeds.

**Don'ts**
- Don't throw away the juices that collect in the bowl; add them to the salsa.
- Don't wash off the char completely; that's flavor.

***

## 7. Make the salsa

### Basic fresh salsa template (adjust heat/sweetness)

- **Charred/smoked ingredients:**
  - Peeled, chopped charred tomatoes.
  - Peeled, seeded or unseeded charred peppers.
  - Peeled, chopped charred onion.
  - Soft, mashed garlic cloves.
  - Charred pineapple or mango (if using), diced.

- **Add fresh elements:**
  - Fresh cilantro (1/4–1/2 cup chopped).
  - 1–2 limes, juice squeezed in.
  - Salt to taste.
  - Optional: 1/2 tsp cumin, pinch of chili powder, or a tiny bit of brown sugar.

**Process:**
1. Combine all charred/smoked and fresh ingredients in a bowl.
2. Adjust salt, acid (lime), and heat (extra jalapeño/pepper) to taste. [best practice]
3. Let the salsa rest **20–30 minutes** at room temp so flavors meld.

**Do's**
- Balance smoky/charred, fresh, and bright (lime/cilantro).
- Store in the fridge for up to 4–5 days; it actually tastes better the next day.

**Don'ts**
- Don't skip the fresh lime and cilantro; they keep the salsa bright.
- Don't add too much liquid (no water or broth); salsa should be chunky, not soup-like.

***

## 8. Do's / Don'ts summary

<!-- equipment: offset-smoker -->
**Do's (offset smoker)**
- Keep the smoke mild and the veggies blistered, not blackened.
- Use a thermometer to confirm 325–375°F before loading.
- Pull each item as it finishes — they're done at different times.

**Don'ts (offset smoker)**
- Don't smoke the veggies into mush; they should still hold shape when sliced.
- Don't overpower the salsa with smoke; it should be a background note.
<!-- /equipment -->

<!-- equipment: charcoal-grill -->
**Do's (charcoal grill)**
- Full-bed direct heat at 400–450°F, pan mid position.
- Work the grate with tongs — move items that are charring too fast to the edge.
- Pull each item as it reaches blistered and soft; they finish at different times.

**Don'ts (charcoal grill)**
- Don't leave garlic unwrapped directly on the grate; it'll burn before it softens.
- Don't go for 100% char all over — some char is perfect, total blackening is bitter.
