# Ribs & Pork Butt — First Cook on the CC1830S

---

## Firebox setup

1. **Charcoal basket in the firebox:**
   - Fill basket 2/3 full with unlit briquettes or lump.
   - Make a small well at one end. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

2. **Light a small amount of charcoal in the chimney** (about 1/3 chimney).

3. **Pour the lit charcoal into the well** on one side of the basket so the fire gradually burns through the unlit coals (minion‑style). [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

4. **Add 1–2 wood chunks** on or just beside the lit coals once the pit is warmed up — not right at startup to avoid heavy white smoke. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

---

## Vents and exhaust

- **Exhaust stack (chimney):** Run it **fully open** or nearly so; control temps mostly with the firebox intake. [reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/BBQ/comments/ai4ubr/how_critical_is_sealing_an_offset_smoker/)
- **Firebox intake vent:** Start around half open while the pit comes up to temp, then gradually close toward 1/4–1/3 open to hold ~225–275°F. [reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/BBQ/comments/ai4ubr/how_critical_is_sealing_an_offset_smoker/)

Adjust in small increments and give it 10–15 minutes to respond.

---

## Water/drip pan

Inside the main chamber, place a **foil pan with water** on the grate between the firebox side and the meat, or directly on the tuning plates under the meat, to catch drips and help stabilize temps.

---

## Meat placement

Put the **ribs or pork butt on the main grate toward the stack end**, not right next to the firebox opening.

- **Ribs:** Bone‑side down, then rotate halfway through if you want to even things out further.
- **Pork butt:** Fat cap up or down (your choice), but keep it on the stack side where temps are gentler.

---

## Thermometer probes

- Clip or place the **grate probe** near the meat on the cooking grate (not over the baffle opening). [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)
- Stick the **meat probe** in the thickest part of the butt or the center of a rib rack (if using). [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xQ2I_jjAMA8)

---

## Target temps

| What | Temp |
|------|------|
| Cook chamber (grate) | 225–275°F |
| Pork butt internal | ~200–205°F (pullable) |
| Ribs internal | 195–203°F (bones twist easily, meat bends nicely) |
