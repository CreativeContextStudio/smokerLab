**Pamplona** is the festive showpiece of the Uruguayan parrilla \u2014 a thin flank or matambre butterflied wide, loaded with a filling of ham, bacon, roasted peppers, hard-boiled eggs, and parsley, then rolled tight, tied with butcher\u2019s twine, and cooked slowly over a steady ember bed until golden and sliceable. When you cut it at the table, the spiral cross-section turns every guest\u2019s head. For 6\u20138 people, plan one 2\u20133 lb roll and 60\u201380 minutes on the grate.

***

## Fuego / Fire Setup

Pamplona needs steady, moderate-to-low heat \u2014 it\u2019s a thick roll and needs time for the center to cook through without burning the exterior.

1. Start your fire **60\u201390 minutes** before cooking. Burn 3\u20134 hardwood logs (quebracho, eucalyptus, or oak) in the brasero until you have a deep, established ember bed.
2. Rake a generous layer of coals under the full grate.
3. **Set the grate at mid-high position** \u2014 approximately 15\u201320 cm (6\u20138 inches) above the embers. The roll is dense and needs time \u2014 too much heat will char the outside before the center cooks.
4. Maintain the brasero throughout by adding fresh wood and raking new embers every 25\u201330 minutes.

**Do\u2019s**
- After placing the pamplona, maintain a steady, even ember bed \u2014 inconsistent heat will cook one side more than the other.
- Rotate the roll every 15\u201320 minutes to ensure all sides cook evenly.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t use high heat \u2014 the dense roll needs time.
- Don\u2019t let the fire fade; the full cook requires sustained, even ember production.

***

## Preparaci\u00F3n / Meat Prep

### Butterflying the meat

1. Lay the vac\u00EDo or matambre on a cutting board, fat-side down.
2. Using a sharp knife, cut horizontally through the thickness to butterfly it open, stopping about 1 cm from the edge. Open like a book. The piece should be roughly even in thickness \u2014 about 1\u20131.5 cm thick throughout.
3. If needed, cover with plastic wrap and pound lightly with a meat mallet to even it out.

### Building the filling

4. Lay slices of ham evenly over the entire surface of the meat, leaving a 2 cm border at one edge (the seam edge).
5. Lay strips of bacon over the ham.
6. Distribute roasted pepper strips evenly.
7. Place halved or quartered hard-boiled eggs in a line down the center of the roll direction.
8. Scatter minced garlic and fresh parsley over everything.
9. Season with a light pinch of salt and black pepper.

### Rolling and tying

10. Starting from the long edge opposite the seam border, roll the meat tightly \u2014 as tight as you can without tearing it.
11. Tie the roll with butcher\u2019s twine at 2\u20133 cm intervals \u2014 this keeps the spiral together through the cook and slicing.
12. **Season the exterior generously** with sal gruesa.

**Do\u2019s**
- Roll as tight as possible \u2014 a loose pamplona loses its spiral when sliced.
- Tie firmly and evenly.
- Prepare the pamplona 2\u20134 hours in advance and refrigerate \u2014 the filling sets and the roll holds its shape better.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t overfill \u2014 too much filling makes the roll impossible to close and the edges split on the grate.
- Don\u2019t skip the twine \u2014 the roll will unravel over a long cook without it.

***

## Secuencia de Parrilla / Grill Sequence

### Phase 1: All-sides browning (20\u201325 min)

1. Place the pamplona on the mid-high grate over a steady ember bed.
2. Turn it every **5\u20136 minutes** to brown all four sides. Think of it as a cylinder \u2014 you want an even golden crust around the entire exterior.
3. After 20\u201325 minutes, the exterior should be uniformly golden-brown.

### Phase 2: Slow through-cook (40\u201360 min)

1. Continue rotating every 15\u201320 minutes.
2. The interior is done when an instant-read thermometer inserted through the side into the center reads **70\u00B0C (160\u00B0F)** \u2014 enough for the ham, bacon, and egg filling to be fully cooked and set.
3. Without a thermometer: press the center of the roll firmly with tongs. It should feel firm throughout with very little give. A soft, spongy center means 15\u201320 more minutes.

**Sensory cues:**
- Exterior: uniformly golden-brown, pulling slightly from the twine knots
- Aroma: deep roasted meat and caramelized fat
- Press test: firm all the way through

### Phase 3: Rest and slice

- Remove from the grate and rest **10 minutes** on a board.
- Cut the twine strings and slice into rounds 2\u20133 cm thick.
- Arrange the slices on a cutting board or platter so the spiral filling is visible \u2014 this is the moment.

***

## Servicio y Acompa\u00F1amientos / Serving and Sides

- Pamplona is a centrepiece \u2014 slice it at the table if possible, letting guests see the spiral before serving.
- **Chimichurri** passed alongside.
- **Salsa criolla** for brightness against the richness of the filling.
- **Classic sides:** ensalada mixta, papas al rescoldo, morrones asados.
- **Drink:** Tannat \u2014 firm enough to stand up to the richness of the bacon-and-ham filling.

**Cultural note:** Pamplona is the asado\u2019s festive dish \u2014 it appears for birthdays, quincea\u00F1eras, family reunions, and end-of-year asados. The beauty of the spiral cross-section is part of the tradition. It takes patience to make well, which is why it\u2019s reserved for occasions that deserve it.

***

## S\u00ED hac\u00E9 / Do\u2019s

- Butterfly evenly so the roll is consistent in thickness.
- Roll as tight as possible and tie firmly with twine every 2\u20133 cm.
- Prepare and refrigerate the roll several hours in advance \u2014 it holds better.
- Rotate every 5\u20136 minutes during the browning phase, every 15\u201320 minutes during the through-cook phase.
- Rest before slicing so the filling sets.

## No hag\u00E1s / Don\u2019ts

- Don\u2019t skip the twine \u2014 the roll will unravel.
- Don\u2019t use high heat \u2014 a burnt exterior with a raw center is the main failure mode.
- Don\u2019t fill too generously \u2014 restraint makes for a cleaner spiral.
- Don\u2019t slice before resting \u2014 the filling needs to set or it will collapse.
