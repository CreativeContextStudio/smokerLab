## Uruguayan‑style flan with dulce de leche

Goal: Smooth baked caramel custard you chill and serve with dulce de leche. Make this ahead.

### Ingredients (6–8 servings)

**For the caramel**

- 1 cup sugar

**For the custard**

- 4–5 large eggs  
- 2 cups milk (or 1½ cups milk + ½ cup cream)  
- ½–1 cup sugar (adjust to your sweetness preference)  
- 1–2 teaspoons vanilla extract  
- Pinch of salt  

**To serve**

- Dulce de leche

### Step 1 – Make the caramel

1. Add 1 cup sugar to a clean, dry saucepan.  
2. Heat over medium, without stirring at first; swirl the pan occasionally as the sugar melts.  
3. Continue cooking until the sugar is fully melted and a deep amber color.  
4. Immediately pour the caramel into a flan mold or several small ramekins.  
5. Tilt and rotate to coat the bottoms evenly.  
6. Set aside; the caramel will harden.

### Step 2 – Make the custard

1. In a bowl, whisk the eggs until smooth.  
2. Add the sugar, vanilla, and salt; whisk until combined.  
3. Warm the milk (and cream if using) until just lukewarm—not boiling.  
4. Slowly whisk the warm milk into the egg mixture.  
5. For extra smooth texture, strain the custard through a fine sieve into a jug.

### Step 3 – Fill the mold and set up the bain‑marie

1. Pour the custard over the hardened caramel in the mold or ramekins.  
2. Place the mold(s) in a deep baking pan.  
3. Pour hot water into the baking pan until it reaches about halfway up the sides of the mold(s).

### Step 4 – Bake

1. Preheat the oven to 320–350°F (160–175°C).  
2. Carefully place the baking pan with the water and flan into the oven.  
3. Bake until the custard is set around the edges with a slight wobble in the center when gently shaken.  
4. This usually takes about 40–60 minutes, depending on mold size and oven.

### Step 5 – Cool and chill

1. Remove the pan from the oven and take the mold(s) out of the water.  
2. Let cool to room temperature.  
3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, until fully chilled.

### Step 6 – Unmold and serve

1. Run a thin knife around the edge of the flan to loosen it.  
2. Place a plate over the mold and invert quickly and firmly.  
3. Lift off the mold; the flan should release and the caramel will flow over it.  
4. Slice into portions.  
5. Serve each piece with a generous spoonful of dulce de leche on top or on the side.

**Do’s**

- Do use hot water in the bain‑marie to help the custard cook gently.  
- Do chill thoroughly before unmolding so it holds its shape.

**Don’ts**

- Don’t boil the custard mixture; it will curdle.  
- Don’t overbake until it’s solid all the way through; a slight wobble gives a creamy texture.
