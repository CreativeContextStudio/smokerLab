**Entra\u00F1a** is the express cut of the asado \u2014 inside skirt steak that goes onto an intensely hot, close grate after the slow cuts are already resting. It cooks in 6\u20138 minutes total, developing a charred, crackling exterior while staying blood-red inside. For 4\u20136 guests, plan 2\u20133 lb and have everything else ready before it goes on.

***

## Fuego / Fire Setup

Entra\u00F1a needs the **hottest ember bed of the asado** \u2014 this is typically the point in the cook where your brasero is at peak production and you have plenty of established coals.

1. Rake a thick, concentrated layer of embers under the grate zone where you\u2019ll cook the entra\u00F1a.
2. **Lower the grate** to approximately 5\u20138 cm (2\u20133 inches) above the ember bed. This is your hot-sear position.
3. Test the heat: hold your hand 10 cm above the grate. You should feel intense, almost aggressive heat and have to pull away within 2 seconds. That\u2019s right.

**Timing in the asado sequence:** Entra\u00F1a goes on **after** asado de tira and vac\u00EDo have been pulled and are resting, and **after** chorizo has been served. Its speed is the point \u2014 it bridges the gap between the slow cuts resting and serving them.

**Do\u2019s**
- Entra\u00F1a should go on last, when the coals are at their hottest and you\u2019re ready to serve immediately.
- Keep the area under the entra\u00F1a zone dense with coals \u2014 a thin coal bed will lose heat fast.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t cook entra\u00F1a over a fading ember bed. It needs aggressive, sustained heat for the crust.
- Don\u2019t lower the grate if there\u2019s active flame \u2014 wait for the flare to die and then proceed.

***

## Preparaci\u00F3n / Meat Prep

1. Remove entra\u00F1a from refrigeration **20\u201330 minutes** before cooking.
2. If the membrane (silverskin) is still on, trim it off \u2014 it will not render and will make the entra\u00F1a curl and toughen on the grate.
3. Season both sides generously with **sal gruesa** just before it goes on the grate. No advance seasoning, no marinade.
4. Entra\u00F1a is thin \u2014 roughly 1\u20131.5 cm thick. It does not need to come to room temperature as urgently as thicker cuts.

**Do\u2019s**
- Remove the silverskin if present \u2014 this makes a real difference in texture.
- Season at the last moment so the salt doesn\u2019t draw surface moisture before the sear.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t marinate entra\u00F1a. A lime or vinegar marinade will start to cook the surface and prevent a proper crust.
- Don\u2019t slice ahead of cooking \u2014 keep the whole piece intact on the grate.

***

## Secuencia de Parrilla / Grill Sequence

### Phase 1: First side \u2014 direct hot sear (3\u20134 min)

1. Place entra\u00F1a on the **low, hot grate** over the dense ember bed.
2. Leave it alone completely for 3\u20134 minutes. You should hear aggressive, loud sizzling immediately.
3. Watch the edges: when a grey-brown color creeps about 1/3 of the way up the side of the cut, it\u2019s time to flip.

**Sensory cues for Phase 1:**
- Loud, assertive sizzle from the moment it hits the grate: good
- Visible smoke from the fat and char: normal and expected
- Edges showing grey-brown colour rising from the grate side: ready to flip

### Phase 2: Second side (3\u20134 min)

1. Flip once with tongs \u2014 do not press.
2. Cook 3\u20134 more minutes. The total cook time for a 1\u20131.5 cm entra\u00F1a is **6\u20138 minutes** for medium-rare.
3. Pull when the thickest part feels slightly springy under finger pressure \u2014 there should still be a clear give in the center.

**Internal temperature (if using a thermometer):** Pull at 50\u201352\u00B0C (120\u2013125\u00B0F) for medium-rare. Carryover will bring it to 55\u00B0C (130\u00B0F) during the rest.

**Entra\u00F1a is always served pink.** A grey, fully-cooked interior is a sad waste of the cut.

### Phase 3: Rest and slice

- Rest **3\u20135 minutes** on a board.
- **Slice across the grain** at a slight angle in strips about 1 cm wide. The grain of entra\u00F1a runs lengthwise \u2014 slicing across it gives tender bites.

***

## Servicio y Acompa\u00F1amientos / Serving and Sides

- Serve immediately after slicing \u2014 entra\u00F1a loses heat quickly once sliced.
- **Chimichurri** at the table.
- **Sequence:** Served as part of the main course alongside asado de tira, vac\u00EDo, and matambre.
- **Drink:** Tannat or a good Malbec.

**Cultural note:** Entra\u00F1a is the asador\u2019s reward for patience. After tending the long slow cuts for over an hour, the entra\u00F1a is the fast, hot, exhilarating finish \u2014 done before the guests have finished pouring the next glass.

***

## S\u00ED hac\u00E9 / Do\u2019s

- Remove the silverskin/membrane before cooking.
- Season at the last moment with sal gruesa only.
- Cook over the hottest part of the ember bed, grate low.
- Cook quickly: 3\u20134 minutes per side maximum.
- Slice immediately against the grain and serve at once.

## No hag\u00E1s / Don\u2019ts

- Don\u2019t marinate \u2014 it fights the crust.
- Don\u2019t cook past medium-rare \u2014 entra\u00F1a toughens fast when overcooked.
- Don\u2019t press the meat onto the grate.
- Don\u2019t slice with the grain \u2014 it will be chewy and disappointing.
- Don\u2019t let it sit off the grate too long before slicing \u2014 it\u2019s a thin cut and cools fast.
