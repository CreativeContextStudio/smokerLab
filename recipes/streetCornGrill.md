**Step‑by‑step guide** for **grilled corn “Mexican street style”** (elote), tuned for **6–8 ears** over **direct medium heat**. Total time: **15–20 minutes active grilling** after preheat, charred kernels, creamy topping.

***

## 1. Prep the corn (10–15 minutes before grilling)

- **Choose the corn:**  
  - 6–8 ears **fresh corn in the husk** (silks optional to remove).  
- **Topping (make ahead):**  
  - 1/2 cup mayonnaise or Mexican crema.  
  - 1/4 cup cotija or feta cheese, crumbled.  
  - 1–2 limes (juice + zest).  
  - Chili powder or Tajín (1–2 tsp).  
  - Fresh cilantro (optional, chopped).  

**Do’s**  
- Peel back husks (don’t remove), remove silks, brush kernels with **butter or oil + salt**.  
- Pull husks back up to cover kernels.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t soak in water; steaming dulls char.  
- Don’t skip silks removal; gritty kernels ruin it.  

***

## 2. Set up the grill

<!-- equipment: charcoal-grill -->
- **Target temp:** Direct **medium (375–425°F)**.
- **Fire setup:**
  - **Charcoal pan in mid position.**
  - **Full bed** of charcoal across the grate (1–1.5 chimney loads, even layer). [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)
- **Vents:**
  - **Mostly open** for steady airflow. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

**Do’s**
- **Preheat 10–15 minutes** with lid closed until lid thermometer reads ~400°F.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t preheat too long; corn cooks fast.
<!-- /equipment -->

<!-- equipment: gas-grill -->
- **Target temp:** Direct **medium (375–425°F)**.
- **Weber Spirit II setup:**
  - **E‑310:** All three burners MEDIUM‑HIGH.
  - **E‑210:** Both burners MEDIUM‑HIGH.
  - Light with lid **open**, then close to preheat.
- **Preheat:** All burners MEDIUM‑HIGH, lid closed **10–15 min** until ~400°F.

**Do’s**
- Gas gives very consistent medium heat—ideal for husked corn.
- Rotate ears every 2–3 min; no hot spots like charcoal.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t use HIGH; chars husks too fast before kernels steam through.
<!-- /equipment -->  

***

## 3. Place the corn on the grill

- **Position:**  
  - **Direct over coals** across the hot grate.  
- **Orientation:**  
  - Lay ears flat, husks touching grate.  

**Do’s**  
- Grill **7–10 minutes**, rotating every 2–3 minutes for even char.  
- Peel back husks after cooking for final char if desired.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t overcrowd; needs space to char evenly.  
- Don’t soak husks; you want steam + char.  

***

## 4. Grill time and temp targets (375–425°F direct)

**Goal:** Charred husks, tender/hot kernels. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

| Phase | Time | Visual cue |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Husks on:** 7–10 min | Rotate every 2–3 min | Husks charred black in spots, steam escaping. |
| **Optional husk‑off char:** 2–3 min | Peel back, direct heat | Kernels blistered/golden. |

**Do’s**  
- Check after 7 min; pull when husks are charred and kernels yield to pressure.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t let husks burn completely off; protects kernels.  

***

**Target finish:** Charred husks, hot/tender kernels.  

***

## 6. Finish and serve

**Do’s**  
- Peel husks back immediately after grilling.  
- Brush with **mayo/crema**, sprinkle **cotija + chili powder**, squeeze **lime**, garnish cilantro.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t top before grilling; toppings burn.  
- Don’t wrap after; serve hot.  

***

## 7. Do’s / Don’ts for Mexican street corn on CC1830S main grill

**Do’s**  
- Use **full medium grate** and **lid thermometer** to confirm ~400°F.  
- Leave husks on first for steam + char.  
- Top **after grilling** for creamy contrast.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t skip preheat; uneven char.  
- Don’t overcrowd; steaming dulls flavor.  

**Scale notes:**  
- **4 ears:** Shave 2–3 min.  
- **8+ ears:** Cook in batches if grate crowded.
