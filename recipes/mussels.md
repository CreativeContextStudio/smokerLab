Here’s a **tight, step‑by‑step guide** for **smoked mussels** on your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S** at **350–400°F**, with **do’s / don’ts** and time targets for **2–4 lbs of live mussels**. This method focuses on **tender, flavorful mussels with a light smoke flavor**, not a heavy BBQ crust.

***

## 1. Prep the mussels (30–60 minutes before smoking)

- **Choose the mussels:**  
  - Live, in‑shell mussels with tightly closed shells. Discard any open mussels that don’t close when tapped.  
- **Clean the mussels:**  
  - Rinse under cold water to remove barnacles, grit, and seaweed.  
  - Remove beards (stringy fibers) from the shells.

**Do’s**  
- Soak the cleaned mussels in cold water with a bit of salt for **10–20 minutes** to purge sand and grit.  
- Keep them cold until you’re ready to smoke.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t leave the mussels at room temp for long; keep them cold.  
- Don’t skip the beard removal; it can make the mussels gritty.

***

## 2. Season the mussels (right before smoking)

- **Simple seasoning:**  
  - Olive oil, garlic, salt, and a pinch of black pepper.  
  - Optional: A squeeze of lemon juice or a splash of white wine.

**Do’s**  
- Toss the mussels in a bowl with the seasoning so every shell is coated.  
- Drain off any excess liquid to avoid dripping in the smoker.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t over‑salt; the shells already add salt.  
- Don’t add heavy spices or sugar; they can overpower the mussels.

***

## 3. Set up the Royal Gourmet smoker

- **Target temp:** 350–400°F for mussels (this is **hotter** than brisket, which is fine for a small, quick cook).  
- **Fire:**  
  - Charcoal basket in the firebox, building a **hot charcoal bed** (no minion‑style; this is a fast, high‑heat cook).  
  - Add 1–2 small mild wood chunks (apple, maple, or oak) for a light smoke flavor. [best practice]  
- **Vents:**  
  - Stack **fully open**; firebox intake **1/2–3/4 open** to reach 350–400°F. [best practice]  
- **Pan:**  
  - Place a shallow water pan under the grate opposite the heat source to catch drips and keep the smoker clean. [best practice]  

**Do’s**  
- Let the smoker come to temp **before** you load the mussels.  
- Use a **grate‑level thermometer** near the mussels to monitor the surface temp.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t start the mussels too early; the smoker should be hot and stable before you load them.  
- Don’t overcrowd the grate; mussels need space for even air circulation.  

***

## 4. Place the mussels on the offset

- **Position:** Stack side of the chamber, not directly over the hottest coals.  
- **Orientation:**  
  - Place the mussels **shell‑side down** on the grate; the open side will face up.  
  - If you’re worried about the shells sticking, place a **thin sheet of parchment paper** under the shells.  

**Do’s**  
- Use **tongs or heat‑resistant gloves** to place the mussels gently on the grate.  
- Double‑check that the shells are not stuck to the grate before you close the lid.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t put the mussels directly over the hottest coals; it can burn the shells.  
- Don’t move them around once they’re on the grate.  

***

## 5. Smoke time and temp targets (350–400°F)

**Goal:** **All shells open**, with **tender, plump meat** that pulls away from the shell easily. [best practice]  

| Temp range | Time (approx) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 350–400°F | 5–10 minutes | Great for 2–4 lbs of mussels, with a nice balance of smoke and tenderness. |

**Do’s**  
- Use a **meat‑probe thermometer** inserted into the thickest part of a mussel (optional; usually not needed).  
- Check the mussels every 3–5 minutes after the first 3 minutes.  
- If the shells are still closed, keep going; if they’re open and the meat is opaque, they’re done.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the mussels.  
- Don’t let the internal temp exceed 145°F; it can dry out.  

***

## 6. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (2–4 lbs mussels at 350–400°F)

Use this as a mental guide; adjust for your mussels’ behavior.

| Time since loading the mussels | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| 0–3 min | Load the mussels shell‑side down on the offset, close the lid. [best practice] |
| 3–5 min | No opening; let the mussels come to temp and the smoke start to build. |
| 5–7 min | Check the mussels; if shells are still closed, keep going. |
| 7–10 min | Check again; if shells are open and meat is opaque, remove the mussels. [best practice] |

**Target finish:** **All shells open**, **tender and plump meat**, with no rubbery texture.  

***

## 7. Finish and rest the mussels

- **Option A (no additional finish):**  
  - Remove the mussels at 5–10 minutes when all shells are open.  
  - Let them rest for 2–3 minutes before serving.  

- **Option B (broil or sauté finish):**  
  - Remove the mussels at 5–7 minutes, then broil or sauté them in a hot pan with a bit of olive oil and garlic for 1–2 minutes to crisp and brown the shells.  

**Do’s**  
- Let the mussels rest for 2–3 minutes before serving.  
- Serve with a squeeze of lemon and a drizzle of melted butter.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t let the shells stay closed for long; they’re done when they open.  
- Don’t skip the rest; it’s critical for juicy meat.  

***

## 8. Do’s / Don’ts for smoked mussels on your offset

**Do’s**  
- Use a **sharp knife** for cleaning and beard removal.  
- Keep the mussels cold and seasoned lightly.  
- Use a **thermometer** and **probe tenderness** as your final decision‑maker. [best practice]  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t over‑season; mussels are delicate.  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the mussels.  
- Don’t skip the rest; it’s critical for juicy meat.
