Here's a **tight, step‑by‑step guide** for **reverse sear steaks** on the **main grill chamber** of your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S**, tuned for **2–4 thick cuts (1.5–2" ribeye, NY strip, or tenderloin)**. Total time: **45–90 min indirect low heat + 2–4 min sear**, even pink center, steakhouse crust. No offset firebox — all done in the main barrel with banked coals.
<!-- /equipment -->

***

## 1. Prep the steaks (1–4 hours before cooking)

- **Choose the steaks:**
  - **Ribeye, NY strip, or tenderloin** (1.5–2" thick, 12–20 oz each).
- **Seasoning:**
  - Coarse kosher salt + black pepper (or SPG rub).

**Do's**
- Pat dry, season generously; **dry brine** fridge uncovered 1–4 hours (or overnight).
- Let sit room temp **30 min** before going on the heat.

**Don'ts**
- Don't use thin cuts (under 1.25"); they dry out before the technique works.

***

<!-- equipment: offset-smoker -->
## 2. Set up the Royal Gourmet CC1830S offset smoker

- **Smoke phase temp:** **225–250°F** in the smoker chamber.
- **Fire setup:**
  - **Firebox:** Small steady charcoal fire (1 chimney unlit + lit top), add **hickory or oak chunks** for smoke.
  - **Smoker chamber:** Clean grate, water pan half‑full under the grate near the firebox side. [royalgourmetcorp](https://www.royalgourmetcorp.com/pdfs/item/2020/20200922/45bdea1a-4c30-4965-976d-e9fea0aa2fa4__CC1830S.pdf)
- **Vents:**
  - Firebox intake **1/4 open**, exhaust **1/2 open** for thin blue smoke. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

**Do's**
- Preheat and stabilize **20–30 min** to 225–250°F grate temp before loading steaks.

**Don'ts**
- Don't let the firebox overheat; thin blue smoke only — no thick white plumes.

***

## 3. Smoke the steaks (indirect low and slow)

- **Position:** Farthest grate spot from the firebox door.
- **Probe:** Insert instant-read into the thickest part.

**Do's**
- Smoke **lid closed** until **115–120°F internal** (medium-rare target before sear).

**Don'ts**
- Don't open the lid more than needed; hold temp steady.

***

## 4. Temps and times (225–250°F smoke, then 500°F+ sear)

**Goal:** Finish **130–135°F** medium-rare after sear. [chargriller](https://www.chargriller.com/blogs/tips-and-care/how-to-reverse-sear-on-an-offset-smoker)

| Cut / thickness | Smoke time to 115°F | Sear time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1.5–2" ribeye / strip** | 45–90 min | 1–2 min/side |
| **Tomahawk (2"+)** | 90–120 min | 2 min/side [chargriller](https://www.chargriller.com/blogs/tips-and-care/how-to-reverse-sear-on-an-offset-smoker) |

**Do's**
- Pull at **115°F** — carryover and sear add 15°F.

**Don'ts**
- Don't over-smoke; bitter flavor at thick cuts.

***

## 5. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (offset smoker, 225–250°F smoke to 500°F+ sear)

| Phase | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| **Firebox setup** | Small charcoal fire + 2 wood chunks, stabilize 225–250°F smoker chamber. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850) |
| **0–45/90 min (Smoke)** | Steaks on far grate, probe to 115–120°F internal, thin smoke. |
| **Rest** | 10–15 min tented foil (optional). |
| **Sear setup** | Add hot coals to firebox/main, open vents wide, hit 500°F+ hot zone. |
| **Sear (2–4 min)** | Hot zone over coals, 1–2 min/side, hit 130–135°F. Pull. [chargriller](https://www.chargriller.com/blogs/tips-and-care/how-to-reverse-sear-on-an-offset-smoker) |

**Target finish:** Pink smoke ring, steakhouse crust.

## 2. Set up the Royal Gourmet CC1830S main chamber

The charcoal-grill reverse sear uses banked coals for a low indirect phase, then a full-blast sear to finish.

- **Indirect phase temp:** **275–325°F** (cool zone, lid thermometer reading ~325°F).
- **Fire setup (banked):**
  - **Charcoal pan in low position** for the indirect phase.
  - Light **3/4 chimney** (~3 lb briquettes). Pour all coals banked tightly to **one end only** of the charcoal pan — not half, but pushed all the way to one end.
  - Food goes on the **far end, opposite the coals**.
- **Vents:**
  - **1/2 open** during indirect phase to hold 275–325°F lid temp. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

**Do's**
- Preheat 20 minutes with lid closed to confirm the cool end is 275–325°F (lid thermometer ~325°F).
- If you have a grate-level probe, set it in the indirect zone near the steaks.

**Don'ts**
- Don't use the high pan position for the indirect phase — it runs too hot and defeats the purpose.
- Don't spread coals across half the pan; bank them to **one end** to create a longer cool zone.

***

## 3. Cook the steaks indirect (slow phase)

- **Position:** Steaks on the **far end** of the grate, away from the coal bank.
- **Probe:** Insert instant-read into the thickest part before closing the lid.

**Do's**
- Cook **lid closed** until steaks hit **115–120°F internal** (medium-rare target before sear).
- Flip once halfway through the indirect cook for even heat distribution.

**Don'ts**
- Don't open the lid more than once per 15 minutes — every open costs 25–50°F and slows the cook.

***

## 4. Temps and times (275–325°F indirect, then 500°F+ sear)

**Goal:** Finish **130–135°F** medium-rare after sear.

| Cut / thickness | Indirect time to 115°F | Sear time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1.5" ribeye / strip** | 45–60 min at 300°F | 1–2 min/side |
| **2" ribeye / strip** | 60–90 min at 275–300°F | 2 min/side |

**Do's**
- Pull at **115°F** — the sear adds 10–15°F carryover.

**Don'ts**
- Don't let the indirect zone run above 350°F; the reverse sear technique only works in low-heat indirect.

***

## 5. Setting up the sear (transitioning from indirect to 500°F+)

After pulling steaks off indirect, you need to ramp the fire for searing. Two approaches:

**Option A (pre-loaded sear chimney):**
- Start a second **1/2 chimney** around 30 minutes into the indirect cook.
- When steaks hit 115°F, pull them and tent with foil (10 min rest while fire ramps).
- Raise **charcoal pan to high position**.
- Add the fresh lit coals from the second chimney on top of the original bank.
- Open vents **fully open**.
- Preheat 5–8 minutes until the hot zone is screaming (500°F+).

**Option B (use what's there):**
- When steaks hit 115°F, pull them and tent with foil.
- Raise **charcoal pan to high position** and open vents fully.
- Wait 8–10 minutes for the existing coals to intensify.
- This works if the coals are still active; if they've quieted down too much, Option A is better.

***

## 6. Sear the steaks

- **Move steaks to the hot zone** (directly over the coal bank).
- **Sear 1–2 minutes per side** until a deep brown crust forms.
- **Pull at 130°F** (medium-rare) or **135°F** (medium); carryover finishes them.

**Do's**
- Sear **lid open** for full control and the fastest crust formation.
- Use **long tongs**; fat drips can cause flare-ups at this temp.
- Butter baste optional: add a knob of butter and a sprig of thyme, tilt and spoon over the steak during the last 30 seconds.

**Don'ts**
- Don't walk away during the sear — at 500°F+, 30 seconds is the difference between crust and char.
- Don't press the steak down; it releases juices and slows crust formation.

***

## 5. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (charcoal grill, 275–325°F indirect to 500°F+ sear)

| Phase | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| **Grill setup** | 3/4 chimney, banked one end, pan low position, vents 1/2 open. Preheat 20 min. |
| **0–45/90 min (Indirect)** | Steaks on far end of grate, lid closed, flip once at midpoint. Probe to 115°F. |
| **At 115°F: Transition** | Pull steaks, tent foil. Add fresh lit coals if using Option A. Raise pan to high, open vents fully. |
| **Sear setup (5–10 min)** | Let fire ramp to 500°F+ over hot zone. |
| **Sear (2–4 min)** | Steaks over coals, 1–2 min/side, lid open. Pull at 130–135°F. |

**Target finish:** Deep steakhouse crust, even pink throughout.
<!-- /equipment -->

***

## 6. Rest and serve

**Do's**
- Rest steaks **5–10 minutes** tented loosely with foil on a warm plate.
- Slice **against the grain** for maximum tenderness.
- Butter baste (if not done during sear) works beautifully on top of the rested slice.

**Don'ts**
- Don't cut early; juices need to redistribute.
- Don't wrap tightly — a little vent keeps the crust from steaming soft.

***

## 7. Do's / Don'ts summary

<!-- equipment: offset-smoker -->
**Do's (offset smoker)**
- Run 225–250°F thin blue smoke in the chamber; probe-precise temp is everything.
- Dry brine the steaks, rest between smoke and sear phases.
- Hit 500°F+ for the hot coal sear — short and hot. [chargriller](https://www.chargriller.com/blogs/tips-and-care/how-to-reverse-sear-on-an-offset-smoker)

**Don'ts (offset smoker)**
- Don't over-smoke thick cuts; bitter flavor results.
- Don't skip the probe; guesswork fails at this technique.
<!-- /equipment -->

<!-- equipment: charcoal-grill -->
**Do's (charcoal grill)**
- Bank coals tightly to one end, pan on low position, vents 1/2 open for the indirect phase.
- Let the fire ramp hard (pan to high, vents fully open) before the sear.
- Sear lid open for full crust control.

**Don'ts (charcoal grill)**
- Don't let the indirect zone run hot (above 350°F) — bank tighter and close vents if needed.
- Don't skip the transition rest between phases; the sear needs a hot, ready fire.
<!-- /equipment -->

**Scale notes:**
- **2 steaks:** Same timing.
- **4+ steaks:** Stagger indirect by 5 minutes; stagger sear to avoid crowding the hot zone.

Steakhouse result, no special gear needed.
