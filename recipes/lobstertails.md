**Step‑by‑step guide** for **smoked lobster tails** on your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S** at **325–375°F**, with **do’s / don’ts** and time targets for **2–6 lobster tails** (about 4–8 oz each). This method focuses on **delicate, sweet lobster with a light smoke flavor**, not a heavy BBQ crust.

***

## 1. Prep the lobster tails (30–60 minutes before smoking)

- **Choose the lobster tails:**  
  - Fresh, chilled, or thawed frozen lobster tails; **7–10 oz each** is ideal for smoking.  
- **Butterfly the tails (optional but recommended):**  
  - Use kitchen shears to cut down the center of the shell along the back.  
  - Gently open the shell and lift the meat out slightly so it lies flat on the shell.

**Do’s**  
- Rinse under cold water and pat extremely dry with paper towels.  
- If the tails are very thick, you can remove them from the shell entirely for even cooking.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t leave the lobster at room temp for long; keep it cold until you’re ready to smoke.  
- Don’t skip the butterflying step; it helps cook evenly.

***

## 2. Season the lobster tails (10–15 minutes before smoking)

- **Simple seasoning:**  
  - Melted butter, garlic, salt, and a pinch of black pepper.  
  - Optional: A squeeze of lemon juice or zest.

**Do’s**  
- Brush the seasoned butter mixture over the exposed lobster meat; don’t oversaturate.  
- Avoid heavy spices or sugar; lobster is delicate and can burn easily.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t use a heavy rub or glaze; it can overpower the lobster’s flavor.  
- Don’t oversalt; the butter and lemon already add salt.

***

## 3. Set up the Royal Gourmet smoker

- **Target temp:** 325–375°F for lobster tails (this is **hotter** than brisket, which is fine for a small, quick cook).
- **Fire:**
  - Charcoal basket in the firebox, building a **hot charcoal bed** (no minion‑style; this is a fast, high‑heat cook).
  - Add 1–2 small mild wood chunks (apple, maple, or oak) for a light smoke flavor. [best practice]
- **Vents:**
  - Stack **fully open**; firebox intake **1/2–3/4 open** to reach 325–375°F. [best practice]
- **Pan:**
  - Place a shallow water pan under the grate opposite the heat source to catch drips and keep the smoker clean. [best practice]

**Do’s**
- Let the smoker come to temp **before** you load the lobster.
- Use a **grate‑level thermometer** near the lobster to monitor the surface temp.

**Don’ts**
- Don’t start the lobster too early; the smoker should be hot and stable before you load it.
- Don’t overcrowd the grate; lobster tails need space for even air circulation.

## SM045 Setup

Set your SM045 to **275–300°F** — its maximum practical range, which is lower than the offset’s 325–375°F. Preheat fully before loading.

Load **1 oz of apple or cherry chunks** in the wood box. Lobster is extremely delicate; a light fruitwood charge is all you want in the sealed cabinet.

Butterfly the tails (cut down the center of the shell, lift the meat), brush with the garlic butter, and place meat-side up on the middle rack. Close the door.

Budget **15–25 minutes** — longer than the offset version because the SM045 runs at lower heat. Begin probing at 12 minutes. Target **140°F** internal; pull at **138°F** and let carryover finish it. At 145°F+ lobster becomes rubbery — this is a tight window, so watch it closely.

The SM045’s humidity actually helps here, keeping the delicate meat from drying out. Finish with a quick 1–2 minute broil to brown and caramelize the butter on the exposed meat before serving.

## 4. Place the lobster tails on the offset

- **Position:** Stack side of the chamber, not directly over the hottest coals.  
- **Orientation:**  
  - Place the butterflied lobster tails **meat‑side up** on the grate; the shell will protect the meat underneath.  
  - If you’re using larger tails, you can place them on a **wire rack** to allow air circulation.  

**Do’s**  
- Use **tongs or heat‑resistant gloves** to place the lobster gently on the grate.  
- Double‑check that the shell is not stuck to the grate before you close the lid.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t put the lobster directly over the hottest coals; it can burn the meat.  
- Don’t move them around once they’re on the grate.  

***

## 5. Smoke time and temp targets (325–375°F)

**Goal:** 140–145°F internal at the thickest part, with **flaky, moist meat** and **no rubbery texture**. [best practice]  

| Temp range | Time (approx) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 325–375°F | 8–15 minutes | Great for 2–6 lobster tails, with a nice balance of smoke and tenderness. |

**Do’s**  
- Use a **meat‑probe thermometer** inserted into the thickest part of the tail.  
- Check the temp every 3–5 minutes after the first 5 minutes.  
- If the meat is still translucent, keep going; if it’s opaque and slightly firm, it’s done.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the lobster.  
- Don’t let the internal temp exceed 145°F; it can dry out and become rubbery.  

***

## 6. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (4 lobster tails at 325–375°F)

Use this as a mental guide; adjust for your lobster’s behavior.

| Time since loading the lobster | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| 0–5 min | Load the lobster tails meat‑side up on the offset, close the lid. [best practice] |
| 5–7 min | No opening; let the lobster come to temp and the smoke start to build. |
| 7–10 min | Check the internal temp; it should be 110–125°F. If it’s on the lower side, keep going. |
| 10–12 min | Check again; if it’s 130–140°F, start thinking about finishing. |
| 12–15 min | Check again; if it’s 140–145°F, remove the lobster. [best practice] |

**Target finish:** 140–145°F internal, **flaky and moist** with a slight pink or opaque color.  

***

## 7. Finish and rest the lobster

- **Option A (internal crisp):**  
  - Increase the smoker temp to 375–400°F for the last 2–3 minutes to crisp the shell and caramelize the butter.  
  - Keep the lid closed and watch for the shell to turn golden and crackle.  

- **Option B (external sear):**  
  - Remove the lobster at 140–145°F and let it rest for 2–3 minutes.  
  - Then, broil the exposed meat under a hot broiler for 1–2 minutes to crisp and brown it.  

**Do’s**  
- Let the lobster rest for 2–3 minutes before serving.  
- Serve with a squeeze of lemon and a drizzle of melted butter.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t let the internal temp exceed 145°F; it can become rubbery.  
- Don’t skip the rest; it’s critical for juicy meat.  

***

## 8. Do’s / Don’ts for smoked lobster tails on your offset

**Do’s**  
- Use a **sharp kitchen shears** for butterflying.  
- Keep the meat exposed and lightly buttered.  
- Use a **thermometer** and **probe tenderness** as your final decision‑maker. [best practice]  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t over‑season; lobster is delicate.  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the lobster.  
- Don’t skip the rest; it’s critical for juicy meat.
