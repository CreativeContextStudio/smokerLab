**Papas al rescoldo** are the original parrilla side dish: whole, unpeeled potatoes buried directly in the hot ash and glowing coals of the brasero, left to cook for an hour in the earth-dark heat. They emerge with blackened, papery skins and steaming-hot, fluffy interiors \u2014 split open, loaded with butter and a pinch of sal gruesa, and served alongside the main cuts. The brasero provides the heat for free; no extra fuel or space on the grate needed.

***

## Fuego / Fire Setup

Papas al rescoldo cook in the **brasero** \u2014 the ember-making zone of the parrilla \u2014 not on the grate. This means they can go in as soon as you have an established coal bed, which is typically 45\u201360 minutes into the fire.

1. After your fire has been burning for 45\u201360 minutes and you have a deep coal bed in the brasero, you\u2019re ready to bury the potatoes.
2. You need a section of the brasero that has **dense, glowing orange coals** with a good layer of grey ash on top. The potatoes go directly into this material.
3. You do not need special technique \u2014 simply clear a space in the coals with your shovel, place the potatoes, and cover them.

**Timing in the asado:** Bury the potatoes at the same time you put the first beef cuts on the grate. By the time the main cuts are resting and ready to serve, the potatoes will be done.

**Do\u2019s**
- Use the densest, hottest part of the brasero coal bed.
- Bury completely \u2014 the potatoes should be surrounded on all sides by coals and ash.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t try to do this in a gas grill or on a grate. It only works directly in the coals.
- Don\u2019t use a thin, fading coal bed \u2014 you need sustained heat for a full hour.

***

## Preparaci\u00F3n / Prep

1. Choose **large russet or waxy potatoes** \u2014 unpeeled, whole, washed and dried.
2. No oil, no seasoning, no foil wrapping. The skin will char directly and protect the interior.
3. One potato per person is a reasonable plan; they keep hot in the ash for a long time so you can bury extras.

**A note on foil:** Some recipes wrap papas al rescoldo in aluminum foil. This produces a different result \u2014 more steamed, less smoky. The traditional method is no foil. The charred skin is peeled at the table; it is not eaten.

**Do\u2019s**
- Use large potatoes with thick skins \u2014 they protect the interior better during the long coal burial.
- Dry the potatoes before burying \u2014 surface moisture can cause uneven cooking.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t wrap in foil if you want the authentic smoky, rescoldo flavour.
- Don\u2019t use small potatoes \u2014 they cook too quickly and dry out.

***

## Secuencia de Cocina / Cooking Sequence

### Burial (at the start of the asado)

1. Using a shovel or metal poker, clear a space in the active coal bed of the brasero \u2014 push coals aside to create a nest.
2. Place potatoes in the nest, not touching each other if possible.
3. Shovel coals and ash back over the potatoes until they are completely buried under a 5\u201310 cm layer of coals.
4. Continue managing the brasero normally \u2014 add fresh wood, rake fresh coals under the grate. The potatoes are down there looking after themselves.

### During the cook (50\u201370 min)

- At the 45-minute mark, use a shovel to locate and flip each potato in the coals. This ensures even cooking on all sides.
- Re-bury and continue.

### Checking for doneness

- At the 55\u201360 minute mark, excavate one potato with the shovel.
- Pierce it through the skin with a skewer or long knife: it should slide in with zero resistance all the way to the center.
- If there is any firmness at the center, re-bury for 10\u201315 more minutes.

**Sensory cues:**
- Exterior: completely blackened, papery skin
- Aroma: sweet, roasted potato smell rising from the brasero
- Skewer test: no resistance at all

***

## Servicio / Serving

1. Excavate the potatoes with a shovel. They will be extremely hot \u2014 use gloves or tongs.
2. Place on a board. Do not peel in advance.
3. To serve: score the top with a cross cut and press the sides to open \u2014 a cloud of steam should emerge.
4. Add a generous knob of **butter** inside, a pinch of **sal gruesa**, and optionally a spoonful of sour cream or cr\u00e8me fra\u00EEche.

**Cultural note:** The blackened exterior of a papa al rescoldo is not a sign of burning \u2014 it\u2019s the sign of a proper parrilla. Guests who have never seen them before are sometimes alarmed. Reassure them: peel back the charred skin and inside is the most flavourful baked potato they have ever eaten, with a faint smokiness from the wood ash that no oven can produce.

***

## S\u00ED hac\u00E9 / Do\u2019s

- Bury early \u2014 same time the first meat goes on the grate.
- Use large, thick-skinned potatoes, unpeeled, no foil.
- Bury completely under a generous layer of coals and ash.
- Flip once at the 45-minute mark.
- Check for doneness with a skewer \u2014 zero resistance means done.

## No hag\u00E1s / Don\u2019ts

- Don\u2019t wrap in foil \u2014 it changes the character of the dish.
- Don\u2019t use a thin coal bed \u2014 the potatoes need sustained, intense heat for a full hour.
- Don\u2019t try to speed them up by lowering them closer to the fire; they need slow, even heat from all sides.
- Don\u2019t peel before serving \u2014 the heat stays inside the skin until the last moment.
