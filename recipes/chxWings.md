Here's a **tight, step‑by‑step guide** for **smoked chicken wings** on the **Royal Gourmet CC1830S offset smoker**, tuned for **crispy, sticky skin** and juicy meat at **325–375°F**. This assumes **2–3 lbs of whole chicken wings** (split into drumettes and flats; about 18–25 pieces total).
<!-- /equipment -->

<!-- equipment: charcoal-grill -->
Here's a **tight, step‑by‑step guide** for **charcoal-grilled chicken wings** on the **main grill chamber** of your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S**, tuned for **crispy, charred skin** at **375–425°F** using a two-zone fire. Total time: **25–35 minutes active grilling** after preheat. No offset firebox, no smoke wood — just high-output charcoal heat and the two-zone technique.
<!-- /equipment -->

***

## 1. Prep the wings (1–2 hours before cooking)

### Trim and pat dry
- **Goal:** Remove excess fat and dry the skin for maximum crisp.

| Step | How to do it |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Rinse the wings under cold water to remove any blood or debris. |
| 2 | Pat them extremely dry with paper towels. |
| 3 | Trim any excess fat or loose skin from the wing tips; keep the main skin intact. |

**Do's**
- Use a **sharp boning or kitchen shears** to trim cleanly.
- Let them sit uncovered in the fridge for **1–2 hours** to dry the skin slightly (optional but great for crisp).

**Don'ts**
- Don't remove all the skin; it's key for crisp.
- Don't skip the drying step; wet skin = soggy wings.

***

## 2. Season the wings (1–2 hours before cooking)

- **Choose a seasoning:**
  - Simple option: Salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and a touch of sugar.
  - Optional: Add herbs like rosemary, thyme, or oregano, or a light **buffalo‑style** dry rub (celery salt, cayenne, garlic powder).

**Do's**
- Toss the wings in a large bowl with the seasoning so every piece is coated.
- Let them sit uncovered in the fridge for **1–2 hours** to let the rub penetrate.

**Don'ts**
- Don't oversalt; you can always add more at the end.
- Don't apply a wet glaze yet; keep it dry until the cook is done.

***

## 3. Set up the Royal Gourmet CC1830S offset smoker

- **Target temp:** 325–375°F for chicken wings (hotter than brisket — this is a fast, high-heat cook).
- **Fire:**
  - Charcoal basket in the firebox, building a **hot charcoal bed** (not minion-style).
  - Add 1–2 small wood chunks (apple, hickory, or maple) for a light smoke flavor. [best practice]
- **Vents:**
  - Stack **fully open**; firebox intake **1/2–3/4 open** to reach 325–375°F. [best practice]
- **Pan:**
  - Place a shallow water pan under the grate opposite the heat source to catch drips. [best practice]

**Do's**
- Let the smoker come to temp **before** you load the wings.
- Use a **grate-level thermometer** near the wings to monitor the surface temp.

**Don'ts**
- Don't start the wings too early; the smoker should be hot and stable before you load them.
- Don't overcrowd the grate; wings need space for even air circulation.

***

## 4. Place the wings on the offset

- **Position:** Stack side of the chamber, not directly over the hottest coals.
- **Orientation:**
  - Lay the wings **skin-side up** so the skin is exposed to the heat.
  - Arrange them in a single layer, not stacked.

**Do's**
- Use **tongs or a heat-resistant glove** to place the wings gently on the grate.
- Double-check that the skin is not stuck to the grate before you close the lid.

**Don'ts**
- Don't put the wings directly over the hottest coals; it can burn the skin.
- Don't move them around once they're on the grate.

***

## 5. Smoke time and temp targets (325–375°F)

**Goal:** 165°F internal at the thickest part of the wing (drumette), **juicy with crispy skin**. [best practice]

| Temp range | Time (approx) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 325–350°F | 20–35 minutes | Great for 18–25 wings, balance of smoke and tenderness. |
| 350–375°F | 15–30 minutes | Faster cook, crisper skin — watch for burning. |

**Do's**
- Use a **meat-probe thermometer** inserted into the thickest part of a drumette.
- Check the temp every 10–15 minutes after the first 15 minutes.
- If the skin is getting too dark, move to a cooler part of the grate or lower temp.

**Don'ts**
- Don't open the lid constantly to check the wings.
- Don't let the internal temp exceed 170°F; they'll dry out.

***

## 6. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (offset smoker, 18–25 wings at 325–350°F)

| Time since loading | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| 0–10 min | Load wings skin-side up on offset, close the lid. [best practice] |
| 10–20 min | No opening; let wings come to temp and smoke start to build. |
| 20–30 min | Check internal temp; should be 130–150°F. Keep going if lower. |
| 30–35 min | Check again; if 155–160°F, start thinking about finishing. |
| 35–40 min | Probe every 5–10 min until 165°F. [best practice] |

**Target finish:** 165°F internal, juicy with crispy skin.

***

## 7. Finish with a crispy sear (offset, optional but highly recommended)

- **Option A (internal crisp):**
  - Increase the smoker temp to 375–400°F for the last 5–10 minutes to crisp the skin.
  - Keep the lid closed and watch for the skin to turn golden and crackle.

- **Option B (external sear):**
  - Remove the wings at 165°F and rest for 5–10 minutes.
  - Pan-sear skin side in a hot skillet for 1–2 minutes per side.

## 3. SM045 Setup

Set your SM045 to **275–300°F** and preheat fully. Load **1 oz of apple or cherry chunks** in the wood box. One small charge is all that's needed for wings — they're thin pieces with a lot of surface area, and smoke penetration happens fast in the closed cabinet.

Pat wings completely dry before seasoning. Spread them in a single layer skin-side up on the middle and upper racks with space between each piece.

Budget **45–75 minutes** until they reach 165°F in the thickest part of the drumette. The SM045 makes wonderfully smoky, juicy wings — but the skin will be soft. To fix that, finish the wings in one of these ways after the SM045:

- **Broiler:** Spread on a sheet pan, broil 5–8 minutes at high heat, flipping once.
- **450°F oven:** Bake on a wire rack for 8–10 minutes until skin tightens and crisps.
- **Hot grill:** 2–3 minutes per side over direct high heat.

Toss in sauce after crisping, not before — sauce on a cold wing before the finish turn will steam instead of caramelize.

## 3. Set up the Royal Gourmet CC1830S main grill chamber

- **Target temp:** Two-zone **medium-high (375–425°F)** overall.
- **Fire setup (two-zone):**
  - **Charcoal pan in mid position.**
  - Light **1 full chimney** (~4–5 lb briquettes). Pour all coals onto **one half** of the charcoal pan.
  - **Hot zone:** Over coals, 400–450°F.
  - **Cool zone:** No coals under the other half, 275–325°F with lid closed. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)
- **Vents:**
  - **3/4 open** during preheat; adjust to 1/2 open once at temp to hold medium-high heat.

**Do's**
- **Preheat 15–20 minutes** with lid closed until lid thermometer reads ~400°F.
- Oil the grate right before loading — wings stick aggressively to a dry grate.
- Place wings in a single layer; don't stack them.

**Don'ts**
- Don't skip preheat; a cold grate tears the skin off when you try to flip.
- Don't spread charcoal full-bed — the cool zone is your escape valve when wings flare up.

***

## 4. Cook the wings (charcoal grill two-zone)

- **Start cool zone, skin-side up:** Load wings on the indirect half, lid closed.
- **Cook 20–25 minutes indirect** until internal temp reaches 150–155°F.
- **Move to hot zone for finish:** 3–5 minutes per side over direct heat for char and crispy skin.

**Do's**
- Keep the lid **closed** during the cool zone phase — don't peek.
- Flip wings once at the halfway point during indirect (at ~10 minutes).
- Watch closely on the hot zone; wings char fast at 400°F+.

**Don'ts**
- Don't start wings on the hot zone — the fat will render and drip, causing flare-ups before the meat is cooked through.
- Don't walk away during the hot zone finish — 30 seconds matters at this heat.

***

## 5. Grill time and temp targets (charcoal grill, two-zone)

**Goal:** 165°F internal at the thickest drumette, **crispy skin**. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850)

| Phase | Time | Internal temp |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Cool zone indirect** | 20–25 min | 150–155°F |
| **Hot zone direct** | 3–5 min/side | 165°F |

***

## 6. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (charcoal grill, 18–25 wings, two-zone)

| Time since grill preheat | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| **Preheat:** 15–20 min | Lid closed, coals under half grate, vents 3/4 open, hit ~400°F lid temp. [manuals](https://manuals.plus/m/db251b7540fb780b412c58fcea4064c10d3102e8641e3cf051440a2357e13850) |
| **0 min:** | Load wings skin-side up on cool zone, lid closed. |
| **10 min:** | Flip wings, lid closed. Check they're not sticking. |
| **20–25 min:** | Probe thickest drumette — should be 150–155°F. Move to hot zone. |
| **25–30 min:** | Wings on hot zone, skin-side down first, lid open or closed. Flip after 3–5 min. |
| **30–35 min:** | Pull at 165°F internal. Rest 3–5 minutes. |

**Target finish:** 165°F, charred skin, juicy interior.

***

## 7. Sauce and serve

- **Sauce after cooking** — apply at the end, not during.
- Toss in a bowl with your sauce (buffalo, honey sriracha, BBQ) right before serving.
- Or serve sauce on the side for dipping.

**Do's**
- Use a **light coat** of sauce; too much makes the skin soggy.
- Serve immediately after saucing for maximum crisp.

**Don'ts**
- Don't sauce before the hot phase; it burns and sticks.
- Don't over-sauce; the crust is the point.

***

## 8. Do's / Don'ts summary

<!-- equipment: offset-smoker -->
**Do's (offset smoker)**
- Keep the skin intact and dry before seasoning.
- Use a thermometer — probe is your final decision-maker. [best practice]
- Finish crispy with a high-heat blast at the end.

**Don'ts (offset smoker)**
- Don't open the lid constantly.
- Don't skip the rest after cooking.
<!-- /equipment -->

<!-- equipment: charcoal-grill -->
**Do's (charcoal grill)**
- Two-zone fire, cool zone first, hot zone finish.
- Oil the grate before loading — wings stick badly on a dry grate.
- Watch closely on the hot zone; wings char in seconds at 400°F+.

**Don'ts (charcoal grill)**
- Don't start on the hot zone; flare-ups will burn the outside before the inside cooks.
- Don't crowd the grate — space between wings means better airflow and crispier skin.
<!-- /equipment -->

**Scale notes:**
- **2 lb wings:** Shave 3–5 minutes off indirect time.
- **3+ lb wings:** May need a second batch on the hot zone; don't crowd it.
