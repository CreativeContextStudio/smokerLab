Here’s a **tight, step‑by‑step guide** for **smoked chicken wings** on your **Royal Gourmet CC1830S**, tuned for **crispy, sticky skin** and juicy meat at **325–375°F**. This assumes **2–3 lbs of whole chicken wings** (split into drumettes and flats; about 18–25 pieces total).

***

## 1. Prep the wings (1–2 hours before smoking)

### Trim and pat dry
- **Goal:** Remove excess fat and dry the skin for maximum crisp.

| Step | How to do it |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Rinse the wings under cold water to remove any blood or debris. |
| 2 | Pat them extremely dry with paper towels. |
| 3 | Trim any excess fat or loose skin from the wing tips; keep the main skin intact. |

**Do’s**  
- Use a **sharp boning or kitchen shears** to trim cleanly.  
- Let them sit uncovered in the fridge for **1–2 hours** to dry the skin slightly (optional but great for crisp).  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t remove all the skin; it’s key for crisp.  
- Don’t skip the drying step; wet skin = soggy wings.  

***

## 2. Season the wings (1–2 hours before smoking)

- **Choose a seasoning:**  
  - Simple option: Salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and a touch of sugar.  
  - Optional: Add herbs like rosemary, thyme, or oregano, or a light **buffalo‑style** dry rub (celery salt, cayenne, garlic powder).  

**Do’s**  
- Toss the wings in a large bowl with the seasoning so every piece is coated.  
- Let them sit uncovered in the fridge for **1–2 hours** to let the rub penetrate.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t oversalt; you can always add more at the end.  
- Don’t apply a wet glaze yet; keep it dry for the smoke.  

***

## 3. Set up the Royal Gourmet smoker

- **Target temp:** 325–375°F for chicken wings (this is **hotter** than brisket, which is fine for a small bird).  
- **Fire:**  
  - Charcoal basket in the firebox, building a **hot charcoal bed** (not minion‑style; this is a fast, high‑heat cook).  
  - Add 1–2 small wood chunks (apple, hickory, or maple) for a light smoke flavor. [best practice]  
- **Vents:**  
  - Stack **fully open**; firebox intake **1/2–3/4 open** to reach 325–375°F. [best practice]  
- **Pan:**  
  - Place a shallow water pan under the grate opposite the heat source to catch drips and keep the smoker clean. [best practice]  

**Do’s**  
- Let the smoker come to temp **before** you load the wings.  
- Use a **grate‑level thermometer** near the wings to monitor the surface temp.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t start the wings too early; the smoker should be hot and stable before you load them.  
- Don’t overcrowd the grate; wings need space for even air circulation.  

***

## 4. Place the wings on the offset

- **Position:** Stack side of the chamber, not directly over the hottest coals.  
- **Orientation:**  
  - Lay the wings **skin‑side up** so the skin is exposed to the heat.  
  - Arrange them in a single layer, not stacked, to ensure even cooking.  

**Do’s**  
- Use **tongs or a heat‑resistant glove** to place the wings gently on the grate.  
- Double‑check that the skin is not stuck to the grate before you close the lid.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t put the wings directly over the hottest coals; it can burn the skin.  
- Don’t move them around once they’re on the grate.  

***

## 5. Smoke time and temp targets (325–375°F)

**Goal:** 165°F internal at the thickest part of the wing (drumette), **juicy with crispy skin**. [best practice]  

| Temp range | Time (approx) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 325–350°F | 20–35 minutes | Great for 18–25 wings, with a nice balance of smoke and tenderness. |
| 350–375°F | 15–30 minutes | Faster cook, crisper skin, but watch for burning. |

**Do’s**  
- Use a **meat‑probe thermometer** inserted into the thickest part of a drumette.  
- Check the temp every 10–15 minutes after the first 15 minutes.  
- If the skin is getting too dark, move the wings to a cooler part of the grate or slightly lower the temp.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the wings.  
- Don’t let the internal temp exceed 170°F; it can dry out.  

***

## 6. Minute‑by‑minute cook script (18–25 smoked wings at 325–350°F)

Use this as a mental guide; adjust for your wings’ behavior.

| Time since loading the wings | What to do |
| --- | --- |
| 0–10 min | Load the wings skin‑side up on the offset, close the lid. [best practice] |
| 10–20 min | No opening; let the wings come to temp and the smoke start to build. |
| 20–30 min | Check the internal temp; it should be 130–150°F. If it’s on the lower side, keep going. |
| 30–35 min | Check again; if it’s 155–160°F, start thinking about finishing. |
| 35–40 min | Probe every 5–10 minutes until it reaches 165°F. [best practice] |

**Target finish:** 165°F internal, **juicy with crispy skin**.  

***

## 7. Finish with a crispy sear (optional but highly recommended)

- **Option A (internal crisp):**  
  - Increase the smoker temp to 375–400°F for the last 5–10 minutes to crisp the skin.  
  - Keep the lid closed and watch for the skin to turn golden and crackle.  

- **Option B (external sear):**  
  - Remove the wings at 165°F and let them rest for 5–10 minutes.  
  - Then, pan‑sear the skin side in a hot skillet with a little oil for 1–2 minutes per side for a super‑crispy crust.  

**Do’s**  
- Use a **thermometer** to confirm 165°F before you finish.  
- Let the wings rest for 5–10 minutes before serving to let the juices redistribute.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t let the internal temp exceed 170°F; it can dry out.  
- Don’t skip the rest; it’s critical for juicy meat.  

***

## 8. Sauce and serve (optional)

- **Option A (before smoking):**  
  - Toss the wings in a light sauce (e.g., barbecue, buffalo, teriyaki) after the smoke and before the final sear for extra stickiness.  
- **Option B (after smoking):**  
  - Toss the wings in a hot sauce (e.g., buffalo, honey mustard, teriyaki) right before serving for a wet, sticky finish.  

**Do’s**  
- Use a **light coat** of sauce; too much can make the skin soggy.  
- Serve immediately after saucing for maximum crisp.  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t sauce before the smoke; it can burn or make the skin soggy.  
- Don’t over‑sauce; keep it balanced.  

***

## 9. Do’s / Don’ts for smoked chicken wings on your offset

**Do’s**  
- Use a **sharp boning or kitchen shears** for trimming.  
- Keep the skin intact and dry before seasoning.  
- Use a **thermometer** and **probe tenderness** as your final decision‑maker. [best practice]  

**Don’ts**  
- Don’t remove all the skin; it’s key for crisp.  
- Don’t open the lid constantly to check the wings.  
- Don’t skip the rest after cooking; it’s critical for juicy meat.
