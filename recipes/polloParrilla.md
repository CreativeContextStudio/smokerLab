**Pollo a la parrilla** on a Uruguayan live-fire grill is a study in patience \u2014 a butterflied whole chicken cooked bone-side down over steady embers for 30\u201340 minutes, then flipped for a crackling skin-side finish. The ember heat renders the skin slowly from below and above, producing a result that no oven or gas grill can fully replicate. For 4\u20136 guests, plan one chicken per 3\u20134 people and 45\u201360 minutes of cooking time.

***

## Fuego / Fire Setup

Pollo needs sustained medium heat \u2014 hot enough to crisp the skin, gentle enough to cook through without charring.

1. Build your fire **60\u201390 minutes** before cooking. Burn 3\u20134 hardwood logs in the brasero to a deep, even ember bed.
2. Rake an even layer of coals under the full grate \u2014 the chicken needs even heat distribution.
3. **Set the grate at mid position** \u2014 approximately 10\u201315 cm (4\u20136 inches) above the embers.
4. Hold your hand at grate height: you should feel strong, sustained heat and need to pull away within 3\u20134 seconds. That\u2019s the right level for pollo.

**Important:** Chicken fat is very volatile and will flare aggressively if there\u2019s active flame. The ember bed must be settled \u2014 no visible orange flames, only glowing coals.

**Do\u2019s**
- Maintain the brasero throughout \u2014 chicken takes 45\u201360 minutes and you need consistent heat.
- Keep a metal shovel nearby to quickly rake in fresh coals if the bed fades.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t cook over active flame. Chicken fat will drip and cause serious flare-ups.
- Don\u2019t start with a thin ember bed \u2014 a deep, established coal layer is essential.

***

## Preparaci\u00F3n / Meat Prep

### Spatchcocking (butterflying)

1. Place the chicken breast-side down on a cutting board.
2. Using kitchen shears or a heavy knife, cut along both sides of the backbone and remove it entirely.
3. Flip the chicken breast-side up and press firmly on the breastbone until it cracks flat. The chicken should now lie completely flat.
4. Tuck the wing tips behind the breast if possible \u2014 this prevents them from burning.

### Seasoning

5. Season all surfaces \u2014 both sides \u2014 generously with **sal gruesa**. Press it in firmly.
6. Optional (but traditional): rub a mixture of minced garlic, dried oregano, and a little olive oil under the skin of the breast and thighs. This adds flavour from inside.
7. Let the chicken sit at room temperature for **20\u201330 minutes** after seasoning.

**Do\u2019s**
- Flatten the chicken completely \u2014 an uneven butterfly cooks unevenly.
- Season both sides and under the skin if using the garlic-oregano option.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t marinate in acid (lemon, vinegar) before cooking on the parrilla. It compromises the skin\u2019s ability to crisp.
- Don\u2019t cook a cold chicken straight from the fridge.

***

## Secuencia de Parrilla / Grill Sequence

### Phase 1: Bone-side down (30\u201340 min)

1. Place the butterflied chicken **bone-side down** on the mid grate.
2. The bone structure conducts heat inward to the thicker joints while the skin side faces up and dries out gradually.
3. Leave it completely undisturbed for the first 20 minutes.
4. At 25\u201330 minutes, press the breast: it should feel firm to the touch with the juices beginning to run clear when poked with a knife at the thickest point.

**Sensory cues for Phase 1:**
- Strong steady sizzle from the bones conducting heat: good
- The exposed skin surface drying out and pulling tight: good \u2014 this prepares it for the crisping phase
- Drumstick joints beginning to loosen slightly from the heat

### Phase 2: Flip to skin-side down (10\u201315 min)

1. Flip the chicken **skin-side down**.
2. This is when the skin will crisp and brown rapidly over the ember heat.
3. Watch carefully: chicken fat will drip and cause flare-ups. Have your shovel ready to adjust coals if necessary.
4. Cook 10\u201315 minutes until the skin is uniformly golden-brown and crackling.

**Doneness check:**
- Pierce the thickest part of the thigh near the joint: the juices should run completely clear with no pink.
- Internal temperature (optional): **74\u00B0C (165\u00B0F)** at the thigh, away from bone.

### Phase 3: Rest

- Pull off the grate and rest **8\u201310 minutes** on a board before cutting.
- The carryover heat continues cooking the joints during the rest.

***

## Servicio y Acompa\u00F1amientos / Serving and Sides

- Cut into pieces at the board: legs, thighs, breast halves, wings.
- **Chimichurri** at the table \u2014 it is extraordinary on chicken cooked over hardwood embers.
- **Classic sides:** ensalada mixta, papas al rescoldo, morrones asados.
- **Drink:** Chilled Albar\u00EDo or a light Tannat ros\u00e9 works beautifully. Or cold beer.

**Cultural note:** Pollo a la parrilla in Uruguay is not a compromise for guests who don\u2019t eat beef \u2014 it is a classic in its own right. The hardwood smoke gives the chicken a flavour dimension that is impossible to replicate on a gas or even charcoal grill. An experienced asador treats the chicken with exactly the same attention as the beef cuts.

***

## S\u00ED hac\u00E9 / Do\u2019s

- Spatchcock (butterfly) the chicken completely flat.
- Season generously with sal gruesa; optionally rub garlic and oregano under the skin.
- Cook bone-side down first over steady medium heat for 30\u201340 minutes.
- Flip for a 10\u201315 minute skin-crisping finish.
- Rest before cutting.

## No hag\u00E1s / Don\u2019ts

- Don\u2019t cook over active flame \u2014 chicken fat will cause serious flare-ups.
- Don\u2019t flip too early \u2014 the bone side needs a full 30\u201340 minutes.
- Don\u2019t skip resting \u2014 the joints need the carryover.
- Don\u2019t press down on the chicken to force it flat after it\u2019s on the grate.
