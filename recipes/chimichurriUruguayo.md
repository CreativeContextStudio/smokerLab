**Chimichurri uruguayo** is the essential table sauce of the asado \u2014 parsley-forward, brightened with red wine vinegar, fragrant with dried oregano and garlic, and unified by good olive oil. It is made the day before and served at the table, never used as a marinade, never applied during cooking. The overnight rest is not optional: chimichurri made the same day tastes raw and sharp. Made the day before, it mellows into something genuinely special.

***

## Preparaci\u00F3n / Prep

Chimichurri has no cook step. Total active time is about 15 minutes.

### Ingredient notes

- **Parsley:** Flat-leaf (Italian) parsley only \u2014 curly parsley has the wrong flavour and texture. Use 1 packed cup of finely chopped parsley (leaves and fine stems, not thick woody stems).
- **Garlic:** Use 4\u20136 cloves, finely minced. The garlic mellows significantly overnight in the oil and vinegar.
- **Oregano:** Dried oregano \u2014 not fresh. The dried form has the right intensity for chimichurri. Use 1 tablespoon.
- **Red pepper flakes:** 1\u20132 tsp depending on your preferred heat level. Uruguayan chimichurri is mildly spicy \u2014 present but not dominant.
- **Vinegar:** Red wine vinegar only. White wine vinegar is too sharp, balsamic too sweet. 3 tablespoons.
- **Oil:** Good-quality olive oil, 1/2 cup. This is the base of the sauce \u2014 use the best you have.
- **Salt:** 1 tsp. Add, taste, adjust.

### Method

1. **Finely chop the parsley** with a sharp knife until the pieces are roughly 2\u20133 mm \u2014 not a paste, not a rough chop. The texture should be visible but fine.

2. **Combine in a bowl:** parsley, minced garlic, dried oregano, red pepper flakes, black pepper, and salt. Stir together.

3. **Add the vinegar** and stir to combine \u2014 the acid will begin to soften the garlic immediately.

4. **Add the olive oil** slowly, stirring to incorporate. The chimichurri should be loose and pourable \u2014 not a thick paste and not a thin vinaigrette. Add a tablespoon more oil if it seems too acidic, a splash more vinegar if too oily.

5. **Taste and adjust:** more salt, more pepper flakes, or more vinegar as needed. Remember it will mellow significantly overnight.

6. **Cover and refrigerate overnight** (or at least 4 hours). Remove from the refrigerator 30\u201360 minutes before serving to let it come to room temperature.

**Do\u2019s**
- Make it the day before \u2014 overnight resting is what transforms the flavour.
- Chop the parsley fine but not to a paste \u2014 texture is part of the sauce.
- Bring to room temperature before serving.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t use a food processor or blender \u2014 you want a textured sauce, not a smooth emulsion.
- Don\u2019t use fresh oregano instead of dried \u2014 the flavour is different and less traditional.
- Don\u2019t apply chimichurri to the meat during cooking. It is a table sauce.

***

## Variaciones / Variations

**Classic Uruguayan vs. Argentine:** The Uruguayan version tends to be slightly less acidic and more herb-forward than the Argentine version. Some Uruguayan families add a small amount of finely diced red onion. Some add a pinch of cumin. Both are acceptable variations.

**Heat level:** Start with 1 tsp of red pepper flakes. After tasting the next day, add more if you want it spicier. It\u2019s easier to add heat than remove it.

***

## Servicio / Serving

- Present chimichurri in a small bowl with a spoon at the table.
- Let guests sauce their own meat \u2014 some prefer a lot, some very little.
- Chimichurri keeps refrigerated for up to 5 days. The garlic mellows further with time.

**Cultural note:** Chimichurri is the great leveller at the asado table. A beautiful chimichurri can elevate a simple cut; a bad one can undermine a perfect one. It is the sauce that the asador makes with their own hands, and it is placed on the table as a gesture of hospitality \u2014 the same generosity of spirit that defines the entire asado tradition.

***

## S\u00ED hac\u00E9 / Do\u2019s

- Make the day before and rest overnight in the refrigerator.
- Use flat-leaf parsley, finely hand-chopped.
- Use dried oregano, red wine vinegar, and good olive oil.
- Bring to room temperature before serving.
- Serve at the table; let guests apply their own.

## No hag\u00E1s / Don\u2019ts

- Don\u2019t blend or process \u2014 hand-chop for proper texture.
- Don\u2019t apply to meat during cooking.
- Don\u2019t use white or balsamic vinegar.
- Don\u2019t serve cold from the refrigerator.
- Don\u2019t make it the same day you\u2019re planning to serve it.
