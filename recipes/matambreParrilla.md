**Matambre** \u2014 the name literally means \u201ckills hunger\u201d in Spanish \u2014 is the thin flank cap that sits between the skin and the ribs of the steer. It\u2019s lean, flavourful, and cooks fast over a hot ember bed: 12\u201318 minutes for a cut that arrives at the table crispy-edged, deeply beefy, and beautifully thin-sliced. For 4\u20136 guests, plan 2\u20133 lb and a confident, hot fire.

***

## Fuego / Fire Setup

Matambre is a medium-heat, medium-speed cook \u2014 hotter than asado de tira but not the intense sear of entra\u00F1a.

1. Start your fire **60\u201390 minutes** before cooking, as always. Burn 2\u20133 hardwood logs in the brasero until you have a strong, even ember bed.
2. Rake a solid layer of coals under the grate.
3. **Set the grate at mid position** \u2014 approximately 8\u201312 cm (3\u20135 inches) above the embers.
4. Hold your hand 10 cm above the grate: you should feel strong heat and want to pull your hand away within 3 seconds. That\u2019s the right zone for matambre.

**Do\u2019s**
- Matambre often goes on the grate in the second half of the asado, after the slow cuts are already well underway.
- Keep the brasero fed so the coals are active and hot, not fading.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t cook over active flame \u2014 matambre is thin and will char in seconds over open fire.
- Don\u2019t let the ember bed fade before cooking \u2014 a weak fire means steaming, not searing.

***

## Preparaci\u00F3n / Meat Prep

1. Remove matambre from refrigeration **20\u201330 minutes** before cooking.
2. Matambre is typically sold flat and thin \u2014 if yours is rolled, unroll it and press it flat.
3. If there is a tough silverskin membrane on one side, trim it off with a sharp knife. It will not render and will cause the cut to curl on the grate.
4. Season both sides generously with **sal gruesa** just before placing on the grate.
5. Optional: a light scatter of dried oregano on the meat side adds a classic Uruguayan accent.

**Do\u2019s**
- Work with a flat, even piece \u2014 if it\u2019s uneven in thickness, pound the thick parts gently.
- Season at the last moment.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t marinate \u2014 matambre is tasty on its own and the simplicity is the point.
- Don\u2019t skip the silverskin removal \u2014 it will cause curling and uneven cooking.

***

## Secuencia de Parrilla / Grill Sequence

### Phase 1: First side (6\u20139 min)

1. Place matambre flat on the mid grate over the hot ember bed.
2. You should hear an immediate, strong sizzle.
3. Cook without moving for **6\u20139 minutes**. The edges will begin to crisp and pull slightly upward.
4. Check the bottom: it should be golden-brown with charred patches at the thinnest edges.

**Sensory cues for Phase 1:**
- Strong sizzle from first contact: good
- Edges beginning to turn pale grey on the sides: almost ready to flip
- Pressing the top surface: should feel firmer than raw but still have some give

### Phase 2: Second side (6\u20139 min)

1. Flip once. Salt the now-facing-up side with a light additional pinch of sal gruesa.
2. Cook 6\u20139 more minutes.
3. **Total cook time:** 12\u201318 minutes. Matambre is best served **medium** \u2014 the interior should be slightly pink, not grey.

**Press test:** Press the thickest part with your fingertip. Medium feels like a firm but yielding give \u2014 similar to the base of your thumb when you press your thumb and ring finger together.

**Internal temperature (optional):** Pull at 58\u201360\u00B0C (135\u2013140\u00B0F).

### Phase 3: Rest and slice

- Rest **5 minutes** on a board.
- Slice across the grain into thin strips or on a diagonal \u2014 matambre has good texture when thinly sliced.

***

## Servicio y Acompa\u00F1amientos / Serving and Sides

- **Chimichurri** at the table.
- **Sequence:** Matambre fits naturally alongside entra\u00F1a in the second wave of the asado \u2014 after the slow cuts and before or alongside any final sausages.
- **Classic sides:** ensalada mixta, crusty bread, morrones asados.
- **Drink:** Tannat or a young Malbec.

**Cultural note:** Matambre\u2019s name tells you everything about its role in the asado \u2014 it appears early enough to \u201ckill the hunger\u201d of waiting guests, or it anchors the second wave alongside faster cuts. Either way, it\u2019s straightforward, honest, and deeply satisfying.

***

## S\u00ED hac\u00E9 / Do\u2019s

- Remove the silverskin for flat, even cooking.
- Season simply with sal gruesa and (optional) oregano only.
- Cook at mid-height over a hot, established ember bed.
- Flip once; 6\u20139 minutes per side.
- Rest briefly and slice thinly across the grain.

## No hag\u00E1s / Don\u2019ts

- Don\u2019t marinate \u2014 it complicates a cut that shines in simplicity.
- Don\u2019t cook over flame.
- Don\u2019t skip the rest before slicing.
- Don\u2019t overcook past medium \u2014 matambre at well-done is dry and tough.
