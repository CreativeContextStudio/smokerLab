**Pollo al disco** is one of the great campfire dishes of the R\u00EDo de la Plata \u2014 chicken thighs and drumsticks braised with onions, peppers, wine, and tomatoes in a cast-iron disco (the repurposed plow disc that has become a beloved cooking vessel) set directly over the glowing coals of the parrilla. It\u2019s communal, aromatic, and deeply satisfying. For 6\u20138 guests, plan 4\u20135 lb of bone-in chicken and 50\u201370 minutes of active cooking.

***

## Fuego / Fire Setup

The disco sits directly on or over the embers \u2014 much closer to the heat source than a standard grate cook. You need a hot, active coal bed.

1. Build your fire **60\u201390 minutes** before cooking. Burn 3\u20134 hardwood logs to a deep ember bed.
2. Rake a thick, concentrated pile of embers in one zone \u2014 this is where the disco will sit.
3. The disco can rest directly on a grate at its lowest position, or even be placed on a ring of bricks over the coals if your parrilla allows.
4. Heat the disc empty for 5\u201310 minutes before adding oil \u2014 it needs to be screaming hot before the first ingredient goes in.

**Managing heat during the cook:** Disco cooking is interactive. You will need to add or remove coals from under the disco to control temperature \u2014 more coals for a sear, fewer for a braise. Keep the brasero active throughout.

**Do\u2019s**
- Preheat the disco thoroughly before adding any ingredients.
- Have a metal shovel nearby to add or remove coals under the disc as needed during the cook.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t add oil to a cold disco \u2014 it should shimmer and nearly smoke when the oil goes in.
- Don\u2019t use a thin or warped disc \u2014 even heat distribution is essential.

***

## Preparaci\u00F3n / Meat Prep

1. Pat the chicken thighs and drumsticks dry with paper towels.
2. Season generously with **sal gruesa**, black pepper, and sweet paprika.
3. Prepare the vegetables before cooking begins: slice onions into rings, slice peppers into strips, mince garlic, and rough-chop tomatoes.
4. Measure out the wine and have it ready.

**Do\u2019s**
- Use bone-in chicken for flavour \u2014 boneless cooks faster but lacks the depth that the bones add to the braising liquid.
- Have all vegetables prepped and staged before the disco goes on \u2014 the cooking moves fast.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t skip the paprika \u2014 it\u2019s essential to the colour and flavour of the dish.

***

## Secuencia de Cocina / Cooking Sequence

### Phase 1: Sear the chicken (8\u201310 min)

1. Add **3\u20134 tbsp olive oil** to the screaming-hot disco. It should shimmer immediately.
2. Add chicken pieces skin-side down in a single layer. Work in batches if necessary \u2014 do not crowd.
3. Sear **4\u20135 minutes per side** until deep golden-brown. This is the flavour foundation of the entire dish.
4. Remove seared chicken and set aside temporarily.

**Sensory cues:** Deep golden-brown crust, clear sizzle, slight char at the edges. The crust should release cleanly when you try to lift a piece.

### Phase 2: Build the sofrito (8\u201310 min)

1. Reduce the coal intensity slightly if needed \u2014 you want a steady sizzle, not a roar.
2. Add onions to the disc in the chicken fat. Stir and cook **4\u20135 minutes** until translucent and beginning to caramelise.
3. Add garlic. Stir 1 minute.
4. Add peppers. Stir and cook 3\u20134 more minutes until softened.
5. Add tomatoes. Stir to combine.

**Sensory cues:** The sofrito should smell deeply sweet and roasted. The vegetables should be visibly soft and glossy.

### Phase 3: Deglaze and braise (30\u201345 min)

1. Pour in the **white wine** \u2014 it will hiss and deglaze the bottom of the disc. Scrape up any stuck bits with a wooden spoon \u2014 that\u2019s all flavour.
2. Return the seared chicken pieces, nestling them into the vegetables. They should be mostly submerged.
3. Add the oregano and any remaining salt adjustment.
4. Reduce the coal intensity under the disco \u2014 you want a gentle, steady simmer, not a roaring boil.
5. Braise **30\u201340 minutes**, turning the chicken pieces every 10 minutes and spooning the braising liquid over the top.

**Doneness check:** Pierce the thickest thigh at the bone \u2014 the juices must run completely clear. The meat should be pulling away from the bone slightly. Internal temperature (optional): **74\u00B0C (165\u00B0F)** at the thigh.

### Phase 4: Final reduction (5 min, optional)

1. If the braising liquid seems thin, increase the coal intensity for a final 5 minutes to reduce and concentrate the sauce.
2. The sauce should coat a spoon and have a deep, glossy colour.

***

## Servicio y Acompa\u00F1amientos / Serving and Sides

- **Serve directly from the disco** at the table \u2014 the cast iron keeps everything warm.
- Crusty bread to scoop up the braising liquid is essential.
- **Ensalada mixta** on the side for freshness.
- **Drink:** A glass of white wine (the same wine used in the braise) or a light Tannat.

**Cultural note:** Pollo al disco started as a dish of the Argentine and Uruguayan countryside \u2014 the plow disc was repurposed as a cooking vessel by farm workers who had access to a disc, an open fire, and whatever ingredients were at hand. It\u2019s a dish of improvisation and abundance, and it has no set recipe. What you see here is a classic base \u2014 but the disc welcomes whatever vegetables, herbs, or proteins are available.

***

## S\u00ED hac\u00E9 / Do\u2019s

- Preheat the disco thoroughly before adding any ingredients.
- Sear the chicken in batches for deep colour \u2014 this is the flavour foundation.
- Build the sofrito properly: onions first, then garlic, then peppers, then tomatoes.
- Deglaze with wine and scrape up the stuck bits.
- Maintain a gentle simmer for the braise, not a boil.

## No hag\u00E1s / Don\u2019ts

- Don\u2019t crowd the chicken in Phase 1 \u2014 steaming instead of searing ruins the flavour base.
- Don\u2019t rush the braise with too-high heat \u2014 the chicken will tighten and dry out.
- Don\u2019t skip the wine deglaze \u2014 that browned fond on the bottom of the disc is pure flavour.
- Don\u2019t serve until the juices run completely clear at the bone.
