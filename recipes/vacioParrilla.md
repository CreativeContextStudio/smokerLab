**Vac\u00EDo** is the great Uruguayan slow cut \u2014 a whole flank steak with a thick, luxurious fat cap that must be coaxed, not forced. It goes on the parrilla fat-side down over moderate embers and stays there, almost untouched, for 45\u201360 minutes while the fat renders golden and the meat inside turns deep pink and impossibly juicy. For 4\u20136 people, plan a 3\u20135 lb vac\u00EDo and the patience to let it work.

***

## Fuego / Fire Setup

Start your fire **60\u201390 minutes before cooking.** Vac\u00EDo needs steady, moderate heat \u2014 not the raging intensity of a quick sear.

1. Build a wood fire in the brasero with 3\u20134 hardwood logs (quebracho, eucalyptus, or oak). Let them burn down fully to glowing orange coals \u2014 about 45\u201360 minutes.
2. Rake a generous, even layer of embers under the grate.
3. **Set the grate at mid height** \u2014 approximately 10\u201315 cm (4\u20136 inches) above the embers.
4. Test the heat by holding your hand 10 cm above the grate for 3\u20134 seconds. You should feel steady, serious heat but not be forced to pull your hand away instantly. That\u2019s the right level for vac\u00EDo.

**Maintain the brasero** throughout the cook by adding one log every 20\u201330 minutes and raking fresh embers under the grate as needed.

**Do\u2019s**
- Keep the ember bed consistent \u2014 hot spots and cold patches will cook the fat cap unevenly.
- If you see open flame under the grate, raise it immediately and let the fire settle.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t start with fresh-added logs directly under the meat. Use only settled, glowing coals.
- Don\u2019t use charcoal briquettes if you can avoid it \u2014 their chemical off-gassing during the long cook is noticeable.

***

## Preparaci\u00F3n / Meat Prep

1. Remove vac\u00EDo from refrigeration **30\u201345 minutes** before cooking.
2. **Do not trim the fat cap.** The entire technique depends on that cap rendering down slowly over the cook.
3. Season the fat side generously with **sal gruesa** \u2014 press it in firmly. Season the meat side just before placing on the grate.
4. No oil, no rub, no acid. The fat will self-baste the meat throughout the cook.

**Do\u2019s**
- Pat the meat side dry if it\u2019s wet from refrigeration.
- Score the fat cap very lightly with a knife in a crosshatch pattern if it\u2019s thicker than 1 cm \u2014 this helps it render without curling the meat.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t marinate. The fat cap will be ruined by acid, and vac\u00EDo doesn\u2019t need it.
- Don\u2019t trim \u2014 not a single gram of fat.

***

## Secuencia de Parrilla / Grill Sequence

### Phase 1: Fat-side down, slow rendering (30\u201340 min)

1. Place vac\u00EDo **fat-side down** on the grate over your steady ember bed.
2. Listen: within 5\u201310 minutes, you should hear a steady, low sizzle as the fat begins to melt and drip.
3. Do not move it, press it, or lift it to check. The fat cap is building a golden crust. Every time you disturb it, you lose that crust.
4. At the **30-minute mark**, gently lift one edge. The fat side should be deep golden-brown, translucent where it\u2019s rendered, and crackling at the edges.

**Sensory cues for Phase 1:**
- Steady sizzle: good
- Occasional light pop and drip onto embers: good
- Fat cap turning golden and pulling back from the edges: Phase 1 complete

### Phase 2: Flip to meat side (15\u201325 min)

1. Flip the vac\u00EDo **meat-side down**. The fat cap should be uniformly golden \u2014 if it\u2019s still pale in patches, give it 5 more minutes before flipping.
2. Now salt the fat cap side generously (it\u2019s now facing up).
3. Cook the meat side for 15\u201325 minutes depending on thickness. You\u2019re aiming for **medium** at most \u2014 vac\u00EDo at medium-rare to medium has a deeply pink interior with a slightly firm exterior.

**Press test:** Press the thickest part with your finger. Medium-rare feels like the base of your thumb when your hand is relaxed. Medium feels slightly firmer. Vac\u00EDo is best served somewhere in between.

**Internal temperature (if using a thermometer):** Pull at 52\u201355\u00B0C (125\u2013130\u00B0F) for medium-rare, 58\u00B0C (135\u00B0F) for medium.

### Phase 3: Rest

- Pull off the grate and rest **8\u201310 minutes** on a board, fat-side up.
- Slice **against the grain** in thick slices. The fat cap should be golden-crisp and separated slightly from the meat \u2014 serve it alongside.

***

## Servicio y Acompa\u00F1amientos / Serving and Sides

- **Chimichurri** passed at the table \u2014 let each guest sauce their own slice.
- **Salsa criolla** alongside for brightness.
- **Classic sides:** ensalada mixta, papas al rescoldo, morrones asados, crusty bread.
- **Drink:** Tannat, preferably from Carmelo or Canelones \u2014 its firm tannins and dark fruit match the richness of the vac\u00EDo fat perfectly.

**Cultural note:** Vac\u00EDo is the cut that defines the asador\u2019s relationship to time. It cannot be rushed. Uruguayan asadores speak of vac\u00EDo with the same reverence that pit masters reserve for brisket \u2014 you either trust the process or you don\u2019t.

***

## S\u00ED hac\u00E9 / Do\u2019s

- Keep the fat cap on \u2014 it is the point of the entire cut.
- Start with a full, established ember bed, not a fresh fire.
- Keep the grate at mid height for the entire cook.
- Let it render fat-side down for a full 30\u201340 minutes without disturbing it.
- Rest generously before slicing.

## No hag\u00E1s / Don\u2019ts

- Don\u2019t flip early \u2014 the fat needs time to render or it will simply flare.
- Don\u2019t cook over flame \u2014 the fat cap will scorch before the interior cooks.
- Don\u2019t press down on the meat with tongs or a spatula.
- Don\u2019t serve without resting \u2014 the juices are still moving inside.
- Don\u2019t apply chimichurri during cooking \u2014 always at the table.
