Here's a **step‑by‑step guide** for **Uruguayan‑style salsa criolla**, a fresh, tangy condiment that goes alongside grilled beef, sausages, and choripán. This recipe is sized for **6–8 people** and comes together in about 15 minutes of active prep plus resting time. Make it at least an hour before serving so the flavors meld.

***

## 1. Ingredients

- 1 large **onion** (white or yellow), medium dice
- 1 ½ **bell peppers** (ideally 1 red + ½ green), medium dice
- 3 medium **tomatoes**, cored, medium dice (seeds OK)
- 1 small clove **garlic**, finely minced
- 3 tablespoons **red wine vinegar** (or white wine vinegar, or apple cider vinegar)
- ½ cup **olive oil** (or neutral vegetable oil)
- 1–2 teaspoons **oregano** (dried)
- 1 teaspoon **salt**, or to taste
- Fresh ground **black pepper**, to taste

***

## 2. Prep the onion

1. Peel the onion and cut into medium dice (roughly ¼").
2. If you want a milder bite, soak the diced onion in cold water with a pinch of salt for 10 minutes, then drain well.

**Do's**
- Keep the dice consistent; even pieces make the salsa look and eat better.

**Don'ts**
- Don't mince the onion too fine; you want visible, crunchy pieces with some bite.

***

## 3. Prep the peppers

1. Cut the bell peppers in half, remove seeds and white membranes.
2. Dice the peppers to a similar size as the onion so the salsa has an even texture.

***

## 4. Prep the tomatoes and garlic

1. Core the tomatoes; if skins are thick, you can peel them, but this is optional.
2. Dice tomatoes the same size as the onions and peppers (include seeds for a juicier salsa).
3. Finely mince the garlic clove.

**Do's**
- Use firm, ripe tomatoes; they hold their shape and balance the vinegar.

**Don'ts**
- Don't use canned or mushy tomatoes; this is a fresh salsa and needs the texture.

***

## 5. Make the dressing

1. In a small bowl or measuring cup, whisk together:
   - 3 tbsp red wine vinegar
   - ½ cup olive oil
   - 1–2 tsp dried oregano
   - 1 tsp salt
   - Several grinds of black pepper
2. Taste and adjust salt/vinegar so it's bright but balanced; it should be tangy enough to cut rich grilled meat.

***

## 6. Combine and rest

1. In a medium bowl, add onion, bell peppers, tomatoes, and minced garlic.
2. Pour the dressing over the vegetables and mix gently until everything is evenly coated.
3. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for **at least 1 hour**, ideally 2, so the flavors meld.
4. Before serving, taste again and adjust salt, pepper, or vinegar if needed.

**Do's**
- Mix gently; you want the vegetables coated, not crushed.
- Let it rest long enough for the vinegar and oregano to permeate the vegetables.

**Don'ts**
- Don't skip the resting time; freshly mixed criolla tastes flat compared to a rested one.

***

## 7. Serving and storage

- Serve at **cool room temperature** in a small bowl with a spoon, alongside grilled beef, sausages, and choripán for a Uruguayan‑style asado.
- Leftovers keep **2–3 days** in the fridge in a sealed container; stir before using as the oil and vinegar separate.

***

## 8. Do's / Don'ts for salsa criolla

**Do's**
- Make it ahead; the flavor improves after resting.
- Keep the dice uniform for even texture and presentation.
- Use good olive oil and real red wine vinegar; they're the backbone of the dressing.

**Don'ts**
- Don't add too many extras; criolla is intentionally simple—onion, pepper, tomato, dressing.
- Don't drown it in oil; the ratio should be bright and vinegary, not greasy.
- Don't serve it ice‑cold straight from the fridge; pull it out 15–20 minutes early so it comes to cool room temp.
