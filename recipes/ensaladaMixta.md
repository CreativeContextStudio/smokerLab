**Ensalada mixta** is the non-negotiable side dish of every Uruguayan asado \u2014 torn lettuce, sliced tomato, and thin onion rings dressed simply with red wine vinegar, olive oil, and salt. It is prepared before the fire is even lit and sits on the table throughout the meal, offering a clean, acidic counterpoint to the richness of the grilled meats. There is no recipe to master here; the discipline is in its simplicity.

***

## Preparaci\u00F3n / Prep

This salad has no cook step. Prepare it 30\u201360 minutes before the asado begins.

1. **Lettuce:** Tear a large head of romaine or butter lettuce by hand into large, irregular pieces. Do not cut with a knife \u2014 tearing gives better texture and surface area for dressing. Place in a large, wide bowl.

2. **Tomatoes:** Slice 3\u20134 medium, ripe tomatoes into rounds approximately 5 mm thick. Arrange over the lettuce.

3. **Onion:** Peel and slice one medium white or red onion into very thin rings (2\u20133 mm). If the onion is sharp, soak the rings in cold water for 10 minutes and drain \u2014 this takes the edge off without losing flavour.

4. **Dressing:** In a small bowl or directly at the table, combine:
   - 3 tbsp red wine vinegar
   - 4 tbsp good olive oil
   - 1 tsp salt
   - Black pepper to taste

   Whisk briefly and dress the salad only at the moment of serving \u2014 dressed lettuce wilts quickly.

**Do\u2019s**
- Use ripe, in-season tomatoes \u2014 this salad has nowhere to hide.
- Dress only at the moment of serving, not in advance.
- Use a generous amount of olive oil \u2014 this is not a diet salad, it\u2019s a counterbalance to rich grilled meat.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t use pre-cut bagged lettuce \u2014 the texture is wrong.
- Don\u2019t dress in advance.
- Don\u2019t complicate it with additional vegetables, croutons, or cheese. Simplicity is the point.

***

## Variaciones / Variations

Uruguay\u2019s ensalada mixta can expand slightly depending on the season:
- **Avocado slices** added on top are common in summer.
- **Cucumber rounds** alongside tomato are a natural addition.
- Some families add a scatter of dried oregano over the top.

But the core \u2014 lettuce, tomato, onion, vinegar, oil, salt \u2014 never changes.

***

## Servicio y Acompa\u00F1amientos / Serving

- **Place on the table** when the first cuts are being served.
- Serve communally \u2014 guests help themselves throughout the meal.
- The dressing at the bottom of the bowl, accumulated with tomato juice and meat drippings if bread is dipped, is its own pleasure.

**Cultural note:** The simplicity of ensalada mixta is a statement. At an asado, the salad does not compete with the meat \u2014 it supports it. An asado host who arrives with an elaborate salad has misunderstood the meal. The elaborate thing is the fire and the meat. The salad is the honest, quiet companion.

***

## S\u00ED hac\u00E9 / Do\u2019s

- Prepare in advance but dress only at serving time.
- Use ripe tomatoes and good olive oil.
- Soak the onion rings in cold water if they\u2019re very sharp.
- Tear the lettuce by hand.

## No hag\u00E1s / Don\u2019ts

- Don\u2019t over-dress or dress early.
- Don\u2019t complicate the salad with extra ingredients.
- Don\u2019t use a sharp, pungent dressing \u2014 red wine vinegar is mild and appropriate; lemon or balsamic are too assertive.
