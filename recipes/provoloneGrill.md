## Provoleta (grilled provolone on the parrilla)

Goal: Bubbling, golden provolone you scoop with bread. This assumes a charcoal or wood grill.

### Ingredients (serves 2–3)

- 1 round provolone (or “queso parrillero”), ½–¾ inch thick  
- 1–2 teaspoons dried oregano  
- Pinch red pepper flakes (optional)  
- Fresh black pepper  
- Olive oil  
- Crusty bread, sliced

### Step 1 – Prep the cheese

1. Pat the cheese dry with paper towels.  
2. Lightly brush both sides with olive oil.  
3. Sprinkle dried oregano, a pinch of red pepper flakes (if using), and black pepper on both sides.  
4. Set on a plate while you heat the grill.

### Step 2 – Set up the grill

<!-- equipment: charcoal-grill -->
1. Build a medium–high direct heat fire.
2. Clean the grate well with a brush.
3. Oil the grate lightly, or place a small cast-iron skillet on the grate to preheat.
<!-- /equipment -->

<!-- equipment: gas-grill -->
1. Place a small cast-iron skillet on the grate.
2. Light with lid **open**. Set burners to MEDIUM‑HIGH.
3. **E‑310:** Left or center burner MEDIUM‑HIGH; others LOW. **E‑210:** Left burner MEDIUM‑HIGH; right LOW.
4. Preheat grill AND skillet **5–10 minutes** until skillet is smoking hot.
5. Do not attempt to grill provolone directly on gas grates—it will melt through. The cast iron skillet is required.
<!-- /equipment -->

### Step 3 – Grill the cheese

1. Place the cheese directly on the hot, oiled grate or into the hot skillet.  
2. Let it cook without moving for 1–3 minutes, until the bottom is browned and the edges just start to soften.  
3. Using a spatula, carefully flip the cheese.  
4. Grill the second side another 1–3 minutes, until the edges are browned and the center is molten but still mostly holding together.

### Step 4 – Serve

1. Transfer the cheese to a small skillet, plate, or shallow dish.  
2. Drizzle with a little olive oil and a pinch more oregano or chili if you like.  
3. Serve immediately with crusty bread to scoop the cheese.

**Do’s**

- Do keep the cheese cold until just before grilling so it holds its shape.  
- Do stay at the grill the entire time; it cooks very fast.

**Don’ts**

- Don’t let the fire flare up under the cheese.  
- Don’t overcook until it completely runs through the grate.
