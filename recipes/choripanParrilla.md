**Choripán** is the universal asado opener \u2014 fresh chorizo criollo grilled over the parrilla while the slow cuts are still in their first hour, then tucked into crusty bread with chimichurri and salsa criolla. Every asado in Uruguay starts here. For 6\u20138 guests, plan 6\u20138 chorizos and serve them as the appetizer that buys the asador another hour of patient tending.

***

## Fuego / Fire Setup

Chorizo goes on the parrilla during the **initial burn**, often before the main cuts. The fire doesn\u2019t need to be at full ember production \u2014 a lively but settling fire is fine.

1. The brasero should be well-established: 2\u20133 hardwood logs burning actively, starting to produce coals.
2. Set the grate at **mid position** \u2014 10\u201315 cm (4\u20136 inches) above the embers or early coals.
3. The chorizo\u2019s job is to cook slowly and render its fat without bursting. Medium heat is right. High heat will split the casing before the inside is cooked.

**Timing in the asado:** Chorizo and morcilla go on first, early, before the main beef cuts. They cook while the fire settles and guests are arriving \u2014 served as appetizers with the first glass of wine or beer.

**Do\u2019s**
- Use the mid-grate zone, not the hottest spot directly over the densest coals.
- Allow chorizo to rotate and cook on all sides.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t put chorizo over fierce direct heat \u2014 it will split and lose its fat before the interior cooks through.
- Don\u2019t poke or prick the casing \u2014 the fat inside is the flavour.

***

## Preparaci\u00F3n / Meat Prep

1. Use **fresh chorizo criollo** \u2014 the raw pork sausage seasoned with cumin, paprika, and garlic that is the foundation of every Uruguayan asado. This is not Spanish cured chorizo; it\u2019s a raw, fresh link.
2. No seasoning needed \u2014 the chorizo is already seasoned inside.
3. Remove from refrigeration **15\u201320 minutes** before cooking.

**Do\u2019s**
- Buy from a good butcher if possible \u2014 fresh chorizo quality varies enormously and it is the flavour engine of choripán.
- Leave the casing intact.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t score, slash, or prick the casing before cooking.
- Don\u2019t freeze and thaw repeatedly \u2014 fresh is always better.

***

## Secuencia de Parrilla / Grill Sequence

### Phase 1: Low and slow until skin tightens (8\u201310 min per side)

1. Place chorizos on the **mid grate** over moderate heat.
2. The casing will start to tighten and take on colour within 5 minutes.
3. Roll them with tongs every **5\u20136 minutes** to colour all sides evenly.
4. **Total cook time:** 15\u201320 minutes. You\u2019re aiming for the casing to be deeply golden with the fat beginning to bubble at the ends \u2014 and the interior fully cooked through.

**Sensory cues:**
- Casing tightening and turning golden: on track
- Fat beginning to ooze at the link ends: nearly done
- Skin crisping and beginning to split slightly at the fattest points: ready to pull (this is desirable \u2014 the small natural split gives you a taste window)
- Interior: cut one open to check \u2014 the inside should be grey-pink throughout, not red-raw

**Do not** purposely slash or butterfly them at this stage. In Uruguay, chorizo is served whole in the bread. The small natural splits from cooking are part of the texture.

### Phase 2: Rest 2\u20133 minutes

- Pull off the grate and rest briefly on a board.
- Slice the bread rolls and warm them briefly on the edge of the grate, cut-side down, for 1\u20132 minutes.

***

## Armado del Choripán / Building the Sandwich

1. Slice a crusty marraqueta or baguette segment lengthwise.
2. Place the chorizo inside \u2014 whole or sliced lengthwise if very large.
3. Add a generous spoon of **chimichurri** and/or **salsa criolla** directly in the bread.
4. Serve open or closed \u2014 there is no wrong answer.

**Do\u2019s**
- Warm the bread on the grate edge briefly \u2014 the crunch of warm, slightly toasted bread against the chorizo is essential.
- Be generous with the chimichurri \u2014 this is not a place for restraint.

**Don\u2019ts**
- Don\u2019t use soft sandwich bread \u2014 the chorizo fat will collapse it. Crusty bread only.

***

## Servicio y Acompa\u00F1amientos / Serving and Sides

- **Serve as the asado opener**, before the main cuts arrive at the table.
- Pass alongside cold beer or a glass of young Tannat.
- **Companion:** morcilla alongside \u2014 the two are inseparable as asado starters.

**Cultural note:** The choripán is the great Uruguayan equalizer. You will find it at Sunday family asados, at obra (construction site) cookouts, at Carnaval, and at Mercado del Puerto in Montevideo where the smoke never stops. It is democratic, unpretentious, and magnificent. The asado is not truly underway until the first choripán is in someone\u2019s hand.

***

## S\u00ED hac\u00E9 / Do\u2019s

- Use fresh chorizo criollo \u2014 quality here matters enormously.
- Cook at medium heat, mid-grate, rotating every 5\u20136 minutes.
- Let the casing split naturally \u2014 don\u2019t force it.
- Warm the bread on the grate edge.
- Be generous with chimichurri.

## No hag\u00E1s / Don\u2019ts

- Don\u2019t prick or slash the casing before cooking.
- Don\u2019t cook over fierce heat \u2014 it splits the casing and dries out the inside.
- Don\u2019t skip the bread warming \u2014 it changes the texture experience completely.
- Don\u2019t use soft bread \u2014 crusty rolls only.
